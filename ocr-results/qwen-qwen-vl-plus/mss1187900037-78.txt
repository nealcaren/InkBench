Winchester/Ms./Aug/2.1845

My dear friend,
Your kind favor came in due time for which all thanks. Its suggestions were sensible of service, and its kind sayings most grateful to both of us. He will stay when he is at school, with an increase in his salary of $200, and has also engaged to teach Elocution at the Catholic College, where his rate of compensation will be more liberal than he has had before—and in addition to this, he is determined to try at Public Reading the next winter—and can furnish you with a list of the different Lyceums where he might be able to make engagements.
