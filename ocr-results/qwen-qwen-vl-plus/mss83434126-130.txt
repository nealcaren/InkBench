1861 Aug 6th. Congress closed its extra session. Congress adjourned.
1861 Aug 7th. The town of Hampton, Va., destroyed by the rebels.
The private York, burned by the U.S. Gunboat Union, the two latter prisoners.
1861 Aug 10th. Battle of Wilson's Creek, Mo. The federals, under the command Gen'l. Lyon, made a planned attack on the rebels, and then from their encampment and contacted the fight. For six hours hard fighting and in leading a bayonet charge, Gen'l. Lyon was killed instantly. Col. Dizel carried out Lyon's plan, and retired in an orderly manner to Springfield, leaving captives 400 horses, and 40 prisoners. Union loss 200, killed 700 wounded. Rebel loss in killed and wounded was much greater. The brave Gen'l. Lyon, the hero of the West, gave his honor, his fortune (money) and his life, to his country that his country might live. Number of federal troops engaged was 3,000, number of rebel troops engaged was 2,000.
1861 Aug 13th. President Lincoln issues a proclamation appointing the 30th day of September as a day of fasting and prayer.
1861 Aug 14th. Major Gen'l. John C. Frémont abolishes and places the city of St. Louis, Mo. under martial law.
1861 Aug 16th. Major Gen'l. John C. Frémont placed in command of Fortress Monroe and its military surroundings.
