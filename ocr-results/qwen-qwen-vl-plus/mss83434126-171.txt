Repeated dashes, very furious and furious struggles took place to carry the works by them, but often on the verge of success, the federals were often pushed and driven back by the indomitable bravery of the enemy. The federals were eventually ignominiously repulsed with many losses. Federal loss 668 in killed, wounded and missing. Number of federals engaged 14,000. Number of rebels engaged 8,000. Genl. Benham was subsequently summoned to Washington to explain and justify his conduct.

1862 June 17th. Evacuation of Cumberland Gap, Tenn. The federals under Genl. Morgan, advanced towards this place for the purpose of expelling the rebel Genl. Stevenson & Bonish, who occupied the gap with 13,000 men and well fortified the same. This gap is a cliff in the Cumberland mountains, and is so steep and narrow that there is through the gap, only a single roadway. It was expected that the rebels would defend its possession with the utmost tenacity. No such result followed. When the rebels were informed of the approach of the federals, they precipitately evacuated the place.
