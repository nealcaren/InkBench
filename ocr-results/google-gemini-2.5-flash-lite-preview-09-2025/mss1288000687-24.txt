An undaunted devotion to the cause of woman suffrage links the name of Alice Stone Blackwell, co-counselor of the Boston League of Women Voters, with that of her famous mother, Lucy Stone, a pioneer of woman's rights. Her father, Henry B. Blackwell, was the only man who really de-voted his life to obtaining equal rights for women. One of Miss Black-well's aunts, Elizabeth Blackwell, was the first woman to be able to [?] take a medical degree, and another, Antoinette Brown Black-well, was the first woman to be ordained a minister. Miss Black-well was graduated from Boston University in 1881 and then joined her parents in working for suf-frage and in publishing the Woman's Journal, carrying the paper on alone after their death until 1917 when it became the Woman Citizen.
