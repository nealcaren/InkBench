The Federals were commanded by [Genle] Curtis & Sigel, this battle was fought for three days without any decisive result, but on the 8th Genl Curtis made additional changes, and at early morn he attacked the rebels along his whole line, the rebels were instantly thrown with confusion and fled precipitately on all the attack. The division of Van Dorn retreated in one direction that of Price escaped by another, The pursuit was contin[ued] by Genl Sigel toward Keitherthville, and by the Cavalry to the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete, Rebel lost 2000 killed and wounded also 1000 prisoners, among the rebel dead was found Genl Mc Culloch, a peculiar feature in this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians under the Command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were demonstrated after battle the federal slain and wounded were found to be scalped, thus renewing the first scenes of sanguinary slaughter, Union lose 500 killed, and 900 wounded.

1862 March 11th Genl Geo McClellan assumes command of the Army of the Potomac in the field, - Genl Fremont assumes command of the Mountain Department - Genl Halleck assumes command of the Department of the Mississippi - Manassas occupied by the Federals,
