Buc. 4 Sept. 1922

Dear Mrs Chapman,

We took in our program for the year
to read lectures on "Famous Women." Mrs Reus Ianulescu said
I will do it, she in the L. D. I. F. and in popular evening course.
But as here are no complete libraries in Roumania and not
a single feminist library, it is impossible for us even to know
the titles of the books, necessary for preparation. Readers therefore
to you as to the other sections of L. D. I. F. to ask your help.
Feeling so much the loss of a fine library we will
try at one time with our studies, to found one at B. and
perhaps than will we be able to assemble round it the
Women in a club; we are dreaming of such a long time.
Would you, asociación be able for the generous act of sorry
help into sisters and to send us one or the other standard work
on women movement in your country and specially
books, there we could find biographies of your famous women.
We would speak in a first series of lectures on political and social 

movement Women and in a second one the portraits of women

wise and severe. But we know so little, it is to say not much
