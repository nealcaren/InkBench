Washington April 10, 1887
Dear Father

Your letter dated March 24th I received yesterday, it found us all well, and pleased to hear from you.

Mr. Trotter, the new Recorder of Deeds is seriously sick with pneumonia. Gerrit S. Soquen has been appointed Deputy Recorder of Deeds. The Lawyers are much put out about it.

Senator Sherman is making a trip South; in Georgia, a colored delegation called at his hotel, and was refused admission. As soon as he heard of it, he paid his bill and went to another hotel. A colored man by the name of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed to the Siberian Mission. The political air work at present on the Republican side is wavering between Blaine and Sherman.

Congress has made an appropriation for a new Bridge across the Eastern Branch at the foot of Penn'a Ave., Business in the Recorder Office is booming. Griswold, the President of the Anaestia R.R. is married. Barbados has been appointed "Examiner of Deeds". The President and Mr. Matthews have been taken back in the prompt confirmation of Mrs. Trotter.  They expected he would be rejected.  Lawyer
