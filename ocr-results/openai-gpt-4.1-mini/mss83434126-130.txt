1861   Aug 6th   Congress closed its extra session - Congress adjourned

1861   Aug 7th   The town of Hampton Va destroyed by the rebles
                 The privateer York, burned by the U. S. Gunboat Union, the
                 crew taken prisoners

1861   Aug 10th  Battle of Wilson Creek Mo The federals under the
                 command Genl Lyon made a planned attack on the rebels
                 and drove them from their encampment and contested the field
                 for six hours hard fighting and in leading a bayonet charge
                 Genl Lyon was killed instantly.  Col Sigel carried out Lyon's
                 plans and retired in an orderly manner to Springfield,
                 having captured 400 horses and 70 prisoners.  Union loss 200
                 killed 700 wounded     Rebel loss in killed and wounded was
                 much greater.     The brave Genl Lyon the Hero of the West
                 gave his honor, his fortune ($10,000) and his life, to his country
                 that his country might live.  Number of Federal Troops engaged
                 was 5,000.   number of rebel troops engaged was 9000

1861   Aug 12th  President Lincoln issues a proclamation appointing
                 the 30th day of September as a day of fasting and prayer.

1861   Aug 14th  Major Genl John C Fremont declares martial law in the city
                 of St Louis Mo under martial law.

1861   Aug 16th  Major Genl John Cook joined in command of Fortress 
                 Monroe and its military surroundings
