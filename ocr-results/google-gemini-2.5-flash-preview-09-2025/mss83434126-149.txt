The federals were commanded by Genl. Curtis & Sigel.  The battle
was fought for three days without any decision result, but on the
3d Genl. Curtis made additional changes, and at early morn
he attacked the rebels along his whole line, the rebels were in-
stantly thrown with confusion and fled precipitately on all the
attack. The division of Van Dorm retracted in one direction
that of Rice escaped by another, The pursuit was Contin-
ued by Genl. Sigel toward Keithsville, and by the Cavalry to
the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete, Rebel loss
2000 killed and wounded also 1000 prisoners, among the rebel
dead was found Genl. McCullooh, a peculiar feature in
this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians, under
the Command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were di-
mostrated, after battle the federal slain, and wounded were
found to be scalped, thus renewing the primitive scenes of
sanguinary slaughter, Union loss 500 Killdo, and 900 Wounded.
