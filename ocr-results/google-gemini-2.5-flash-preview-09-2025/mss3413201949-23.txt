(1) The Senseless Waste of War
When the great War began 27 billions of gold War debts
stood upon the banks of Europe. The total coin money
and bullion in circulation in the whole world was about
eleven billions of dollars. Yet the nations plunged
full well into the War, borrowing all they could,
printing paper money, and not stopping to count the
cost. At the end the international debts were so
complicated and burdensome that a universal panic
followed. What senseless waste! Any wonder they
can not pay their debts to us?
Do you know that every man killed in the late
War cost the living $17,800?
Let me read you a little dialogue, an extract from
a conversation between a King and his prime minister
in the 3rd century before Christ. Cynes - Pyrrhus
