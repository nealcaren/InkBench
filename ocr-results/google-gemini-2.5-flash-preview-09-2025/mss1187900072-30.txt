Washington April 10" 1887
Dear Father
Your letter dated March 24" I
received yesterday. it found us all well, and
pleased to hear from you.
Mr. Trotter, the new Recorder of Deeds is se[r]
-iously sick with pneumonia. Gerret S. Loguen
has been appointed Deputy Recorder of Deeds.
The Lawyers are much put out about it.
Senator Sherman is making a trip South: in
Georgia. a Coloured delegation called at his ho-
tel, and was refused admission. as soon as
he heard of it, he paid his bill and went to
another hotel. A Colored man by the name
of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed
to the Liberian Mission. The political
as look at present on the Republican side
is wavering between Blaine and Sherman.
Congress has made an appropriation for a
new Bridge across the Eastern Branch 
at the foot of Penn'a. Ave., Business in
the Recorders Office is booming. Griswold,
the President of the Anacostia R.R. is mar-
ried. Barbadoes has been appointed "Com-
parer of Deeds." The President and Mrs.
Matthews have been taken back in the
prompt Confirmation of Mr. Trotter. They
expected he would be rejected. Lawyer
