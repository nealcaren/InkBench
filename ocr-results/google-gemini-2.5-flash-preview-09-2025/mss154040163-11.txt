OWEN C. BECKER, PROP.
July 15. 1907.

My dear Steady: I have to thank you for his
letters and the notes and clippings concerning
Mrs. Blinn. I thank you especially for the clip-
pings for as you suspect I am interested
in her outgoing. Last year, in Buffalo, at the
National Convention, I think, Mrs Swest brought
a letter she had received from Effie Vance,
and she had described very vividly how
Mrs. Blinn had been able to rise above her
suffering thro' Christian Science. So sob'd his
tears flowed as she read it, and she was no
great admirer of Mrs. B. I heard, she told
me that E. Vance was also getting under
studied in Ch. Sci. Mrs. B. was one of the most
generous and forgiving natures I ever
met, and altho she also had her weaknesses
of character, as well as the rest of us, she was
a woman to be admired for very many
estimable qualities.
