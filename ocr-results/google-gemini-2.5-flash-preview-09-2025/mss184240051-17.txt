[*The Light in the Tunnel: From the Topeka City Chronicle. A new device for the bedevilment of lovers was exemplified successfully by a fearless young man on the leavenworth train yesterday. A couple sat in the seat before him. The lady was young and modest, and the swain wore very large cuffs. One arm was thrown carelessly along the back of the seat, and upon this the evil-minded person placed a white paper phosphorus. The affection-ate performances of that arm, when the train was rush-ing through the kindly darkness of the tunnels, were be-held by all the unfeeling and snickering occupants of the car, whose cruel enjoyment was not a little increased by the demur and unaccountably surprised appearance of the lovers each time the train bounded again into light. The wife of Thomas H. Banner of Hagerstown had been warned before she married him that his temper was violent, and that sooner or later, he would whip her. She seems to have made up her mind what to do if he struck her, for when he did so, she drew a re-volver from her pocket and shot him dead. *]
