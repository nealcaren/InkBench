Buc. 8th of Sept. 1922

Dear Mrs. Chapman Catt,

We took in our program of this year
to read lectures on "Famous Women." Mrs Reuss Ianculescu said
I will do it, she in the L. D. I F and I in popular evening course.
But as there are no complete libraries in Roumania and not
a single feminist library, it is impossible for us even to know
the titles of the books, necessary for preparation. I address therefore 
to you as to the other sections of I.W.S.A to ask your help.

Feeling so much the hips of a fem. library we wil
try at one time with our studies, to found one at B. and
perhaps than will be able to assemble round it the
Women in a club. We are dreaming of such a long time.
Would your association be able for the generous act of xxxx
help our sisters and to send us one or the other standard works
on women movement in your country and specially
books, where we could find biographies of your famous women.
We would speak in a first series of lectures, on political and social
prominent Women and in a second Draw the portraits of women
in Art and Science. But we know so little, it is to say not much
