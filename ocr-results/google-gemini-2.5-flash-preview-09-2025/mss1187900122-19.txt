London Sunday May 6. 74.
Dear Mr. Douglass:
Your letter directed Manchester has followed me allround till it reached me here. I am indeed glad to get it. When I wrote to you
I had not seen your letter to Mr.
Aked; since seeing it I know
from what he and others said,
that while they did not expect
"gush" (may they pay you the same compliment) I do, in knowing
you to be incapable or such a thing,
still they would have been
better satisfied if you had
spoken more positively regarding me and my work. Mr. Aked thought that since the Society had failed so miserably and I was thus left to vouch for myself, you would not hesitate to vouch for me.
We cannot go thro the country telling people about this personal quarrel and why I am un-
accompanied by the Persons
who brought me over. As the
best known member of the

race, a positive letter orvoucher
