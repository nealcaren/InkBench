February 6, 1911.

Dear Miss Peck:

"WOMAN'S JOURNAL" of next week there will be a statement concerning boarding houses, hotels and steamers, and this is the best I can do in the way of information.

I am expecting to sail on March first. Lloyd, but if I fail there I shall try for a steam-er sailing the same date on the Hamburg American line. Miss H. is going with me. Do you want to go as early as that? It would be a great pleasure to have you go with us. I do not suppose you would enjoy staying in Stockholm so long, but I’d not be there a while anyway.

I have made my application for spaces on the North German

I suppose you are arranging for the German "Inter-allierte" conference, and I hope things will go well. We are apparently even getting more hopeful here, and “prussianizing” seems to be a great success.  Please do write me soon – I am always interested to hear news from You.

Yours always, 
Florence Kelley.
