Gens; Office and so here I am this accounting to you for my pleasant days of the oldest town in the United States, I spent but a shy an a half there after in historical associations and could have spent much longer time there very agreeably but I had to obey [end of paper two] orders and not inclinations. It is a paradise of orange groves and not inclinations. In the yard I noticed the date palm and banana as among the tropical fruits grown here fruit in the season of it which I judge to be at least from six weeks to two months earlier than with us as a little I have been here the weather has come down on us a little (degrees) the more I had to obey here in the same office that [Moore] left to become adjt. of our dept. as you may have heard. He took a fine leap up from a private to lst Lieut. and Adjt. but I consider it a very worthy bestowment of favor or influence. Sammy has come to be as tough as an alligator and at meal times has almost as open countenance. His company with one other garrison the Fort (Marion). It is a curious looking piece of old pottery but I judged it a material of which it is constructed. I should judge it some considerable of a battering before it would come down. Here of late two little sands on the main have sharpened up our appetites for something glorious from Vicksburg. We have four negro sergts in this Dept or parts of them besides the 54th Mass (colored) just here Col.
