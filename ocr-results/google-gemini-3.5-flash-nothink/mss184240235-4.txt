My dear Miss Dickinson
That you should last evening have
experienced any inconvenience whatever
has been to me a cause of much regret, yet
can I not admit that the responsibility there-
for rests at all on me. My position in re-
gard to succession to the presidential chair
is as well known as that of almost any
other person in the city, and I cannot but
regard it as having been a great mistake
on the party your friends that they should
have urged upon me in any measure to
preside as lecturer the whole drift of
which was to be in direct opposition to
my own political views. - 
In the very brief conversation which
preceeded my release you expressed surprise
that I should have known nothing of a lecture
