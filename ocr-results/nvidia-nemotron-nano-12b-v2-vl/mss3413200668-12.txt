**Part 1: Handwritten Pages (Corrected Notes)**  

**Round-the-World Study Project**  
*A World Crusade Against Communism*  

**Where RTWSP Students Will Go:**  
- The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will be made by air. It will take World Students into more than 25 foreign lands where they will be official guests.  
- Specially chartered flight of RTWSP will begin from Byrd Airfield in Richmond and make stops in various U.S. cities to receive the bon voyage applause of America. Stopping in Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, San Francisco. From California, stops include Hawaii, Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, and Burma. Then on to India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine.  
- Over 10 weeks, students will meet foreign heads of state, spread goodwill, visit antiquity, explore Africa (land of ancestors), and pay homage to Western civilization’s original centers (Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, London).  
- The return journey will be via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes:**  
- Fifty students from Virginia Union University (VUU). Candidates will be selected based on ability, recommendations, recommendation letters, and attitude. Character and intelligence, not money, will determine selection.  

**College Credit:**  
- Credit awarded: 9–12 hours, divided among courses like International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology, Field Work, etc.  
- Two university professors will escort and tutor the students. Various lectures/conferences/seminars will supplement their learning.  
- Students will live with local populations, take sightseeing tours, map-making courses, and maintain diaries/reports to fully round out their learning.  

**Where the Money Will Come From:**  
- Funded through cooperative financing (students, friends, relatives, organizations) and a special RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund.  
- Foreign governments will cover living expenses; a steward or chartered plane charter will reduce costs (0–$250 fare).  
- Final cost per student depends on individual needs.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund:**  
- Provides need-based scholarships for students, awarded via a university committee.

**Part 2: Typed Letter**  
*Bern, the 20 August 1920*  
*Kalcheggweg 20*  
*By M-mail, Leuch 20 Kalcheggweg 20, BERN.*  

**To:** Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt  
*President of the Leslie Woman Suffrage Commission*  
*New York*  

**Dear Mrs. Catt,**  

I am sure you received my last letter with the receipt and my very best thanks for your draft. I have the liberty to submit to you the following communications and motions to be transmitted to the Leslie Woman Suffrage Commission in September.  

1. **Request for Grant Augmentation:**  
   - Melle Gourd requested an increase of the subvention (grant) from $500 to $1000 for 1925.  
   - I assured her that raising this additional $500 would not negatively portray our poor suffragists.  

2. **Report on Fund Use:**  
   - A report will be sent in a few weeks on how the funds were used. At a central Swiss board meeting this autumn, we will make a final decision on the proper use of the grant.  

3. **Proposed New Grant for 1926:**  
   - Let me know if the Leslie Commission could grant us another $1000 in addition to the $1000 already received for 1926.  

With deepest gratitude for your genuine interest in our movement, I remain yours sincerely.  

**Annie Leuch Peliche**  
*Treasurer, Swiss Alliance for Woman Suffrage*  

*Annexure No. 2*
