

### Manual Video Game

#### Introduction
Manual video games are interactive experiences that require players to perform specific sequences of actions, or "moves," on a gaming device. These games often blend entertainment with educational elements, challenging players to think critically and strategically while navigating complex rules and environments.

#### Game Platforms
- **Console**: Traditional gaming systems like PlayStation, Xbox, or Nintendo Switch.
- **Computer**: PC games that require a keyboard and mouse.
- **Handheld Console**: Portable devices like the Nintendo 3DS or Steam Deck.
- **Smart Device**: Mobile games played on smartphones or tablets.

#### Controller Types
Different games utilize various controller setups:
- **Gamepad Joystick**: Used for movement (e.g., left hand on joystick, right hand on buttons).
- **Start/Select Button**: For pausing or exiting the game.
- **Esc Keyboard**: Often used in PC games to exit the game or menu.

#### Gameplay Mechanics
Players engage in actions such as:
- **Joystick Motion**: Navigating character movements.
- **Button Presses**: Interacting with game environments (e.g., attacking, defending).

#### Educational and Strategic Elements
Many manual video games incorporate realistic scenarios, often inspired by real-world challenges. These can include:
- **Resource Allocation**: Similar to "Strategy for hospital resource allocation."
- **Risk Management**: Understanding the implications of decisions made within the game.

#### Conclusion
Manual video games offer a unique blend of entertainment and education, allowing players to immerse themselves in various scenarios while developing critical thinking and problem-solving skills. By engaging with these games, players can explore complex rules and strategies that mirror real-life situations.
