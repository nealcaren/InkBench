

### Complete Transcript of Item 1: "**Round-the-World Study Project"**  
**World Crusade Against Communism**  

**Where RTWSP Students Will Go**: The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will be made by air and take participants into more than 25 different foreign lands, where they will be official guests. The specially chartered flight will begin from Byrd Airfield in Richmond and make stops in various U.S. cities to receive bon voyage applause, including Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, and San Francisco. From California, no further stops until alighting in Hawaii. From there, the route continues to Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam (Thailand), Burma (Myanmar), India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine. Over a 10-week span, participants will meet foreign heads of state, extend friendship networks, tour ancient sites (e.g., Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, and London), and explore the ancient shrines and civilizations of Africa – the ancestral homeland. The expedition concludes in Richmond via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes**: Fifty Virginia Union University students. Selection criteria prioritize ability, recommendation, and attitude over financial capacity. Traits like character and intelligence determine eligibility rather than monetary contributions.  

**College Credit**: Participants earn 9–12 credit hours apportioned across courses like International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology Fieldwork, and oath-Taking (transcript note: misspelled). Two university professors will tutor the project. Foreign universities and governments will host lectures, conferences, and seminars. Participants will live with host families, partake in cultural excursions, and maintain detailed diaries/reports.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**: Funded via collaborative financing from participants, their networks, organizations, and the "Assrance Scholarship Fund." The latter provides scholarships awarded by a university committee. Participants bear living expenses unless subsidized by host governments. A shared charter cost model reduces individual airfare expenses. No participation fee exists; costs vary by individual needs.  

**Assrance Scholarship Fund**: Provides needs-based scholarships awarded via university committees.

### Complete Transcript of Item 3: **Press Release – "Press Release: For PMs, Thursday, March 30"**  
**Release Details**:  
- **Case**: District of Columbia v. John R. Thompson Co.  
- **Court**: 2nd Floor, Municipal Court, Criminal Division.  
- **Time**: 1:30 PM on March 31, 1954.  

**Case Summary**:  
- **Issue**: Testing anti-discrimination laws (1872, 1873) prohibiting racial segregation in public establishments.  
- **Thompson Cafeteria** (725 14th St. NW) prosecuted for refusing service to an African American group on 28 February 1950.  

**Complainants**:  
- **Mary Church Terrell** (D.C. Board of Education, NAACP charter member).  
- **David H. Scull** (Society of Friends).  
- **Rev. Arthur F. Elmes** (Peoples Congregational Church).  
- **Essie Thompson** (United Cafeteria & Restaurant Workers Union).  

**Attorneys**:  
- **Clark King** (prosecuting attorney).  
- Mary Terrell, Scull, and Essie Thompson’s committee (Mary Chairwoman).  

**Legal Rulings**:  
- Coroner's ruling enforces past anti-discrimination statutes, but the Thompson Corporation Counsel notes no further prosecutions until court rulings. Some restaurants may comply by 1950.  

**Amicus Brief**:  
- Filed by the **Coordinating Committee for the Enforcement of D.C. Anti-Discrimination Laws** (organized post-1949 Nat. Lawyers Guild opinion), cites inconsistent enforcement and cites Washington’s compliance. Supports aggressive enforcement.  

**Statements**:  
- Committee claims, "Washington is trying to reclaim its title as the greatest democracy on Earth" by enforcing laws. Mentions cooperation from other cities except Washington.  

**Contact Information**:  
- **Mrs. Annie Stein** (Secretary, Committee).  
- **Margaret A. Haywood** (Attorney, Committee).
