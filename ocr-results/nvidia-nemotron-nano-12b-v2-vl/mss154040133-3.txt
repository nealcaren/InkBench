**round-the-world study project  
world crusade against communism**

**where rtwsp students will go:**  
the round-the-world journey of rtwsp will be made by air and will take world students into more than 25 different foreign lands, where they will be official guests in each. the specially chartered flight of rtwsp will begin from byrd airfield in richmond and make stops in various united states cities to receive the bon voyage applause of america. cities listed: washington, baltimore, philadelphia, pittsburgh, detroit, chicago, st. louis, los angeles, and san francisco. from california, no other stops until they alight in hawaii. from there to wake island, tokyo, formosa, the philippines, indonesia, singapore, cambodia, siam, and burma. then to india, pakistan, arabia, ethiopia, the sudan, egypt, and palestine. in these 10 weeks, students will meet heads of foreign states, proclaim faith in democracy and their arm of friendship, visit ancient shrines of antiquity, see africa (land of their ancestors), and visit original centers of western civilization: athens, rome, paris, antwerp, and london. the journey concludes with a return to richmond via ireland, canada, and new york.

**who goes:**  
fifty virginia union university students. selection is based on ability, recommendation, and attitude. character and intelligence, rather than financial capacity, determine selection.

**college credit:**  
credit given ranges between 9 and 12 hours. credit will be apportioned among such courses as international affairs, cultural relations, art appreciation, geography, anthropology, and field work. two university professors will escort and tutor students. foreign universities and government representatives will hold special lectures, conferences, and seminars. students will live with local populations wherever possible. additional activities include sightseeing tours, planned study outlines, special mapmaking, trip diaries, written reports, and scrapbooks to complete the learning abroad program.

**where the money will come from:**  
funding comes from cooperative financing—students, friends, relatives, and organizations. a special rtwsp assistance scholarship fund is being created. friends of the university and interested organizations will contribute. foreign governments visited will cover students’ living expenses extensively. savings are achieved through a special aircraft charter (rather than purchasing individual fares). no fixed participation fee—cost depends on individual needs (e.g., diaries, maps, medical expenses). regulations are in place to cap charges.

**assistance scholarship fund:**  
this fund provides scholarships to each needy participant in varying amounts. scholarships are awarded through a university-appointed committee. selected criteria prioritize need.
