The following is a copy of a letter dated April 22, 1889, sent to the internal consul at the Navy Department in London by the Assistant Secretary of the Navy, Dr. Seth B. Wylie.

Re. Lyceum arrangements.- Pray direct meh to forward to you the published action of the Ebenezer Lyceum, as directed, and ask your acceptance of the same as taken by these and request for your great acknowledgment. Catherine, Meadedy Att'y, & Secretary.

The undersigned acting as co-chairs of the Ebenezer Lyceum had an interesting meeting yesterday afternoon, Hon. F.S. Atkinson, President and Assistant Secretary. Hon. E. E. Ryder, Secretary, sang the lyrics of a duet and listened to an address.

Re. Published announcement.—Pray direct me to forward to you the published action of the Ebenezer Lyceum, as directed, and ask your acceptance of the same as taken by these and request for your great acknowledgment. Catherine, Meadedy Att'y, & Secretary.

HEREIN IT IS CERTIFIED THAT I, FREDERICK DOUGLASS, AGED FIFTY FOUR YEARS, RESIDING AT NEW ROCCUM, MASSACHUSETTS, HSUBSCRIBE THAT—WHEREAS IT IS A DUTY WE OWE TO EVERY CITIZEN TO EXPRESS OUR GRATITUDE FOR ANY AND EVERY INTELLIGENT AND VIRTUOUS ACT OF PUBLIC INTEREST AND BENEFIT DONE FOR THE GOOD OF THE COMMUNITY; AND WHProcured HONESTLY SPECULOUSLY IN THE SOUNDS AND WORDS OF WISDOM SPOKEN WITH HONEST COURAGE FOR THE GOOD OF THE WHOLE PEOPLE ANDHIS COUNTRY; NOWTHEREFORE, (LE), I DO HEREBY SUBSCRIBE AND APPOINT HIM (WISDOM) AS MY PUBLIC TEACHER AND GUIDE, A SOLEMN PROMISE AND OBLICATION PERMANENTLY, REISH, AND REVOKABLE ONLY BY MYSELF, TO ALLAS CONCERNING SAID INTELLIGENCE AND VIRTUE RESPECTING THE GOOD OF THE COMMUNITY; AND I, FREDERICK DOUGLASS, REISH, AND BY THESE POWERS OF SPEECH, SUBSCRIBE TO ALLS CONCERNING SAID INTELLIGENCE AND VIRTUE RESPECTING THE GOOD OF THE COMMUNITY, ON THISES DOCUMANT HANDBOOK II. RESPENDENTLY WITNESSED BY HERSEL METBY (NOTED AND NOTARIZED).

SPENDATHER AND

Signed,  Fredrick Douglass
