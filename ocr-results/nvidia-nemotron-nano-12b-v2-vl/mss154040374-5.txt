

## Concerning Department of Books  
**September 28, 1937.**  

**Mrs. Mary R. Beard,**  
New Milford, Conn.  

**Dear Mrs. Beard:**  

I am only trying to fill in some deficiencies  
in my library. There were some books which appeared  
before I arrived in this world that I have never seen  
and I am sure they would be only amusing and not in the  
least valuable, but I am sure that some of the old suf-  
fragists must have had some of these books and I am trying  
very hard to chase them down.  

When and if I am able to complete my collection  
I shall write and tell you what I have and what I will consider  
what to do with them.  

I am glad you got such a start as those come as standard  
I think American history should remain in this country, but  
the women in Amsterdam have had a remarkable start that  
their archives will turn out to be of great authority and importance  
in Europe. A bountiful space, for which there is no charge for rent,  
houses the Amsterdam archives.  

I have consulted you and shall make no disposition of  
my collection until I have consulted you.  

**Sincerely,**  
**Justine Haven**  
**Justine**  
**C.C.: H.W.**  

&#160;You’ll need to carry her out of life at  
once. The just letter I’ll have from her here pay me  
me coherent & sufficient.  

This is to keep you informed of matters looking  
"blind in law" only. Had I demanded it sooner, I might  
just have done so, advised not too aggressively however.  

He has been a capital assistant and has done a great service,  
but I think it's best that he be dismissed. Never a signer,  
but staying on in a non signer capacity.  

Set this typeface for type (11-point Roman).  

He was in the wrong mood for this kind of thing.
