A criterion of approval from the "MASTER OF ALL LODGES IN BRITAIN AND THE UNITED STATES," indicating its authority.

## THE HIGH PRIESTHOOD IN THE ROYAL ARCH CHAPTER  

The duties of the Chief Officer are discussed, with debates on whether its conferral is attributed to the Supreme Architect or human intervention. The text compares divine judgment to the omnipotent spirit and reflects on the role of sacred texts in ritual practices.  

---

## GREAT VOLTS-ROYALE-CHAPTERE  
References to Masonic degrees such as Chosen, Holy Royal Arch, Past and Most Excellent Mark Master. Emphasizes the historical and ritual significance of these degrees in Royal Arch Masonry.  

---

- **James L. Gould, D. G. H. P. of Connecticut.**

- **Holy Royal Arch,**  
- **Past and Most Excellent Mark Master,**  
- **The Manual of the Chapter,** based on the text of John Sheville and James L. Gould, with Emendations and Critical Notes.
