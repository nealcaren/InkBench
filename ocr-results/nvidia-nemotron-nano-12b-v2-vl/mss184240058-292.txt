  **Where RTWSP Students Will Go:**  
  -   The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will be made by air. It will take World Students into more than 25 different foreign lands where they will be official guests in each.  
  -   On the list are Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, San Francisco.  
  -   From California no other stop until they arrive in Hawaii. From there to Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, and Burma.  
  -   Then onward to India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine.  
  -   Over the 10-week journey, they will meet heads of foreign states, extending their arm of friendship and goodwill. They will see the ancient shrines of antiquity and the continent of Africa—land of their ancestors.  
  -   They will pay homage to the original centers of Western civilization—Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, and London. Their global crusade will return them via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes**:  
-   Fifty Virginia Union University students. World Students will be selected from many sections of the United States. Selection criteria: ability, recommendation, and attitude. Character and intelligence (not money) determine selectees.  

**College Credit**:  
-   Credit ranges from 9 to 12 hours. Apportioned across courses like International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology Field Work, etc.  
-   Two university professors will escort and tutor the project. Special lectures, conferences, seminars, etc., will be offered by foreign universities and government representatives.  
-   As far as possible, World Students will live with host populations. Specific tours, outlined study schedules, mapmaking, diaries, reports, and scrapbooks will structure RTWSP's overseas learning.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**:  
-   Funding staged via **Cooperative Financing**: students, friends, relatives, and organizations contribute. A **RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund** (currently being created) supports selectees.  
-   Partner governments cover students' living expenses abroad via a special charter for a plane (cost-saving vs. individual fare purchases).  
-   No fixed participation fee; costs depend on individual needs.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund**:  
-   Administered internally, the fund provides scholarships uniformly awarded by universities and employer-provided health insurance during excused absences for students on co-op programs.
