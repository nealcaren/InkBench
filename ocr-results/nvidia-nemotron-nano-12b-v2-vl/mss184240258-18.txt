**"Round-the-World Study Project"**  
**"World Crusade Against Communism"**  

**Where RTWSP Students Will Go**: The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will involve charter flights from Byrd Airfield in Richmond. Students will travel to more than 25 foreign lands, with stops across the United States (Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, and San Francisco). From California, the journey continues to Hawaii, Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, and Burma. Following this, stops include India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine. Over a 10-week journey, students will meet foreign state leaders, promote democracy and goodwill, visit ancient shrines in Africa and other continents, and explore Europe’s historic sites like Athens and Rome. The final return leg is via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes**: Fifty Virginia Union University students, selected based on ability, attitude, and character rather than financial status.  

**College Credit**: Students will earn between 9 and 12 hours of college credit. Courses include International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology, and Field Work. Two university professors will lead the project, supplemented by foreign university lectures, seminars, and guided tours. Students will reside with local populations, and activities will include sightseeing, map-making, trip diaries, and written reports.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**: Funding will come from cooperative financing involving students, their networks, and organizations. An RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund will provide scholarships. Living expenses, largely covered by foreign governments, reduce costs. A special charter plane will save on airfare expenses, with no participation fee; only individual costs affect affordability.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund**: Scholarships will cover varying amounts for needy participants. Awarded through a university-assigned committee.
