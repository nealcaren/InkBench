## History and Geography Evenings:  
#### A Series of Dialogues  

At the Round-the-World Study Project, Global Missions members embark on a journey to more than 25 countries. Stops include the United States (Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles), Hawaii, Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, Manila, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam (Thailand), Burma, India, Pakistan, Ethiopia, Sudan, Egypt, Palestine, Iran, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Java, Sumatra, Africa, "the cradle of western civilization," and European nations, ending in Richmond, Virginia by way of Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes:** Fifty Virginia Union University students selected based on ability, recommendation, and attitude rather than financial means.  

**College Credit:** Credit ranges between 9–12 hours, earned in courses such as "International Affairs," "Cultural Relations," "Art Appreciation," "Geography," "Anthropology," and "Field Work." Two university professors lead the project, supplemented by foreign experts. Students immerse themselves in local cultures, participate in lectures, museums, and sporting events.  

**Funding:** Financed through cooperative contributions from students, alumni, organizations, and foreign governments covering essential expenses. Individual costs depend on participant needs. Scholarships are awarded based on need.
