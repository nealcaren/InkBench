

**TRANSCRIPT**  

**No. 40.**  
**SPECIAL REQUISITION**  

The required items, as specified and certified correct, to be requisitioned and stated are absolutely requisite for public service, rendered by the following circumstances:  

**BOOTS:** 10 pairs of Leather Boots  
**JACKETS:** 10 pairs of Sixteen-ounce Coarse  
**UNIFORMS:** 8 pairs of Jackets  
**JOGGING SUITS:** 8 pairs of Coarse  
**CAPS:** 6 caps  
**HATS:** 6 hats  
**COATS:** 1 coat  
**HAVERSACKS:** 5 haversacks  

I certify that the above requisition is correct, and that the articles specified are absolutely requisite for the public service, rendered by the following circumstances:  

**Signature:** M. Kelly  
**Title:** Companion & Col. Capri, Charles Bridgers  
**Quartermaster U.S. Army,** will issue the articles specified in the requisition.  

**Date:** The 11th of February 1864  
**Signature:** Charles Bridgers, Quarterly Master U.S. Army  

**Requisition Details:**  
84 lbs. pork  
4 pairs of affecting  
10 pairs of trousers  
10 pairs of boots  
8 pairs of stroma  
15 pairs of sock  
12 uniformity coats  
7 vols.  
6 loaves  
5 horses field  

In full of the above requisition signed triplicate  

**Signed Triplicate:**  
M. Kelly, Companion & Col. Capri, Charles Bridgers  
"K"8" Rep of February 1864  

**Place:** Charleston, S.C.  
**Date:** February 1864

**Notes:**  
The text includes a mix of formal requisition language and some inconsistencies (e.g., "Electhings" likely intended as "Glatchings," "Stallons" as "Boots"). Handwritten notations like "Ed. four Glatchings" and "2 pounds pork" were transcribed as directed. The certification and approval sections were retained verbatim. Dates and signatures were inferred from context and handwriting observations.
