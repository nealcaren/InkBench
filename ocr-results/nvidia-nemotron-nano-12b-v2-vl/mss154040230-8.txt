### Page 1: Round-the-World Study Project  
**"Round-the-World Study Project"**  
*World Crusade Against Communism*  

---  
**Where RTWSP Students Will Go**:  
The **round-the-world journey** of RTWSP will be made by air. It will **take more than 25 different foreign lands**, where students will be official guests. The **specially chartered flight** will begin from Byrd airfield in Richmond and make stops in various U.S. cities: **Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, and San Francisco**. From California, the next stop is **Hawaii**, then **Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, Burma**. The journey continues to **India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine**. Over the 10-week journey, students will **meet heads of foreign states**, extend friendship and goodwill, and visit ancient shrines of antiquity, including the **continent of Africa** (land of their ancestors). They will pay homage to the original centers of Western civilization: **Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, and London**. The trip concludes with stops in **Ireland, Canada, and New York**.  

**Who Goes**:  
**Fifty Virginia Union University students**, selected based on **ability, recommendation, and attitude** (character and intelligence over money).  

**College Credit**:  
Credit given will range between **9 to 12 hours**, allocated to courses like **International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology, Field Work, etc.** Two university professors will **escort and tutor** students. Additional activities include seminars, study outlines, map-making, **trip diaries**, written reports, and scrapbooks.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**:  
Funding comes from **cooperative financing**: students, friends, relatives, and organizations. A **special RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund** will cover living expenses in visited countries. **Foreign governments** will help through **special chartered planes** (avoiding individual fares). Costs depend on participant **individual needs and abilities**.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund**:  
A **university-appointed committee** awards scholarships to **needy participants** in varying amounts.

### Page 2: Diplomatic Correspondence  
**National Feminist Union**  
**Buenos Aires, January 17, 1922**  

---  
**Translated by**:  
**Medice Adams**-to-Archentine  

---  
**Recipient**:  
Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt  
New York  

---  
**Letter Content**:  
**My Dear Madam:**  
I have had several interviews with the **Minister of Foreign Relations, Senor Honorio Pueyrredon and his secretary**, in which the following has been established: The Argentine government is **not able to name an official delegate** for the Congress of Baltimore. This is clear from the note of **Nov. 28, 1921**, a copy of which has been officially sent to me by the Charge d’Affaires of the United States. This is further supported by Mr. Fracis Wlute’s transmission to your government, stating: *"In transmitting this invitation to the government of your excellency, I must draw your attention to the fact that the invitation is not official, as it emanates from a private organization. Although the government of the United States sees with pleasure all conferences which have as object the promotion of friendly understanding and closer relations between American citizens, it will have no part in the coming conference."*  

**Key Points**:  
1. Mr. Wlute reiterates that the invitation is **not official**, as it originates from a **private organization**.  
2. The government of the United States supports **all conferences promoting friendly relations** but will **not participate** in the upcoming one.  
3. The Charge d’Affaires, after interviewing the Minister, noted the latter’s insistence that the matter be resolved diplomatically.  

**Marginalia**:  
-*Translated by Medice Adams*  
*Argentine*  
- **Note**: "Do not send this letter to the Argentine government, as it will not be needed. Send only the typed portion."
