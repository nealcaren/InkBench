

This sheet contains two distinct texts. The primary text is a formal letter dated August 8, 1923, from Carrie Chapman Catt, President of the Leslie Woman Suffrage Commission, Inc., to Miss Clara M. Hyde at 366 Madison Avenue, New York. The letter discusses a book Rosa Hyde mentioned to Catt, which was incorrectly recalled by Catt as "Her Secret." Catt asks Hyde to confirm the correct title for her records and mentions other details like the dry heat in New York compared to Panama, Mary Gray Peck's visit, and financial expectations regarding Hyde's upcoming visit. The letter concludes with a request for information about coupons. The signature at the bottom is Catt's, with "President" typed underneath.
