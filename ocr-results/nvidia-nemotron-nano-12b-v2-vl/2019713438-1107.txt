**Who Goes:**  
Fifty Virginia Union University students will be selected based on ability, recommendation, and attitude. Character and intelligence, rather than financial means, determine eligibility.

**College Credit:**  
Students earn between 9 and 12 hours of college credit. Credit applies to courses such as:  
- International Affairs  
- Cultural Relations  
- Art Appreciation  
- Geography  
- Anthropology Field Work  
Two university professors will escort and tutor the group. Participating foreign universities and government representatives will offer special lectures, conferences, and seminars.

**Where the Money Will Come From:**  
Funding comes from cooperative financing. Sources include:  
- Students themselves  
- Friends, relatives, and organizations  
- A special RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund (still in creation)  
- Interested organizations and concerns  
- Host countries covering living expenses proportionally  
A special charter plane is used instead of individual fares, eliminating participation fees. Costs depend on individual needs and abilities.

**Assistance Scholarship Fund:**  
A scholarship program for needy participants, awarded in varying amounts via a university-appointed committee.
