

WRECKS AND RESCUES. By an EARLY MEMBER of the BOARD of MANAGERS of the A. F. G. S. REVISED BY THE PUBLISHING COMMITTEE. LIFE'S ocean has its WRECKS—e’er which we weep— Strewn long the coast and founded in the deep; Its rocks and shoals, the treacherous calm beneath, Its stormy blasts, that spread destruction—death. It has its RESCUES too—for Christian bands. Stand on the shores of time, with outstretched hands, To sue to imperiled, and to save, From dangers here, and death beyond the grave.
