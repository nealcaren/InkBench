### **"Round-the-World Study Project"**  
In **bold**, beneath quotation marks: **"Round-the-World Study Project"**  
*World Crusade Against Communism*  

**Where RTWSP Students Will Go**: The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will be made by air and it will take World Students into more than 25 different foreign lands, where they will be official guests in each. The specially chartered flight of RTWSP will begin from Byrd Airfield in Richmond and will make stops in various United States cities to receive the bon voyage applause of America. On the list are Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, and San Francisco. From California no other stop until they alight in Hawaii. From there to Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, and Burma. Then on to India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine. On the 10-week journey, they will meet heads of foreign states, extend their arm of friendship and goodwill, visit ancient shrines of antiquity, and traverse the continent of Africa—land of their ancestors. They will pay homage to the original centers of Western civilization—Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, and London. Their global-girdling crusade will return them to Richmond via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes**: Fifty Virginia Union University students. World students will be selected from many sections of the United States. Selected based on ability, recommendation, and attitude. Character and intelligence rather than monetary value will determine selectees.  

**College Credit**: Credit awarded will range between 9 to 12 hours. Credit will be apportioned across courses in International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology Field Work, etc. Two university professors will tutor and escort the project. Lectures, conferences, seminars, etc., will be co-hosted by foreign universities and government representatives. To maximize cultural immersion, students will reside with host families. Supplemental experiences such as guided sightseeing, cartography workshops, and annotated journals will enrich their transnational curriculum.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**: Funding will derive from cooperative financing—the Responsibility Fund, contributions from the RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund, and subsidies negotiated with host nations. Participating governments in visited territories are expected to assume a portion of living costs. The RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund, administered via an independent university committee, will provide bursarial support based on individual economic circumstances.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund**: A decentralized committee, appointed by the university’s Board of Trustees, will disburse scholarship funds. Financial aid will be granted exclusively to students demonstrating socio-economic need. Distribution criteria will weigh heavily on family income, asset liquidity, and duration of civic employment.
