

**“Round-the-World Study Project”**  
**World Crusade Against Communism**  

**Where RTWSP Students Will Go**:  
The round-the-world journey of RTWSP will be made by air and will take World Students into more than 25 different foreign lands, where they will be official guests in each. The specially chartered flight of RTWSP will begin from Byrd Airfield in Richmond and will make stops in various United States cities to receive the "bon voyage applause of America." The U.S. stops include Washington, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Detroit, Chicago, St. Louis, Los Angeles, and San Francisco. From California, there are no other stops until the students arrive in Hawaii. From there, the flight route continues to Wake Island, Tokyo, Formosa, the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Siam, and Burma. The journey then proceeds to India, Pakistan, Arabia, Ethiopia, the Sudan, Egypt, and Palestine. During the 10-week trip, students will meet heads of foreign states, engage with local populations to demonstrate their commitment to democracy and their rejection of communism, visit ancient shrines across Africa (the continent of their ancestors), and explore the birthplaces of Western civilization, including Athens, Rome, Paris, Antwerp, and London. The expedition concludes with a return to Richmond via Ireland, Canada, and New York.  

**Who Goes**:  
Fifty Virginia Union University students. Selection criteria include ability, recommendation, and attitude. Academic achievement, character, and intelligence—not financial status—will determine eligibility.  

**College Credit**:  
Credit hours for the study abroad project range between 9 and 12. The credit applies to courses such as International Affairs, Cultural Relations, Art Appreciation, Geography, Anthropology Field Work, and other relevant disciplines. Two university professors will accompany and tutor the group. Additional academic enrichment will include special lectures, conferences, seminars, and opportunities to reside with host families in visited countries. Students will fully immerse themselves through site-seeing tours, pre-planned study plans, map-making projects, journaling, writing reports, and collecting scrapbooks, ensuring a comprehensive educational experience.  

**Where the Money Will Come From**:  
Funding will be generated through **CO-Operative Financing**, which includes contributions from students, their families, relatives, and supporting organizations. A dedicated **RTWSP Assistance Scholarship Fund** has been established to aid financially disadvantaged participants. The governments of countries visited will cover the living expenses of students during their stay. The project benefits from a charter airline arrangement, reducing costs significantly compared to purchasing individual tickets. Participation fees are optional, though not mandatory, as expenses are kept minimal. Costs will ultimately vary depending on each student’s personal requirements.  

**Assistance Scholarship Fund**:  
The **Assistance Scholarship Fund** aims to provide financial aid to needy participants in amounts determined by a specialized university-appointed committee. Scholarships will be distributed individually, ensuring equitable access to the program for all qualifying students.
