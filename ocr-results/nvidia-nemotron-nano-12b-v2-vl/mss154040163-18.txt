

**
Mrs. Catt's Itinerary up to Nov. 7, 1911

1. Care of Union Castle Line, Cape Town, South Africa. Up to Sept. 5th; allowing six weeks for passage.  
    - **Annotations**: Unclear circling and scribbles: "Letter mails Sept 6 1911" (circled), "$4000," "3 weeks," "Sept 18th," and "Uncertain, noting important here."  

2. Union Castle Line, Durban, Natal, So. Africa. Up to Sept. 25th; same allowance of time.  

3. Zanzibar, East Africa. Going North, SS. Gason, Union Castle Line, about Oct. 6th. (Steamers going south carry mail) Concerns about mail uncertainty.  

4. Package details: 1 package sent.  

5. Care of Cooks, Pt. Said, Egypt. Arriving Oct. 17th. (3 weeks should be enough time to allow.)  

6. Care of Cooks, Jerusalem, Palestine. For Oct. 25th.  

7. Care of Cooks, Cairo, Egypt. Nov. 1 to 7th.  
    - **Annotations**: "Other addresses later" in parentheses.  

**Handwritten Correspondence:

[Transcription above, continue after:**  
1. To "My Dear Lizzie": A reflection on Lizzie’s efficiency and character, addressing doubts with an anecdote about me. Mentions H. Shober’s efficiency, Mrs. Steele, Judy’s pricing, and Joe O’Neil’s offer to write a letter. Ends with affection, signed Agnes.  
    - *Date unclear but precedes Sept 18th.*  

2. To "My Dear Agnes": Thanks Agnes for a visit and scarf gift. References Joe O’Neil’s opinion and mentions Mrs. Jessop arranging Clara for Germany. Tearful sign-off.  
    - *Dated Sept 18th.*  

3. To "My Dear Agnes": Complains about a ready-made card, not a letter as requested. Notes Joe O’Neil’s refusal to write correspondence. Home updates: Arnold Clyde’s help, Carley’s service, and school semesters. Signed Agnes.*  

**Notes**:  
- Mixed styles: Typed itinerary with annotations, cursive handwriting for personal letters.  
- Evidence of travel planning, correspondence, and personal reflections spanning Sept–Nov 1911.  
- Key themes: Concern for service quality, family matters, and hometown updates.
