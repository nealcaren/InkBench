

A short written note on the top refers to Dr. Tibbain Johnson, who is representing three groups of women as to rural organizations, Smith Leverill, and sends $10,000 annually.  

A longer note mentions Mrs. Johnson, women's suffrage (Suffragists?) and talks about bringing up women's rights that have been denied since forever. The Legislature's equal opportunities, and the Legislature itself well appropriation. The equal opportunity amendment will be brought up, and women will be able to claim the rights that have been denied since forever. The Legislature is for the equal opportunity of women, and the suffragists demand that women should have fair share of all things. Only if they demand it will they legislate. Dr. Johnson knows more about state legislation, and he wants to know what more can be done for state equal opportunities. Mrs. Johnson writes that women have better chances with the legislature and legislatives. The note ends by requesting Dr. Johnson to write more about state suffrage demanding. It has been signed off "Violence to recommend State Suffrains to do that".
