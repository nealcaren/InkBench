About a month later, the Secretary of War, for the first time, authorized the raising of negro troops, by directing General Rufus Sax-ton to arm, uniform, equip and receive into the service of the United States such numbers of volunteers tion of African descent as the might deem expedient, not exceeding five thousand, and to detail officers to instruct and command them in September, the Union victory at Antietam so strengthened the administration that the President at once issued his preliminary Emancipation, which was to go into effect January 1. After that step all logical objection to using the negroes as a military factor ceased. On January 1, 1863 (now just fifty years ago), President Lincoln issued his final Emancipation Proclamation, and the project of making the negroes as soldiers was then considered man-govibly; but not until the 22d of May following was the Bureaus of Colored of the Datepartment established in the Department of War government itself undertook the War military force.
