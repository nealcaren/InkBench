1710 Locust St. Philadelphia
March 21, 1871

My dear Sister,

I write, first of all, about Ederinus,—because after a long talk we held about himself last night, that takes the first place. I read thy little note to him from Friday in which, while thou hopes to find him better, thou says "I fear me—". There has been nothing startlingly new with him, only there seems to be less of him from week to week, and his energy and ability to go or do anything grows less continually. Last night, after one had gone to bed, he spoke for the first time.
