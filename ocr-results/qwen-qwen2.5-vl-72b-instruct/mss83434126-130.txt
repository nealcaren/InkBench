1861 August 10th Battle of Wilson Creek, Mo. The Federals under the command Genl. Lyon, made a planned attack on the rebels and them from their encampment and contested the field for six hours hard fighting and on leading a bayonet charge Genl. Lyon was killed violently. Col. Sigel carried out Lyon's plan, and retired in an orderly manner to Springfield, having captured 400 horses and 500 prisoners. Union loss 200, killed 700 wounded. Rebel loss in killed and wounded was much greater, The brave Genl. Lyon the Hero of the West gave his honor his fortune (400000) and his life, to his country that his country might live, Number of Federal Troops engaged was 5000 number of rebel troops engaged was quad (5000)
