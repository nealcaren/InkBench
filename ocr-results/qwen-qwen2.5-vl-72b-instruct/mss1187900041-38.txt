Mr. W.D. Kelley has my application for a clerkship in the Treasury in hand and shortly, and both Lew and Fred are doing all they can for me. Libbie is expecting to be sick nothing and I hate to tell them of my circumstances as I know either of them would share their last dollar with me. Judge Kelley has got Sec'y Boutwell to promise me a $1600 clerkship provided I will do some extra writing for the Judge out of office hours. So at her own expense to take care of Lizzie. I would have to pay a nurse here $6 a week. I spent little time. Senator Pomeroy gave me a very flattering recommendation, as also did Senator Pomeroy, Hon. Geo. W. Julian, and Hon. Davis member from our Dist. who all the gentlemen to work. I could borrow the money here, but as I have not had that to do thus far I dislike to ask anybody here to loan me any. I want now about $100 until I can get to my appointment is only a question of a few days ago in paying for my fence, garden tools, seed, and a new stove which cost me $25 with all the furniture.
