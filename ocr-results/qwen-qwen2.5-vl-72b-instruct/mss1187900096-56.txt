Nantucket, July 18, 1891.

My dear friends:—

Mary Charlotte and I,
Congratulate you upon your safe
arrival back to Washington.
Charlotte is sojourning in Nantucket
for a few weeks. Mrs. Mann re-
mains at New Haven. Mrs. No.
comes frequently to Pass the day
with me and I return the
Visit. She suggested my writing
to you. Writing has become
quite burdensome to her she
is so feeble —steadily like whistle
down! But that she has improved
since her arrival on the Island
is clearly evident. She did not think
of walking out at all
when I was in New Haven — now,
she does more walking than
