Washington, D.C.

January 19, 1957

My dear Mr. Tinsley,

I am enclosing copies of resolutions adopted by the legislatures of Illinois and North Carolina urging the President to sign the Gardner bill. He is pledged to sign the bill but if he fails to do so, the pressure from these and other legislatures would be such that he could not ignore it.

I have received similar expressions from several other states, Rhode Island among them. If the President receives more and more expressions of hope for this form of legislation, it will be of great assistance, as we spoke of.

Yours truly,

Carl Hayden

(Nev.)

To: Hon. Joseph G. Minish

Secretary to the President,

The White House.
