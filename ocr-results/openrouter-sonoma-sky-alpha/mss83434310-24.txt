Navy - Lieut. James M. Ransom

To the Honorable the Secretary of the Navy -

Lieut. James M. Ransom, late of the United States
Navy, prays that you will cause to be paid to him
the amount due him for services rendered the United
States as an officer in the Navy, during the late war
with Mexico.

He was attached to the sloop of war Dale, under
command of Commodore William Branford, and
was in the naval operations on the California coast
during the years 1846 and 1847. He was honorably
discharged from the service on the 31st day of July,
1847, at Monterey, California.

He has never received any pay for his services
and prays that the same may be allowed him.

Respectfully

James M. Ransom
Late Lieut. U.S.N.

No. 18 1848

The within petition of James M. Ransom is
respectfully referred to the Hon. Secretary of the
Navy with the request that he will cause the
petitioner to be paid the amount due him for
services as an officer in the Navy during the
late war with Mexico, if in his judgment the
same ought to be done.

J. Y. Mason
Sec. of the Navy

To the Hon. Secretary of the Navy

The petition of William H. Thomas, a private in
Captain John W. Young's Company, 1st Regiment
Tennessee Volunteers, respectfully represents
that on or about the 15th day of June 1847, whilst
in the discharge of his duty as a soldier in the
service of the United States, he received a
severe wound in the head, from the premature
discharge of a loaded musket, by which he
was disabled from further performing military
duty, and was honorably discharged from the
service on the 15th day of July 1847, at Vera
Cruz, Mexico.

Your petitioner would further represent that
by reason of said wound he has been disabled
from laboring or following any business or
calling by which he might earn a support for
himself and family, and has been ever since
said discharge dependent upon the precarious
charity of friends for a support.

Your petitioner therefore prays that your
Honorable body will take his case into
consideration and that he may be allowed such
pension or compensation as in your wisdom
may seem just and proper.

And your petitioner as in duty bound will
ever pray &c

William H. Thomas

No. 19 1848

The within petition of William H. Thomas

the petitioner to be allowed such pension
or compensation as in his judgment the
circumstances of the case may seem to
justify.
