Fort Monroe
By Special Order No. 31, [Department of]

Leave of Absence.—Captains and Lieutenants, present being
relieved from duty at this post, will report by letter to the
Commanding General of the Army of the Potomac for assignment
to duty with that army; and on being so assigned, will report
in person to the Chief of Artillery of the Army of the Potomac
for orders.

Sick Leaves.—Officers absent on sick leave will
report by letter to the Medical Director, Army of the Potomac,
the state of their health, and whether they are able to join
their commands.

Furloughs.—Officers absent on furlough will report
by letter to the Adjutant-General of the Army, Washington, D.C.

No. 6.

Leave of absence on account of disability from wounds
or sickness will be granted on the certificate of the Medical
Director of this Department, and will not be extended beyond
sixty days, unless upon the recommendation of a board of
examination, to be convened for that purpose.

Officers applying for leave of absence or furlough will
make application through their immediate commanders, who will
endorse thereon their opinion as to the necessity of the same.

Leaves of absence or furloughs will not be granted to
officers who have been absent from their commands more than
sixty days in any one year.

The foregoing regulations will be strictly enforced.
By command of Brigadier-General John A. Dix:
WM. P. WHIPPLE,
Lieutenant-Colonel and Assistant Adjutant-General.
