CHAPTER XI.

The Chapter in New York

About the two hundredth anniversary of the landing of Hendrick Hudson in New York in October, 1909, there was a great celebration of the four hundredth anniversary of the discovery of the Hudson River, which was the scene of much of the fighting in the Revolution, when the city was the seat of our government.

All the way up the valley from New York I went to witness illuminations, bonfires, fireworks, etc. bright lights in Philadelphia on the night when the first great night parade took place.

Behind that scene, over the city, hung a great searchlight, after the manner of the Capitol at Washington.

It was an unforgettable sight. I have known the city so well over a period of fifty years. The whole scene was illumed.

From Washington I went to it by large number of bands, etc.

White House in Washington, where the Pennsylvania Avenue and the Mall were thronged with people.

It was impossible to get near the President, but I saw his car pass with twenty-five or thirty girls in it, young women, who were members of the party.

It was in May of 1916 that Washington had its first large celebration, the capital of the nation.
