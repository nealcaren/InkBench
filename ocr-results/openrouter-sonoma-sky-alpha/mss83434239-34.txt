Pope's Wells 1858 on Pecos northwest of Guadalup

Beginning in spring of 1858 seventy soldiers under the direction

of Captain John Pope began drilling wells in the dry bed of Delaware Creek 15 miles northwest of the junction of the Pecos River and Delaware Creek. They used a steam

engine to carry out the operations. The third well reached a depth of 1600 ft.

but eventually all were caved in and Pope abandoned the project.
