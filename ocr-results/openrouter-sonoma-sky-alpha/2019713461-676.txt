Oct 15, 1862

United States

James Miller

In the matter of James Miller, a bankrupt.

In the Southern District of New York, ss.

On this ___ day of ________, in the year one thousand eight hundred and sixty-seven.

It appearing to the satisfaction of the Court, by the creditors' petitions and other proofs, that the said James Miller has been adjudged a bankrupt under the Act of Congress of the United States, entitled "An Act to establish a uniform system of bankruptcy throughout the United States," approved March 2, 1867, and that he has duly surrendered all his property and rights of property, and fully complied with all the requirements of said Act, and of the several rules and orders of the Court made in reference thereto, and that there are no assets to be administered for the benefit of creditors;

It is therefore ordered that the said James Miller be, and he is hereby, discharged from all debts and liabilities which he or his assignee in bankruptcy incurred or owed prior to the date of his adjudication as a bankrupt.

By the Court,

[signature line blank]

James Miller

Southern District of New York
