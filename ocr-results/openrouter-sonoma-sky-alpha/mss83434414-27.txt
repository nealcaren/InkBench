FREEDMEN'S BUREAU.

General Rufus Saxton, born in Massachusetts, and at the breaking out of the war was holding the position of military secretary on the staff of General Scott. He was soon promoted to a brigadier-generalship, and assigned to the command of the First Regiment of South Carolina Volunteers, composed entirely of freedmen. He has since been assigned to the Freedmen's Bureau in South Carolina, where he has labored with great zeal and efficiency in behalf of the freed people.

The schools established under his direction are in a very flourishing condition. Several of them have been visited by the writer, and found in excellent order, the children well clothed and happy, and making rapid progress in their studies. The number of pupils in attendance in the State of South Carolina at the end of December was 10,400, and it is increasing daily.

These women are doing much to aid in the erection of these schools in the face of great difficulties, blacks and whites. A full statement is better compiled in Harper's Weekly.
