64 The city of Havana exported about 50,000 bales of tobacco from the port

in the year 1840. The export of leaf tobacco, the principal article of export,

which finds a ready market in all parts of the world, amounts to about

50,000 bales, from 30 to 35 millions of pounds. The tobacco is brought

from the provinces of Santa Clara, Matanzas, Cardenas, and the interior of

the island. The value of the export of tobacco is very considerable, and

has a great influence on the commerce of the country, and gives employment

to a great number of persons in various ways. There are at present two

establishments in the city of Havana for the preparation of tobacco for

export, and the establishment of a third would be of great advantage to

the commerce of the island. It would probably be highly advantageous to

the trade of this country to establish direct communications with the

ports of the island. By such communications trade could be carried on

with greater facility and at less expense than at present. The following

statement shows the amount of tobacco exported from the port of Havana

for several years:
