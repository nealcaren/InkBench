Translation of J. M.'s letter December 18th, 1863

Dear Sir,

I do not reply immediately to your last because I wished to wait until I had news from our Minister in Buenos Ayres by our last mail.

The Argentine Congress is not sitting. This prevents our delegates from presenting the subject to the Congress.

Owing to this, no steps have been made to aid in bearing our expenses which the United States Government expects our Minister will defray.

The United States expects, consequently, that the prices of hotel &c. & transportation will be defrayed by us.

We shall communicate the result to you shortly, & support of the Government all possible aid which we may receive on this point is possible.

If you wish that we may treat with the Congress I beg you will inform me so & I shall do so with pleasure.

With the assurance of my best wishes
