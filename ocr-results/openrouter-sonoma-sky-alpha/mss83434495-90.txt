toward the end of the war in camp with Genl Joseph E Johnston, I was conversing with a number of officers who had been captured at the battle of Vicksburg & paroled. Among them were two or three from the 13th Mississippi Regiment. I asked them if they knew Joseph E. Johnston? They all said yes, that he was a fine officer, but not a great general. One of them said he was not competent to command an army. I told him that I thought he was a good officer, but not a great general. He said he was not a great general, but a good officer.
