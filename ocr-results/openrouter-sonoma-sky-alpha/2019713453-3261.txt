United States District Court  
Southern District of New York  

In the matter of John F. Brown  
Bankrupt.  

In Bankruptcy.  

To the Hon. R. C. Ingersoll, Judge of said Court.  

The petition and schedules of John F. Brown of the City of New York in the County of New York and State of New York within said District, who has resided, kept a store and carried on business at the City of New York in the County of New York and State of New York for the six months next immediately preceding the date of filing the said petition, duly filed in said Court on the 6th day of June A.D. 1866, praying to be adjudged a Bankrupt under the Act of Congress entitled "An Act to establish a uniform system of Bankruptcy throughout the United States" approved March 2d 1867, and the amendments thereto, and that a day may be fixed for the hearing of the same, and for the discharge of said petitioner from all his debts, is referred to the undersigned one of the Registers in Bankruptcy of said Court, and he hereby gives notice that the day of hearing is fixed on the 13th day of June A.D. 1866 at 11 o'clock in the forenoon at his office No. 66 Broadway in the City of New York.  

Dated this 6th day of June A.D. 1866.  

R.C. McAllister  
Register in Bankruptcy
