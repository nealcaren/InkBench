But let us turn to brighter subjects. Thy letter brought me much joy, and I am heartened to learn of the progress in thy recovery. The "North Easter" must have been a trial, but spring is upon us now, and with it, renewal. My own headache has passed, leaving me stronger, though I am reminded daily of the fragility of health in these trying times.

I have been much occupied with reading and correspondence. The papers are full of news from Washington—Lincoln's plans for reconstruction fill me with hope, yet I fear the divisions that remain. Hast thou heard from our cousins in the South? Their letters, when they come, speak of hardship but also of a quiet determination to rebuild.

As for Lizzie, I shall make inquiries as soon as possible through Mary and others. It is essential that we secure reliable help without delay. If Lizzie Lee is unavailable, perhaps there is another in the circle who might serve. I will write again soon with what I learn.

Pray give my warmest regards to all at home. May peace attend thee.

Thy affectionate friend,

E. P. Smith
