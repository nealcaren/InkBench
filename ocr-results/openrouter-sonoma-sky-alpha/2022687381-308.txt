Prospectus

Feb July, 185

To the Patrons of Literature,

In submitting to your consideration the accompanying work, it affords me much pleasure to state that it is the result of much labor and research, and that every exertion has been made to render it worthy of your patronage.

It is designed to give the intellectual history of the world, in which all the great names of antiquity and modern times will be included, and from which the reader will derive a knowledge of those grand truths and principles which have been evolved from the white heat of those great minds.

The greatest labors have been bestowed upon it to make it a work of real value, and to bring it within the reach of all who desire a book of this kind. I would ask of my patrons a liberal support for this work, and assure them that no labor or expense will be spared to make it a credit to the publisher and a source of instruction and pleasure to the reader.

The work will contain a library of useful knowledge in a single volume, and will be got up in the best style of the art.

The price will be five dollars for the work, bound in cloth, and will be delivered to subscribers free of postage.

Subscriptions will be received by the undersigned, and by the principal booksellers throughout the country.

John Wike, Publisher.
