Tho' all through our stay in Phila. the time grew more & more upon our minds from day to day that we must leave it, yet we were in no wise in haste to do so.

From the first of July our friends were one by one leaving the city, & we shall join them in a few days. Our own will leave us this morning for one week in Cape May. From thence they go to Atlantic City, where we hope to join them.

The 4th of July was spent quietly, with a few friends. We had a fine sermon from Rev. Dr. Furness in the morning, & in the afternoon a very interesting address from John Frost Esq. on the History of Philadelphia. It was well attended, & listened to with great attention.

Our dear friend Mary is with us, & seems much better. We hope she will be able to join us at the sea shore.

Yours affectionately,

Mary Wistar

Mr Richard Dangler  
Washington D.C.  

July 10th
