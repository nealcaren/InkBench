Born in Fort Scott, Kansas, Gordon Parks received his first camera in 1941 while working as a waiter on the Northern Pacific Railway. In 1942, he was awarded a Rosenwald Fellowship in photography, and in 1948 he became a staff photographer for Life magazine. He has published articles in a variety of magazines, including Ebony, Look, and Vogue. In 1961, he became a staff writer for Life, where he wrote the novel The Learning Tree, which was published in 1963. He has also written books of poetry, including A Poet and His Camera (1968) and Moments Without Proper Names (1975). In 1970, he directed the film version of The Learning Tree, and in 1971 he directed Shaft, which was a major success. He has also directed Leadbelly (1976) and Shaft's Big Score (1972). In addition to his work in film and photography, Parks has been active in music, composing a ballet, Martin, which was performed by the New York City Ballet in 1965. He has received numerous awards and honors, including the NAACP Image Award in 1970.

Parks has three children, and one grandchild.
