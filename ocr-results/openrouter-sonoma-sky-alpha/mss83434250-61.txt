Upon the high seas, (the high seas being a

part of the territory of all nations,) a right

is inferred, it is necessary that, before some prize

can be captured, the vessel must be on the high

seas. The property captured or found there

must be the property of the enemy. The decision

of the prize court, according to the law of nations, is

final, unless an appeal is taken to the sovereign

or his council. No decision of any court in this

country can by capture or any other means

divest the property of an enemy and vest it in a

citizen of the United States.

Some persons have urged, as an argument

in favor of this clause or section of the Constitution,

that it only mentions or enumerates

properties, impeachments, and arrests, and that the

power to issue writs of habeas corpus, which the

clause contains, and suspending which the clause

alludes to, is not one of the powers which the

Constitution confers upon Congress.

*And this view of the subject is, in my opinion,

correct.
