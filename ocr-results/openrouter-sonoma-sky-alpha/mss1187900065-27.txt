The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals  
Mansion House, 4th Avenue, cor. 27th St.  

New York January 30th 185  

To The Police Judge of Audrain County, Mo.  

Sir,  

Mrs. Olivia H. Dimmitt, late of this city, died on the 24th inst., leaving a will, which was proved & recorded in the Surrogate's office, in this city, on the 28th ult.  The said will directs that her estate shall be divided into two equal parts, one of which is given to her son, John Dimmitt, and the other to her daughter, Eliza D. Walton.  The testatrix, in her said will, directs that her property in Missouri, if any she has, shall be sold, & the proceeds divided between her said son & daughter.  Her executor has received information that she died possessed of a tract of land in the county of Audrain, containing about 100 acres, on which there is a mill.  The executor is desirous that the property should be sold, & claims the property as the property of the estate.  

Very respectfully,  

Pierre Berthoud,  
President.  

1894
