JOHNSON'S UNIV. CYC. VOL. III

Jews, the name given to the posterity of
in Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, or Israel. The
word is used in a collective sense for the
whole nation, and in a particular sense for
the tribe of Judah. The term Hebrew is
supposed to be derived from the name of
Eber, one of the ancestors of Abraham, or
from the word 'ibri, which means one who
crosses over, in allusion to the passage of
the Euphrates by Abraham. The Jews were
originally a nomadic tribe in the land of
Canaan, but under the leadership of Moses
they conquered and occupied the country.
They established a kingdom under Saul,
David, and Solomon, which was divided after
the death of Solomon into the kingdoms of
Israel and Judah. The kingdom of Israel
was conquered by the Assyrians in 722 B.C.,
and the kingdom of Judah by the Babylonians
in 586 B.C. The Jews were carried into
captivity, but many of them returned to
their own country under Cyrus the Persian,
who conquered Babylon in 538 B.C. They
rebuilt the temple and restored the worship
of Jehovah. The country was afterward
governed by high priests, and then by
the Asmonean princes, who were of the
Maccabean family. In 63 B.C. the country
was conquered by Pompey, and became a
province of Rome. The Jews revolted against
the Romans in 66 A.D., but were defeated
and the city of Jerusalem was destroyed in
70 A.D. Many of the Jews were killed or
sold into slavery, and the rest were scattered
over the world. This dispersion is called
the Diaspora. The Jews have preserved
their religion and their national charac-
teristics amid all the persecutions and
oppressions to which they have been sub-
jected. They have been accused of many
crimes, but have always maintained their
innocence. The most remarkable feature
of the Jewish character is their attach-
ment to their religion and their law. The
Jews are now scattered over the whole
world, and are found in every country.
They are engaged in commerce, banking,
and the professions. They have produced
many distinguished men in literature,
science, and art. The number of Jews in
the world is about six millions.
