Legislature of Pennsylvania.

File of the House of Representatives.

No. 104. In Senate, March 6, 1911.

Referred to Judiciary General Committee

A JOINT RESOLUTION

Proposing an amendment to section two, article eight of the Constitution

of Pennsylvania, so as to give the right of suffrage to women.

Section 1. Be it resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives

of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania in General Assembly met, That the following article is proposed as an amendment to the Constitution of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, in accordance with the eighteenth article thereof:—

That section two of article eight, which reads as follows: — "Laws shall be made to exclude from office, and from suffrage, those who shall have been convicted of infamous crimes, but the elective franchise shall not be taken away from any citizen, except as hereinafter provided in this article, unless the General Assembly shall provide by law for the loss of the right of suffrage by those who shall be convicted of infamous crimes, and for the restoration of such right by a general or special law," be amended, by striking out the whole thereof, and inserting in lieu thereof the following:

"Every citizen twenty-one years of age, possessing the following qualifications, shall be entitled to vote at all elections: he shall have been a citizen of the United States at least thirty days; he shall have resided in the State one year (or, having previously been a qualified elector or native born citizen of the State, he shall have removed therefrom and returned, then six months immediately preceding the election), in the election district at least thirty days; if twenty-two years of age or upwards, he shall have paid within two years a State or county tax, which shall have been assessed at least two months and paid at least one month before the election; but a citizen of the United States who had previously been a qualified voter of this State, and removed therefrom and returned, and who shall have resided in the election district and paid taxes as aforesaid, shall be entitled to vote after residing in the district six months: Provided, That all citizens residing in any election district adjacent to another State whose boundary line is not defined by a natural stream or definite geographical boundary, and who have resided therein one year and paid taxes as aforesaid, shall be entitled to vote at the next ensuing election after the passage of this amendment, and at all succeeding elections, in such election district, if otherwise qualified according to law. But this provision shall not be so construed as to prevent the General Assembly from providing for the registration of electors.

No person who shall be convicted of treason, felony or bribery in any State, shall be qualified to be an elector unless convicted before an election previously to which the person shall have been fully restored to citizenship and qualified to exercise the right of suffrage under the constitution and laws of the State where the crime was committed.

Section 2. That any and all such amendment as is herein proposed, shall be submitted to the qualified electors of the Commonwealth, for their approval or rejection, at an election to be held on the Tuesday next following the first Monday of November in the year one thousand nine hundred and twelve; and the Secretary of the Commonwealth is directed to cause the said amendment to be published three months prior to said election, in at least two newspapers in every county in which such newspapers shall be published."
