During life of the person attainted.[1]

By this article, the treason has in express
terms been defined; and they declare and not limited. The punish-
ment of treason is expressly limited by the Constitution
itself, to death, or fine, or imprisonment, or disability to hold office, or
some or all of them; it can not be extended to involve the
family of the traitor, or his property beyond his life. The
punishment of treason, under the law of England,
has been unlimited in its effects and operations;
the attainder of treason has worked a corruption of blood, and a
forfeiture of all the estate of the attainted person, and of his
family, and has been perpetual in its effects. The
species of attainder which is not limited, the effect and opera-
tions of which are not confined to the life of the person attainted,
is not known to the Constitution of the United States. It
is not, and can not be exercised.[2]
