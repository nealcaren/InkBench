Apr. 1931  
Woman Citizen  

"Party Clean-ups Are the Crying Need"  

Says  
Carrie Chapman Catt  
Distinguished Suffrage Leader  

Mrs. Catt  

WE have in the past been inclined to overlook the fact that public men have been the greatest offenders in this respect. The women who have entered politics since suffrage came have been guilty of the same thing. They have been inclined to overlook the fact that they are women first and politicians afterward. They have been inclined to think that because they are in politics they must act like men. They have been inclined to forget that they are women and to act like men.  

The result has been that the women in politics have not been as good politicians as they might have been if they had remembered that they are women. The women in politics have been inclined to think that they must do the things that men do in politics, and they have not done the things that women can do better than men. Since suffrage your sex has been inclined to imitate the rough and tough methods of men, but the time has come to strike out on a new path for women in politics.  

The crying need today is for women to clean up the political parties from top to bottom. They must infuse the parties with the clean, honest, public-spirited spirit of womanhood. They must make the parties over in the image of women rather than men. The women must take the lead in this clean-up, using their influence in the parties, their votes at the polls, and their voices in public to accomplish it. They must organize for this purpose through existing and new women's organizations. The goal must be parties that are responsive to the needs of the people, instruments of good government, and expressions of the public will rather than tools of bosses, machines, or special interests.  

This is the task before the women of America. Let them go at it with all the energy and enthusiasm at their command. The parties must be cleaned up through the familiar channels of political action, or they will lose the confidence of the people forever.
