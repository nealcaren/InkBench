CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED STATES

against the people in the government holding public office;
plotting treason, forming and passing ordinances of secession
and the making of treaties between states, and the several
States; refusing to take the oath of allegiance to the
United States; to proceed to the trial of all officers of
the United States, when such officers may be impeached, or
to the trial of all civil officers of the United States,
when impeached by the House of Representatives for high
crimes and misdemeanors in office; but no person shall be
held to answer for any treasonable act, or for any act of
aiding or abetting treason, unless indicted by a grand
jury, or unless the act was committed in the presence of
the court, or the jury; and no person shall be twice put
in jeopardy of life or limb for the same offence, nor shall
be compelled in any criminal case to be a witness against
himself, nor be deprived of life, liberty, or property,
without due process of law; nor shall private property be
taken for public use, without just compensation.

All attempts to overthrow the government should be punished.

Impeachment of the most atrocious murders which are punishable
by law, are by death, but the punishment of all which do
not deserve death, is left to the discretion of the court,
by which the trial is had.
