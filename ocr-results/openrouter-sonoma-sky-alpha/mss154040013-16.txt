In & Around Canton

27

The itinerant barber is ready to do any amount of shaving and haircutting in the open streets. Each of these quick barbers has his own shop, consisting of a chair, a basin, and a few razors and scissors. The itinerant doctors, with remedies for every "ill flesh is heir to," salves, ointments, plasters for cuts, bruises, bones, skins, reptiles, slings, etc., without any crowd from hearing buckets of dirty water.

The sanitary arrangements of this city are primitive as compared with foreign settlements.

The streets are wholly devoid of sewers. There are wood carvers. There are many long rows of all sorts.

Entering a district where silk is manufactured. Farther we pass through a street where [it is] a manufactured [article]. Farther we pass through a [street which is one of the] chief articles of trade. Passing these long [rows] in the next [street we see] shops [filled] with silk stores, marble shops, book stores.

Here a street containing only medicine and drug [shops].

[There are] Japanese made goods. Leaving [these] medicine [shops] we pass in the street for [perfect order]. Here are sights in the city, and shops [full] of dollars and subsidiary coins, some tying the money up.
