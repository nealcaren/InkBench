REMARKS OF MR. WM. B. KELLY, OF PENNSYLVANIA,
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, FEBRUARY 24, 1863.

Mr. SPEAKER: The resolution now pending before the House is one of the most important bills
ever introduced into this body. The gentleman from Kentucky, (Mr. ROEBUCK,) in presenting it, has
said much that was well said. He has used his best efforts to induce his friends on this floor to
vote for it. The gentleman from Virginia, (Mr. SEGAR,) who has just taken his seat, has also
addressed the House in its favor. They have gone so far as to say that if this bill does not pass
it will be because their friends from the North are opposed to it. All right, gentlemen; we are
opposed to it. We are opposed to it because we believe it to be wrong in principle and
injurious in its consequences. We are opposed to it because it is a proposition to abandon the
war and to make peace with the rebels. It is a proposition to recognize the independence of the
so-called Confederate States, and to treat with them as a nation. It is a proposition to dissolve
the Union, and to establish two nations in its place. It is a proposition to abandon the
Constitution and the laws, and to set up a new government in their stead. It is a proposition
to surrender the rights of the loyal people of the United States, and to deliver them over to
the tender mercies of traitors. It is a proposition to betray the trust reposed in us by our
constituents, and to prove false to the oaths we have taken to support the Constitution. It
is a proposition to desert our flag, and to haul down the stars and stripes, and to raise in
their place the rebel banner. It is a proposition to abandon our soldiers in the field, and to
leave them to the mercy of their enemies. It is a proposition to sacrifice the honor of the
nation, and to cover it with everlasting infamy. It is a proposition to make the President of
the United States a traitor, and to make his Cabinet accomplices in treason. It is a
proposition to make the Congress of the United States a band of traitors, and to make every
member of it a participant in the crime. It is a proposition to make the people of the loyal
States slaves to the rebels, and to subject them to the domination of traitors. It is a
proposition to destroy the Government, and to build up a new one on its ruins. It is a
proposition to revolutionize the social and political institutions of the country, and to
introduce anarchy and confusion in their place. It is a proposition to abandon all that is
dear to the patriot and the Christian, and to give up the country to the dominion of the
devil. It is a proposition to make the name of America a by-word and a hissing among the
nations of the earth. It is a proposition to blot out the memory of Washington and his
compeers, and to hand down to posterity the names of the traitors who have destroyed the
Union. It is a proposition to make the history of the United States a history of shame and
disgrace, and to write it in letters of blood on the pages of time. It is a proposition to
abandon the cause of freedom and humanity, and to ally ourselves with the despots and
tyrants of the Old World. It is a proposition to make the American people the laughing-stock
of the world, and to cover them with confusion and ignominy. It is a proposition to destroy
the hopes of the friends of liberty throughout the world, and to extinguish the light of
freedom in this land.
