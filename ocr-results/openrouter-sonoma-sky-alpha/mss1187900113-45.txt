truly sorry to hear that thee
hast been so unwell as to
confine thee to thy chamber
since thy last letter to us. We
have been quite anxious about
thee, & hope thy confinement has
not been occasioned by any thing
serious. Thy last letter was
received on 5th day evening, and
we were truly glad to hear of
thy arrival in Philada. & that
thou hadst seen our dear
Sally. I have been anxious to
hear from her, & hope she
has been able to attend to
her business. We have had
a letter from her since thee
left home, & she mentioned
having seen thee, but said
nothing about thy health. We
are truly sorry to hear of
thy indisposition, & hope thee
will soon be able to resume
thy journey. When thee gets
to N. York, I hope thee will
see our friends there, & give
our love to them. We were
sorry to hear of the death
of our friend A. Walton, but
hope it was not sudden. Thy
letter to Sally was received
on 6th day, & we were glad
to hear from her. We have
had a letter from her since
thee left, & she seems to be
quite well. We are all well
here, & hope this will find
thee the same. Thy affectionate
friend, Sarah H. Walton
