In March,  1855, Frederick   Douglass delivered an oddress to the New York State Legislature. An eyewitness (William H. of Albany) describes the crowded audience, and their rapt attention to the speaker, the grandest scene he ever witnessed in the Capitol. Among those whose eyes were riveted on the speaker full two hours and half were Thurlow Weed and Lieutenant   Governor Raymond; the latter, at the conclusion of the address, exclaimed to 0 friend "1 would give twenty thousand dollars, if could deliver that address in that manner.
