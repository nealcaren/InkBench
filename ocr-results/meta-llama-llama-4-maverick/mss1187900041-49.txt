Washington D.C.
April 18th, 1869

Dear Mr. [Pleasants]

Your reply to my letter was
received yesterday.
I saw what you said in your letter to
Lewis Edwards that you supposed I was
discharged for neglect of duty, or some other
such cause.  But such was not the case.
Genl. Howard did not know at first that I
was among the number, and he wrote to me as I afterwards told 
been a member of the Law Class who
have been relieved.  The following is the
truth;

Mr. Langston has taken charge of the
Law Department of Howard University
and has about twenty colored students-
