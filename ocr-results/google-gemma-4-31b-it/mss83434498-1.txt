SPANISH PORT ALABAMA, APRIL 1865; 4 pgs. In pencil, with overly C. Bedout
New Orleans, La., stamped 3 third cls docket no cover, "Soldiers Letter, Age
signed by Wm Avery, Lt. Col. 95th Ill. Infy." written by Andrew Fern to
indirect to m. e. v. a. d. 1865.  Is she wrote to FERNIZ
We first invested the fort 27th last month, and have been here ever since.
We have out late some parties’ to find out where’ll find the regt
There is another fort 6 miles above here called FORT BLEAKLY; that is invested
by govt. with 1100 men.  About 1000 more are pending on the river; therefore they came out last night and made three charges on our
regiment on the river. Our rifle pits are within 15 rods of the Rebel works.
We are now building a bridge, as we are trying to get on a firmer
communication with MOBILE. Much more excellent content, talks about how they
are continuing to build the bridge, and the padding of the fortifications that they
planting some big 100 lb perrot guns; the Rebs have torpedoes very thick
in the channel where the river bends; but the government has placed some
the ground some torpedoes which have killed quite a number of their men who
have walked on them (for the Rebs have no torpedoes) but has also very
much bold hand, nice content concerning BLACK TROOPS and the TORPEDOES
