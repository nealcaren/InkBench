**The Light in the Tunnel.**
*From the Virginia City Chronicle.*
A new device for the bedevilment of lovers was introduced successfully by a heartless young man on the train yesterday. A couple sat in the seat before him. The lady was young and modest, and the swain wore very large cuffs. One arm was thrown carelessly along the back of the seat, and upon this the evil-minded person behind rubbed some phosphorus. The affectionate performance of that arm, when the train was rushing through the kindly darkness of the tunnels, were beheld by all the giggling and snickering occupants of the car, whose cruel enjoyment was not a little increased by the demure and unconcerned appearance of the lovers each time the train bounded again into light.

—The wife of Thomas H. Banner of Hagerstown had been warned before she married him that his temper was violent, and that, sooner or later, he would whip her. She seems to have made up her mind what to do if he struck her, for when he did so, she drew a revolver from her pocket and shot him dead.
