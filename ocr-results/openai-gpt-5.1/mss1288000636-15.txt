Tuning In On Moscow  

Two western Germans were sentenced to death because they were listening to a radio tuned in on Moscow, two others had their radio confiscated and are being sent to three years for the additional offense of discussing politics while the broadcast was going on.  

To most people who have ever tuned in the “pick‑up” Moscow they would think it a high price to pay for either instruction or entertainment offered. But the same would be true of broadcasts from Berlin or Rome whenever these feed out Mozart and Verdi for the arts with slabs of propaganda.

It would save all the dictators much trouble if they let the broadcasts cancel out. PATRIOTISM  

To the Editor of The Republican:  

Flag waving and lip service to the Constitution are now being resorted to by Veterans of Foreign Wars and other veterans’ organizations lately who have alleged as a basic principle to encourage respect for the law by demanding it be obeyed.  

It is highly ironical that in our own home town, as in many others, for the past few years many of our so‑called law‑abiding citizens have been busy violating the very law they now pretend to support.  

Boston, October 31, 1935. M. G. M.

In the rough marble beauty hides unseen.  

To wake the music and the beauty needs  
The master’s touch,—the sculptor’s chisel keen.

Let not the music that is in us die!

Hidden and lost, thy form within us lie!  

Spare not the stroke! Do with us what thou wilt!  
Let there be nought unfinished, broken, marred;  
Complete thy purpose, that we may become  
Thy perfect image, O our God and Lord.
