MEMORIAL SET UP  
TO DR. EMILY LEVI  

Friends of the late Dr. Emily  
Levi presented a check for $14,000  
yesterday to the trustees of the  
New York Infirmary to start an  
endowment fund in her memory.  
Income will be used for the care of  
ailing women physicians connected  
with the infirmary and their chil-  
dren.  

The check was presented to Mrs.  
Frank A. Vanderlip, president of  
the board of trustees, by Miss  
Marion Manner, medical director of  
the infirmary, at its thirty-  
second meeting at the Infirmary,  
321 East Fifteenth St.,                   Ave-  
nue.  

Mrs. Vanderlip said of Dr. Levi:  
"She was one of the older doctors  
whose courage, because of their con-  
victions and the sacrifices they un-  
derwent in their early careers, en-  
titled them to our highest apprecia-  
tion. Without her service as a  
doctor I do not know how we could  
have carried the hospital through  
all our long service since its founda-  
tion."  

Dr. Levi, who died two years  
ago, was attending physician at the  
infirmary for about forty years and  
served as director and vice-presi-  
dent, and for three years as presi-  
dent. Born in Philadelphia, she re-  
ceived her medical degree from  
Woman's Medical College in that  
city. She was one of the women  
members of the faculty and endow-  
ed one of the wards.  

The floors of the projected new  
infirmary building will be known  
as the Emily S. Levi Memorial  
Home. A ward is endowed, and the  
endowment will support it.
