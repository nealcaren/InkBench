Buc., 7 Sept. 1922

Dear Mrs Chapman Catt,

We had in our program of this year, to read lectures on famous women. Mr Percy Juncker has filled it. She is in the L. D. F. and gives popular evening courses. But as there are no complete libraries in Roumania and not a single feminist library, it is impossible to secure the books necessary for propaganda. We address therefore to you as to the other sections of I. W. S. A. to beg your help.

Feeling so much the loss of a free library we did try, at one time with our studies, to found one at B. and perhaps than will we be able to assemble round it the women in a club, as we are dreaming of since a long time. Would your association be able for the generous act of more help unto sisters and to send us one or the other standard works on women movement in your country and especially those, where we could find biographies of your famous women. We would open in a first serie of lectures, on political and social movement of women and in a second one the portraits of women in art and science. But the honor so little, it is by not much
