1862 June 17th Evacuation of Cumberland Gap, Tenn. The federals  
under Genl Morgan advanced toward this place for the purpose  
of expelling the rebels Genls Stevenson & Barton who occupied  
the gap with 12000 men and fortifications of the most formid-  
able character. This gap is a split in the Cumberland mountain  
and narrow that there is through the gap barely a single  
roadway. It was expected that the rebels would defend  
its possession with the utmost tenacity. The result however  
followed. When the rebels were informed of the approach  
of the federals, they precipitately evacuated the place,
