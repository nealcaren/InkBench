The Federals were commanded by Genls Curtis & Sigel. This battle  
was fought for three days without any decisive result, but on the  
8th Genl Curtis made additional charges, and early of morning  
he advanced along his whole line when the rebels were instantly  
thrown into confusion and fled precipitately on all the  
attack. The division of Gen. Davis attacked in one direction,  
that of Price escaped by another. The pursuit was continued  
by Genl Sigel toward Ritcherville, and by the Cavalry to  
the mountains, the result of the victory was complete, of rebels being  
3000 killed and wounded also 2000 prisoners, among the latter  
were two generals McC[????], a peculiar feature in  
this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians under  
the command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were demonstrated  
after battle the federal dead and wounded were  
found to be scalped, thus rendering this horrid scene of  
sanguinary slaughter, Union loss 300 killed, and 900 wounded.  

1862  
March 11th Genl McClellan assumes command of the Army of the  
Potomac in the field. --- Genl Fremont assumes command of the  
Mountain Department. --- Genl Halleck assumes command of the  
Department of the Mississippi --- Pensacola occupied by the Federals.
