My dear Miss Dickinson  

That you have last since been offensively inconvenienced has been has been such a cause of such regret, you can but credit that the impropriety was far not at all on me. My position in regard to reference to the president’s chair is as well known as that I almost any other person in the city, and I can but state (as it is now) in a past mistake.  

By this fact you proved that they should have urged upon me in any measure to sanction a lecture the substance of which was to be in direct opposition to my own prohibited views.
