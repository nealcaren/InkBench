London Sunday May 6, '94

Dear M. Douglas,

Your letter directed Manchester has followed me around till it reached me here. I am indeed glad to get it. Then I wrote you I had not seen your letter to the Society. Since seeing it I know from what he and others said, that while they did not expect "much" (may they pay you the same compliment) I do, knowing you, still they would have been better satisfied if you had spoken more positively regarding me and my work. M. Aldought that since the Society had failed so miserably and I was thus left to fend for myself, you would not hesitate to vouch for me.
