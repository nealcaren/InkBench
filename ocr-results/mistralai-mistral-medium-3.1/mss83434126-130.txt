1861 Aug 6th. Congress closed its extra session. Congress adjourned.

1861 Aug 7th. The town of Hampton, Va. destroyed by the rebels.
The steamer York, burned by the U.S. Gunboat Union, the crew taken prisoners.

1861 Aug 10th. Battle of Wilson's Creek Mo. The federals under the command Gen'l Lyon, made a planned attack on the rebels and them from their encampment and continued the fight for six hours hard fighting and in leading a bayonet charge Gen'l Lyon was killed instantly, Col Siegel carried out Lyon's plan, and retired in an orderly manner to Springfield, having captured 400 horses, and 700 prisoners. Union loss 200 killed 700 wounded. Rebel loss is killed and wounded was much greater. The brave Gen'l Lyon the hero of the war gave his honor, his fortune ($20,000) and his life, to his country that his country might live. Number of federal troops engaged was 5000. Number of rebel troops engaged was 9000.

1861 Aug 12th. President Lincoln issues a proclamation appointing the 30th day of September as a day of fasting and prayer.

1861 Aug 14th. Major Gen'l John C. Fremont declares and places the city of St. Louis under Martial Law.

1861 Aug 16th. Major Gen'l John C. Fremont arrives in command of Western Missouri and its military surroundings.
