1783-1815:
THE WAR OF 1812

The Treaty of Paris did not eradicate the causes of the Revolutionary War, for the new Republic was still embroiled in economic, political, and territorial difficulties with England and Spain. Portions of the territory ceded to the United States were still occupied by British forces, who remained as late as 1796, and British agents within U.S. continued to foment trouble among the Indians within U.S. territory.
