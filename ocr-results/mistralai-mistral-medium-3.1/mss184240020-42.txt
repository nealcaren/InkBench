1710 Locust St. Philadelphia
March 21, 1871

My dear sister,

I write, first of all, about Edwinus, because after a long
talk we held about himself last night, that takes the first place.
I read thy little note to him from Friday evening,
which, while there hopes to find him better, they say "I fear me."
There has been nothing startlingly new with him, only
there seems to be less of him from week to week, and his energy and ability to
go or do anything grow less continually.
Last night, after I had gone to bed,
he spoke for the first time. He says
he finds his digestion rather better, but
his stamina lessened so that he can
no longer use most of the remedies,
