Washington D.C.
July 18, 1883.

Dear Grandpa;

Perceived your affectionate letter on the 12th, and we were very glad to hear from you, and to know that you are getting along nicely. Everything is passing along smoothly here; excepting the weather, which is at times almost intolerable. It is now about nine o'clock and the thermometer stands about 84 1/2. Charlie has not left yet for his carrying lessons and I doubt whether he will or not. I wrote the latter as I thought near sunday any
