Jan. 27 1874.

Washington D.C.

My Dear father,

   I have received your kind letter and was very glad to hear from you, but I am so sorry that you are still so a dreadful going to Rochester.[sic]. I hope it will be all right their,[sic] when you go their.[sic] I  Shall take good care of our letters and not think of destroying them in anyway we have had a few very cold days but we have very pleasant weather.

J. P. H.
