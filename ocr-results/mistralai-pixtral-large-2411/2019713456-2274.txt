Oliver Optic's Magazine.
1869.
Third Year! Fifth Volume!

For immense popularity which the only Juvenile Magazine published

ONCE A WEEK

has attained, assures the publishers that

OUR BOYS AND GIRLS,

welcomed as an experiment, was needed by the young people, and is indorsed by their parents and guardians, and have declared that it shall not only contain the productions of the

BEST AMERICAN WRITERS,

who have distinguished themselves in the department of juvenile literature, but also that it shall be of the

HIGHEST MORAL TONE.

The publishers of Oliver Optic's Magazine encouraged by the success of the past, will endeavor by the coming volume to meet the requirements of their young patrons ever better than anything on their Trade List more than

TWO HUNDRED JUVENILE BOOKS,

written by the most popular authors, endeavor to excell and furnish to all the

CHEAPEST AND THE BEST MAGAZINE

for the young people in the United States. Their aim will still be to

IMPROVE THE MIND AND HEART,

and while the work shall amuse and entertain, it shall also impart valuable information

ART, SCIENCE AND LITERATURE,

and develop and encourage the purest morality. The publication will continue to be under the editorial supervision of Oliver Optic, whose books for young people have now reached the unrivaled annual sale of

ONE HUNDRED AND FIFTY THOUSAND VOLUMES.

His works are in every family all over the land, and are largely relied upon by parents and guardians, as moral educational agencies in the training of their children. This popular author, while he writes

AN EXCITING STORY,

which shall afford young readers no excuse for resorting to the dangerous trash so abundant at the present time, endows his heroes and heroines with pure and noble characters, worthy the imitation of youth, and depicts like his beautiful sketches aglow life and heart. No young person reading his works the better, and no young person ever to purchase. Oliver Optic writes for no juvenile publication, except Our Boys and Girls, and all his books are issued by the publishers. During the coming year, Mr. Weld will continue the

LAKE SHORE SERIES,

Being Stories of which will appear in this volume, and which, when published in book form, will cost five dollars, will be entitled

LIGHTHING EXPRESS; or, The Rival Academies.
ON TIME; or, The Young Captain of the Ucayga Steamer.
SWITCH OFF; or, The War of the Students.
BRAKE UP; or, The Young Peacemakers.

The Issues of these stories in this magazine afford an opportunity to the young people of the United States which can be presented by no other juvenile publication.

(Concluded on Third page of cover.)
