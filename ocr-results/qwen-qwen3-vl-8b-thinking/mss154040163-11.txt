July 15, 1907.  

My dear Steady; I have to thank you for his letters and the notes and clippings concerning Mrs. Bliss. I thank you especially for the clippings for as you suspect, I am interested in her outgoing. Last year, in Buffalo, at the National Convention, (Dutch), Mrs. Stead brought a letter she had received from Effie Vance, and she had described very vividly how Mrs. Bliss had been able to rise above her suffering thro' Christmas Sececer. Seele's tears flowed as she read it, and she was no great admirer of Mrs. B. I tell him she told me that E Vance was also getting interested in Ch. Sei. Mrs. B. was one of the most generous and forgiving natures ever met, and while she also had her weaknesses, she was of character, as well as the rest of us, she was a woman to be admired for very many estimable qualities.
