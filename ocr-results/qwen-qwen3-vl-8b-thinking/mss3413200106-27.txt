123 E. 72 St.,  
N. Y. City;  
26 June, 1882.  

Dear Miss Blackwell,  

I am blessed with a propensity to be always at something. You are something; still you will not draw an illogical conclusion, as three successive spitters might tempt a less reasonable creature to do.  

The trick is, it's hot; hot beyond creed, to say nothing of death. What resource else was left but you. The other evening, 26 or 20 was spoken of to me as having done this and that as nurse in the war. I was called on to admire her. I had to say, 'yes, I admire what she did,' But must she not be a noble woman to do so much. When I said, 'it's very well for we, you and she & me, to some good.' No, the only recommendation we can have, But really I look to see one who deed no creed to recommends him, seeing whom one thinks not how much he has done or can do, but how much he is.  

I perceived I was talking Greek, & can't go further; but you who understand Greek, is this really so unintelligible?
