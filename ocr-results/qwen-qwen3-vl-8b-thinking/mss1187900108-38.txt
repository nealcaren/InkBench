So though relieved from office you are working away with all your old power. I hope that you are laboring upon the Writings in good health, that you will not run any risks by travelling or lecturing till summer comes again. I think you were in some anxiety

about one of your boys, who was ill, hope that he has by now recovered that all your family are well. I hoped that the book of his Carpenters' sermons was a few other writings, which my nephew Prof. E. Carpenter of Oxford is editing, would have been out by his time, but it will be days or later before it appears; it is entitled "Personal and Social Christianity" & is prepared by a short memoir by Dr. H. H. Cooke. I have been about six weeks for a few weeks, to my sister's on Palak's. I paid it there to leave home, - but any friends I made with you, etc. I'll write him well. I do so.
