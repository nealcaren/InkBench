1917

My dear Lizzie: I cannot think I could possibly convey to you the very great pleasure and satisfaction your bregy graphic letter gave me. I wanted to tell you that you told me, and you told it clearly that it was like a conversation with you.

The Christmas card came and was hanging up on the foot of my bed for a day. They told me that travellers in the tropics must beware of diarrhoea but I thought myself quite immune but I had not had such a thing as I had not had such a thing for thirty years, but I got one nevertheless and was in bed a week.
