J. J. WALKER  
C. E. MATTHEWS  

STATE OF WASHINGTON.  

Franklin, Wash., 1610, 5th Av., Spokane, Washington,  
Aug. 9, 1892.  

Dear Douglas:  

Here I am, away up in the  
North West, on my first visit to my youngest  
daughter, Edella, the wife of R. B. Scott, who  
happens to be absent, just now, in discharge  
of his official duties, at "Sooyoo Lake," in the  
"Okanagan Country," as they Call it here. For  
a year and a half, until two week ago, was almost  
a perfect blank in my life's journey.  
To be blind, is a terrible calamity to any human  
being. Since the surgical operation, two months  
ago, which extrated the Cataract from my  
left eye, and then waiting for the presence and  
Sensitiveness, to light, to subside, two week ago  
I got my new spectacles with a special glass, fitted  
to my left eye, and now, with the aid of that  
glass, I can see almost as well as ever. So much  
for Science and for Providence, has Man discovery  
and skill for my holding out so long. Nature has
