Repeated dashes and furious struggles took place to carry the works by storm, but often on the verge of success, the federals were as often dashed and driven back by the immoderate bravery of the enemy, the federals were eventually (provisionally) repulsed with heavy losses. 1862 June 14th. The evacuation of Cumberland Gap, Tenn. The federals, under Genl Morgan, advanced towards this place for the purpose of expelling the rebel Genl Stevenson & British who occupied the gap with 13,000 men and well fortified the same. This gap is a shift in the Cumberland mountains, and is so deep and narrow that there is through the gap for only a single roadway. It was expected that the rebels would defend its possession with the utmost tenacity. No such result followed. When the rebels were informed of the approach of the federals, they precipitately evacuated the place,

1862 June 19th. The federals advanced from gunboats under the command of Col Fitch to convoy supplies from Memphis to Genl Custis. They proceeded up the White River, which many people have seen as the most celebrated in the war, and encountered the enemy at a place called St. Charles, which the enemy had erected.
