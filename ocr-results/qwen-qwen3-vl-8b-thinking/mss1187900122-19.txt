London Sunday May 6, 94  
Dear Mr. Douglass;  

your letter directed that — Chester had followed me all mud till it matched me here. I am indeed glad by it. Then I wrote down I had not seen your letter 19th. Liked; since seeing it I know from what he add others said, that while they did not expect "quah" (may the fear you the same compliment I do, in knowing you are incapable of such a thing) still I would have been better satisfied if you had spoken more positively regarding me and my work. M. Whell thought that since the Society had failed so miserably and I was much left to myself, you would not hesitate to vouch for me. I cannot go into the country telling people about this personal glaner and why I am unaccompanied of the persons who brought me over. At the death knew member of the race, a positive letter or auch. 168
