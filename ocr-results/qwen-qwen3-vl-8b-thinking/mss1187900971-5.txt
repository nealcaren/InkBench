Grandpa was never called "Lloyd." He was first Frederick Bailey. The slave Changed to Johnson when he ran away and because the man in whose home he lived in New Bedford Mass. was named Johnson, this man, who had just finished reading "The Lady of the Lake" admired so much the character in the book named "Douglass" asked Frederick — to keep from conflicting their two Johnson names, would like to call himself Douglass instead of Johnson. Grandpa told him one name was as good as another and henceforth set out to be called Frederick Douglass. These are facts — not hearsay.

Later — just read letter from Fred — he leaves on his "debating tour" today and really debates at Harvard. We will stop with Oral. Have already met Joe's wife & Marnie and meet Clara.
