RUTH MCCORMICK, DENEEN SOUND CAMPAIGN CRIES

Senator Attacks County Administration.

BY PARKE BROWN.

Both United States Senator Denen and Representative Ruth Hanna McCormick have announced their candidacy against him, manifesting today what was constructed as a desire to see the actual campaign start—although each has chosen the first week in January for the opening of real hostilities. Senator Denen addressed more than 100 of his followers—Chicago ward committeemen, county officers—at a luncheon at the Hotel Morrison. It was a closed meeting, but the senator made public part of his speech, a criticism of the condition existing in the local governments. Mrs. McCormick, just before leaving for Washington, issued a statement charging that a 'propaganda factory' is circulating false news that she is not only does she intend to remain in the race, but also expects to win the nomination. 'Since the election of 1928, ' read Senator Denen's manuscript, 'every week has revealed in some measure the character of the misdeeds, nonfeasance, or malfeasance by certain public officials in this county. 'The people have been deceived, hoodwinked, and in part have permitted the vote to be influenced by a few in power, who have not been interested in the real progress of the city. 'I cannot see a good reason for a man who has the opportunity to see it, and yet does not see it, to remain in public office.'
