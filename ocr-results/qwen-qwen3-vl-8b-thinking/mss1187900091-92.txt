THE REPUBLICAN LEAGUE,  
NEW HAVEN, CONN.  

September 28, 1890.  

My dear Douglass:  

On my passage thro' New York yesterday from Phila. there I spoke on Friday morning, I called at Kummerhardt & Co's office for news about the sailing of our steamer. And this is what the clerks in the office assured me of:  

The steamer is the "Oranje Nassau". She will sail from Piermont Stores (along side of the Wall street ferry), Brooklyn, on Friday next, 4th instant, at noon, for Port au Prince.  

I presume that we can count on this information as entirely reliable.  

Have always understood that the "Oranje Nassau" is the poorest steamer of the line. But she is staunch and sound as a junco we can stand it for six days on board.
