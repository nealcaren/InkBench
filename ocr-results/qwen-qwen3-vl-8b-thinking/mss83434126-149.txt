Cartho made additional changes, and at early noon he attacked the rebels along his whole line, the rebels were instantly thrown with confusion and fled precipitately on all the attack, The division of Van Doen retreated in one direction that of Price escaped by another, The pursuit was continued by Genl. Sigle toward Betherville, and by the cavalry toward the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete, Rebel loss 2000 killed and wounded also 1000 prisoners, among the dead was found Genl. Br. Collick, a peculiar feature in this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians, under the command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were demonstrated that after battle the federal slain and wounded were found to be scalped, these surviving the prisoners seemed to have undergone a savage slaughter, Union loss 500 killed, and 900 wounded.

1862 March 14th Genl. McCullohan assumes command of the Army of the Potomac in the field, — Genl. Fremont assumes command of the Mountain Department — Genl. Halleck assumes command of the Department of the Mississippi — Br. Cameron supplied by the Federals, —
