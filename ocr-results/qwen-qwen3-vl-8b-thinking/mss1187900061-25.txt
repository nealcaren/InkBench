Legation of the United States,  
Monrovia, Liberia,  
July 25, 1883.  

Honorable  
H. D. Douglass,  
Washington, D.C.  

Dear Mr. Douglass:  

Respectfully, as I have been unable to see you, but I have been so happy to hear from you, I am exceedingly pleased at the vigor and brilliancy of your speech delivered on the occasion commemorative of Emancipation in the District of Columbia and I am particularly edified and pleased by your utterances, and surprised at the vigor and brilliancy of your speech after an almost constant absence of thirty for nearly a half century in the cause of oppressed people.
