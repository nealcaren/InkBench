Mery dear Steady: Oct 19th, 1913

I do not think I could possibly convey to you the very great pleasure and satisfaction your breezy graphic letter gave me. I wanted so much all that you told me and you told it clearly that it was like a conversation with you.
The Christmas card came and was hung up on the foot of my bed for alas I was there. They tell us that travellers in the tropics must beware of diarrhea but I thought myself quite immune as I had not had such a thing for thirty years, but I got one nevertheless and was in bed a week.
