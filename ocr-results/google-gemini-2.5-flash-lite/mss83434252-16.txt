About a month later, the Secretary of War, for the first time, authorized the raising of negro troops, by directing General Rufus Saxton to arm, uniform, equip and receive into the service of the United States such numbers of volunteers of African descent as hemight deem expedient, not exceeding five thousand, and to detail officers toimstruct and command them. In September, the Union victory at Antietam so strengthened the administration that the President at once issued his preliminary Emanicipation, which was to go into effect January 1, 1863; and after that step all logical objection to using the negroes as a military factor ceased.
