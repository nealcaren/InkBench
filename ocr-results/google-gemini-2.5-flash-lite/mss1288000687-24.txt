An undaunted devotion to the cause of woman suffrage links the name of Alice Stone Blackwell, colonel of the Boston League of Women Voters, with that of her famous mother, Lucy Stone. Hers was a pioneer of woman's rights. Her father, Henry B. Blackwell, who had been an abolitionist and practically the only man who really devoted his life to obtaining equal rights for women.
