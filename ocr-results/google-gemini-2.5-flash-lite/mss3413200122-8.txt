Nothing could be more illogical, or more unwise, than
to treatvivisection as the vice of Biologists specially;
nothing could be more futile than to hope to stop it while
we treat it so ; it would be on a level with trying to
supress the eruption of scarlatina, treating the spots as
the sole seat of the disease. Vivisection, as I said, is the
outcome of an intellectual disease, which is vitiatiating the
whole scientific and educational life of the nation; and
that disease is, resistance to Truth; unwillingness to know
the Laws of Thought; laziness in following such of them
