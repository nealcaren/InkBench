And am glad to feel that they have
brought me Shaw, they have taken
away. I have lived over and beyond
Graty Brain vigour, and am content to
find myself beloved and useful in a
small sphere.
I think You would be sorry for My
Mother were you to see her now. Her body
lives, but her mental powers are sadly
impaired. The care of her is over-ruled
at times, for she is in the border land
between sanity and -insanity, and
cannot yet be relied on left & other hands than
Mine. She is quite an aged woman
Seventy five years old.

And yet, sad as it is, it is in some ways
easier that the relations between us!
used to be. She has no power any more to
spoil my life, and patience is comparatively
easy, because of the faithfulness of her
present condition.
But you will wonder why I am

This beautiful but far away, land. Separated
from all my friends, and from the many
interests which have heretofore made my
life. A more complete contrast to a large
city can hardly be imagined than my
surroundings here.
My youngest brother, an able fellow
& Medical Examiner, never very robust,
became a physician. He had a brilliant
professional future before him, when, owing
to Mental strain and overwork, his Health
gave way. He fell a victim to that
dreadful disease locomotor ataxia.
He cannot live in New York. The last
winter we were in Madison Square had
a very trying one in regard to weather.
And a very sad one to us as we were
feeling keenly our recent bereavement.
In the sudden death of my oldest brothers
Sweet Young Wife, who had endeared
herself to all our hearts - in quite a
remarkable manner.
