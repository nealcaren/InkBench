OWEN C. BECKER, PROP.

July 15, 1909.

My dear Steady;

I thank you for two
letters and the notes and clippings concerning
Mrs Blinn. I thank you especially for the clif-
pings for as you suspect I am interested
in her uplifting. Last year, in Buffalo, at the
National Convention, I think Mrs Sincle brought
a letter she had received from Effie Vance,
and she had described very vividly how
Mrs Blinn had been able to rise above her
sufferings thro’ Christian Science. Eunice’s
tears flowed as she read it, and she was so
great a admirer of Mrs B. I believe she told
me that E Vance was also getting inter-
ested in Ch. Sci. Mrs. B. was one of the most
generous and forgiving natures I ever
met, and altho she also had her weaknesses
of character, as we all so the rest of us, she was
a woman to be admired for very many
estimable qualities.
