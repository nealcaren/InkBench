Alice Stone Blackwell

More than any other living person, symbolizes
the whole range of the struggle of women throughour generations to win untrammeled
human status. One of her aunts was the first woman in the world to obtain a medical
degree and another aunt was the first to be ordained a minister. Her mother, Lucy
Stone, was the first woman in the state to go to college, became a lecturer against
Negro slavery and for woman's rights when women were considered an indecency; and throughout her life was one of the half-dozen great
natonial figures in the woman movement. Her father gave a lifetime of service to
the woman's cause.

The life of the daughter was inextricably interwoven from babyhood in the widely
varied activities of her parents, who Miss Blackwell has assisted in her book "Lucy
Stone, Pioneer of Woman Rights".  For thirty-four years, Secretary of the National American

In line with her family's pioneering interest in the field of women's education she
has served Boston University, her alma mater, as a trustee since 1907.

Throughout these long years her sensitive humanity has made her responsive and
countless other struggles against the injustices of the world.  Roused by the Armenian massacres of

varied efforts to win them public devotion and support she rendered into English

The atrocious oppressions of the Czar's government led her to active work with
