That after a battle of two hours they (the Northen Forces) were repulsed leaving many dead and wounded, and that tranquility reigned in the South. We shall have on the 19th a mail from Potan Prince via Navaseca, a cable on 20th via Cuba and a mail again on the 25th. I continue to believe that the Northern Forces will raise the siege and retreat to the North, proclaiming peace "secession", and that the unfortunate state of things will continue for months. If the government of Cleveland and Harris now has acted in good faith in their relations with Hayti, this war would not have lasted... I ask myself if I would not be well for me to go to Port-Auprince with Mr. Douglass to see if the two parties continued - ing cannot arrive at an honorable understanding." Now, my dear Douglass, this letter is already so long that I must cut it short right here. Very sincerely and very cordially yours, Mrs. Bassett and Totly with write to Miss Douglass, EE Bassett [SIGNATURE]
