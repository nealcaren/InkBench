1862 June 17th.  Occupation of Cumberland Gap, Tenn.  The federals
under Gen. Morgan, advanced toward this place for the purpose
of expelling the rebel Gen. Stevenson & Smith who occupied
the gap with 13,000 men and well fortified the same.  This
gap is a cleft in the Cumberland mountains, and is so deep
and narrow that there is through the gap forming a single
roadway.  It was expected that the rebels could defend
its possession with the utmost tenacity.  No such result
followed.  When the rebels were informed of the approach
of the federals, they precipitately evacuated the place,
