New York, Oct 21, 1879

My dear Mr. Dickinson –

I am afraid your business affairs are again suffering.  I’m here but the party has exceeded to come next week.  “Nice a man as one live” but “Can’t Keep a Hotel”  I am sorry, I think how I engineered your “bus” with Mr. Mattoon – I might have been now in a different position –
You told me you would see me about your theatrical business, & the week I hear you have “a boy” (as it were) dashing with your affair, & “musing their Chiron up”  I thought you had a clear experience before vent “Amateur Managers” & had learned by experience the value of management.  Don’t think I am scolding you only that I feel slighted.  I cared so much for you, than any man in this business – or out of it – for that matter – (or ruin) but don’t choose fully
