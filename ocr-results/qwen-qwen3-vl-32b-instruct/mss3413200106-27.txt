129 E. 72 St.,
N.Y. City.
26 June, 1882.

Dear Miss Blackwell,

I am blessed with a propensity to be always at something. You are something; still you will not draw an illogical conclusion, as three successive epistles might tempt a less reasonable creature to do. The truth is, it's not; not beyond creed, to say nothing of deed. What resource else was left but you. The other evening, 20 & 20 were spoken of to me as having done this and that as nurse in the war, I was called on to admire her. I had to say, yes, I admire what she did. But must she not be a noble woman to do so much. Then I said it's very well for us you and she & me, to do some good. It's the only recommendation we can have; But really I look to see one who, without creed, to recommend him. Seeing whom one thinks not how much he has done or can do, but how much he is.

I perceived I was talking Greek, & went no further; but you who understand Greek, is this really so unintelligible?
