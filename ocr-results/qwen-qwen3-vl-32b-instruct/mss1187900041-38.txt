Hon. W. D. Kelley has my application for a clerkship in the Treasury in hand, and my chances for a position by the first of next month are good.  Judge Kelley has got Decy Boutwell to promise me a $1600 clerkship provided I will do some extra writing for the judge out of office hours.  Of course I have consented to do so, and my appointment is only a question of a little time.  Senator Anthony gave me a very flattering recommendation, as also did Senator Pomeroy, Hon. Geo. W. Julian, and Noah Davis member from our dist. who gave me a letter to Mr. Boutwell covering two pages.  All of these gentlemen desired particularly to be remembered to you.
I am a little crowded financially as my family is quite large and all had to come through me.  Libbie is expecting to be sick shortly, and both Levi. and Fid. are doing nothing and I hate to tell them of my circumstances as I know either of them would shun their last dollar with me.
I wrote for Lamy to come here, (and she did so at her own expense) to take care of Libbie during her sickness.  I would have to pay a nurse here 6 a week.  I spent my last hundred dollars a few days ago in paying for my fence, garden tools, seed, and a new stove which cost me $25 with all the furniture.
I want now about $100 until I can get to work.  I could borrow the money here, but as I have not had that to do thus far I dislike to ask anybody here to loan me any.
