The Light in the Tunnel.  
From the Virginia City Chronicle.  
A new device for the bafeliment of lovers  
was invented yesterday by a heartless young man on  
the local rails. A couple sat in the seat before  
him. The lady was young and modest, and the swain  
was very love sick. One arm was thrown carelessly  
across the back of the seat, and upon this the evil-minded  
person behind rubbed some phosphorus. The affection-  
ate performance of that pair, when the train was rush-  
ing through the kindly darkness of the tunnel, were be-  
held by all the enfeeling and snickering occupants of the  
car, whose cruel enjoyment was not a little increased by  
the dimness and unconcerned appearance of the lovers  
each time the train bounded again into light.

do if he struck her, for when he did so, she drew a re-
