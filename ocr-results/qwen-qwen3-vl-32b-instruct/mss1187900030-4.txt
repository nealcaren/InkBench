Elizabeth is making preparations to lead
her lecture next Monday night and have
us on Tuesday or Wednesday of next week.
She has not made her other appointments yet but I think that day or
morning few will attend to it, rather widy
in a great deal of noise and confusion being
in the schoolroom and the few who have
brought their diners are as full of tea
as possible.  I hope you will have as com-
fortable a time as possible and that you
will not return here sick.  (Old Brown,
the Governor of Missouri has a reward of
$3,000 offered for his capture and Hand-
ton has given himself up.)

Mother wishes me to give her love
and enclosed you will find a note
from Elizabeth and perhaps Annie
will send a few lines.

Your affec. Daughter,
Rosetta Douglass
