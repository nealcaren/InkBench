May 24 1923

CARRIE CHAPMAN CATT
404 RIVERSIDE DRIVE
NEW YORK

Dear Mary: I received a book yesterday the 24th gift this spring! If this book tells how to attain success with a boat when the author (would be) has neither oars nor pipe and no vocabulary to express the feeble thoughts he has, it will be most welcome.  Unless I have read it I shall not know whether to return it or to keep it with thanks or without thanks either way! I am sending you a tiny box of life over seeds.  This is no gift, I just had them.  I haven't the remotest idea what any of them are, but I'm not sure I'll find any.  If you were nearer I should have given you a garden full of plants from the greenhouse.  Which will either be in my garden or alr finished little blossoms.  They may be give stalwart wonders in yours.  When your corn comes, I shall take you to see a garden that is worth looking at.
