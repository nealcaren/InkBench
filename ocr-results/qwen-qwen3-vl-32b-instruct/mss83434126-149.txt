Curtis made additional charges, and at early dawn he attacked the rebels along his whole line, the rebels were instantly thrown into confusion and fled precipitately on all the attack.  The division of Van Dorn retreated in one direction that of Price escaped by another.  The pursuit was continued by Genl. Sigel toward Keithville, and by the Cavalry to the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete, Rebel loss 2000 killed and wounded also 1000 prisoners, among the rebel dead was found Genl. Dr. Culpeper, a peculiar feature in this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians, under the command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were demonstrated that after battle the federal slain and wounded were found to be scalped, thus renewing the prominent scenes of sanguinary slaughter.  Union loss 300 killed, and 900 wounded.

1862 March 11th  Genl. McClellan assumes command of the Army of the Potomac in the field, — Genl. Fremont assumed command of the Mountain Department — Genl. Halleck assumes command of the Department of the Mississippi — Tennessee occupied by the Federals,
