Tempus fugit
If in the Autumn of life, old age
is the only evil I should dread,
I die in peace: the route is clear
and I know what I desire for self
and family.  I feel perfectly able
for what I wish to do, and
ought to do for the future.
The letters in "N.Y. Tribune"
stirring up strife and
stultify out other difficulty.  The
Buchanan administration is
on its last legs, and will be
gone in a few months.  The
Page Hill at your house
and life which will give the
year on the Atlantic should kill
Buchanan's position.  What in
Richmond our friends are
doing I know not; but I
suppose they are waiting
with impatience the result
of Lincoln's election.  Our
Maryland neighbors: how
impatient they must be
to see the Union preserved
without war.  I have
such trust on that point
that I am not anxious
about it.
