Our Mother's Library

The Mother's Library for what
not from young composers who
print new songs, but from
authors, & we trust for a larger
proportion of them from women
authors, & we trust for a larger
proportion of them from women
of leisure, who have studied
their subject, & whose time
is their own.  But we have
not yet grown so fastidious
that we shall reject a small
piece because it is not a large
one, or a story because it is
not a novel.  If you have
not written a book, do not
suppose that you are not an
author.  If you have written
a story or a poem, send it to us.

Our little editors, however,
help us in another way.  With
the Nursery Song, a Young
Mother writes: "I have been
so much pleased with the
numbers of the Little Corporal
that I have subscribed for
Hall's Journal, (two copies.)
Read & be blest!"  Another
writes: "I enclose you a
dollar for the Little Corporal.
For a conscientious mother you
need not seek further."

This anonymous artist is not to
be envied in her wish to
engage our attention to her
drawing. "Weave" away, & we
will look at your work, as
soon as you have woven it
out of a name.  Tom.
