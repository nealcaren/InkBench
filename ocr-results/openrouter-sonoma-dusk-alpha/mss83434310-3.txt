Omaha, Nov. 14, 1865

Sir,

Enclosed please find draft for $1000, without interest, in payment for the first quarter's rent, due Nov. 1st, on the house in New Orleans, La.

To the second quarter -- Jan. 1st, 1866 -- please apply the enclosed check for $500.

To the third quarter -- April 1st, 1866 -- please apply the enclosed check for $500.

The fourth quarter will be paid on or before July 1st, 1866.

-- Dept. of the Interior, second -- Gen. Land Office, Washington, D.C., Nov. 14, 1865.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your letter of the 10th inst., enclosing a check for $500, in payment of the rent due on the property of the United States, in New Orleans, La., from July 1st to October 1st, 1865.

The rent of the property, which is $500 per quarter, has been paid in full to date.

I am, sir, very respectfully, your obedient servant,

J. H. Surget

Hon. S. P. Chase

Secretary of the Treasury

Washington, D.C.

[On the back, handwritten:]

Received, Washington, Nov. 20, 1865, from the Secretary of the Treasury, the sum of five hundred dollars ($500), in full for the rent of the United States property in New Orleans, La., from July 1st to October 1st, 1865.

J. H. Surget
