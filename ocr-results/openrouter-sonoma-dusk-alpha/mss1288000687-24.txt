ACCURACY

World Watch Has Been a

Standard of Accuracy for

More Than a Half

Century

Accuracy has been the watchword of the Boston Watch Company since it was founded by the late Louise M. Boutelle and her husband, Sylvanus B. Boutelle, in 1884.  In 1893, when the company was incorporated under the name of the Blackwell Watch Company, Mrs. Boutelle became president, a position she held until her death in 1924.  The company was then renamed the Blackwell Watch Company, Inc., in her honor.  From its inception the Blackwell Watch has been known for its high degree of accuracy and dependability.  In 1925 the company introduced a volume-controlled radio which was a forerunner of present-day radios.  In 1930 the company entered the field of electric clocks with the production of the first synchronous electric clock.  Today the Blackwell Watch Company is one of the leading manufacturers of electric clocks in the United States.
