The city of Havana has about 5000 inhabitants in part of the tobacco trade. The export of the leaf, in which the city has a monopoly, is managed by about 20 houses, the principal of which are those of Cabañas & Co., Cabot & Co., the Messrs. Roca, and the house of D. J. de Pablo. The trade is passing, however, from the hands of Spaniards, into those of the United States, and generally English, houses. There are about 50 factories of tobacco, in which a great quantity of cigars is made for exportation, and a great number of kinds are manufactured, some for the home, some for the foreign market. There are also for exportation two manufactories of snuff. The cultivation of tobacco is carried on in the richest districts of the island, and the tobacco is of the finest quality. The exportation of tobacco is about 25,000,000 lbs. annually, of which it is probable not much less than one-half is the produce of the Vuelta de abajo, the district of the western part of the island, which is celebrated for the superior quality of its tobacco. With the exception of the Vuelta de abajo, which is worked from Havana, the other districts are worked from Matanzas, Cárdenas, Sagua, etc. The quantity exported from the port of Matanzas is

The state of things in 1840 was 1,762,000 lbs. in 4 houses, of which 2 were in Pennsylvania Avenue. The increase in exports is due in a great measure to the introduction of the manufacture of plug tobacco in the United States. Messrs. Gilliam, of Richmond, a Spanish house, and others, have established a manufacture of this article, which is exported to the United States in considerable quantities. This trade is an entirely new one.

M. H. Gallatin, of the firm of Gallatin & Co., of Havana, a Spanish resident in the United States, and a gentleman well versed in the tobacco trade, has been kind enough to furnish the following statement in relation to the tobacco trade of Havana: "The tobacco of the island of Cuba is of a superior quality, and the exports from Havana are immense. The cigars manufactured there are of the finest quality, and the trade is constantly increasing. The city of Havana is the center of the tobacco trade of the island, and the exports from that port are very large."
