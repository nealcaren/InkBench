Sunset Park, N.Y.
July 15, 1897

My dear Friend

Much thanks for thy letter & the news & clippings concerning
Mrs. Blinn.  I thank thee for thy sincere courtesy.
Natural affection, in spite of the 
National complications, will, I think, 
cause her to send from her effects, 
a letter & perhaps my girdle from 
suffering that Christian women should 
be so bound by prejudice.  
Great credit is due Mrs. Quimby for 
her tact & patience in the matter 
of the B. & Mrs. B's abrupt return 
from Europe & the finding of a
suitable place for my many
