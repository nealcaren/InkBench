Private.  George Branch, Company G, 18th Regt. U.S. Colored
Infantry.  [In pencil: (In this)]

George Branch, Company G, 18th
U.S. Colored Infantry.  Enlisted 24th July 1863, at Nashville,
Tenn., age 18 years, 5 feet 6 inches high, complexion black,
eyes black, hair black, born in Robertson County, Tenn.,
occupation farmer, enlisted by Lieut. John A. A. Smith, mustered
in by Lieut. John A. A. Smith, at Nashville, Tenn., July 24th
1863.  Now in the hospital, clothes, &c. returned, and
transferred to the V.R.C.  [?]

The V.R.C. is the Veteran Reserve Corps, a body of
soldiers, with the same organization as the regular army,
whose duties are to guard public buildings, &c., in the
cities, and to do duty at the hospitals.  They are
sometimes detailed to do duty at the front, but not
often.  It is considered a soft place, and men are
sent there when they are disabled for field service,
but are still able to do garrison duty.  They are
not mustered out of the service, but simply transferred
from one corps to the other.  The V.R.C. is a part of
the regular army, and the men are subject to the
same rules and regulations as the other troops.

Private William Jones, Company D, 18th U.S. Colored Infantry:
[In pencil: (In this)]

William Jones, Company D, 18th U.S. Colored Infantry.  Enlisted
at Nashville, Tenn., August 2d, 1864, a substitute for William
R. Smith, who furnished him.  He was found in the office
of the Provost Marshal, 18th District, and sent to the
Regiment.  He says he was sent by the officer
of the Board.  He has been in the Regiment but a
short time, and is now in hospital.  He has half pay
attached to left leg.
