A Call to Action
A Speech at the
Democratic National
Convention, 1936

A Democratic Victory Speech by
John L. Lewis, President of the
United Mine Workers of America

There are women in this country who in other years
have been led to believe that their interests would be
better served by the party in power than by the
party which I represent tonight.  Let me tell those
women that their husbands and their sons are now
receiving the benefits of the legislation sponsored
by the party in power.  Let those women ask their
husbands and their sons if they will let them go
back to the conditions which prevailed before 1933.

We don't want this country to go back to the
conditions which prevailed before 1933.  We want
to go forward.  We want to build a new order in
which the people of this country can live in peace
and prosperity.

Everybody knows that this is a critical time in the
history of the world.  We are facing a situation
which is unprecedented in the history of the world.

I am not talking about the League of Nations or
about international relations.  I am talking about
the conditions in this country.

We are living in a time when the people of this
country are demanding that something be done to
relieve their distress.  They are not going to be
satisfied with promises.  They want action.

Today we are in a position where we can say to the
people of this country that we have a program which
will meet their needs.  We have a program which
will bring relief to the unemployed, which will
provide security for the aged, which will protect
the rights of labor.

We have a program which will build homes for the
people, which will provide work for the unemployed,
which will restore prosperity to the farmer.

We have a program which will protect the rights of
the consumer, which will regulate the utilities,
which will control the banks.

We have a program which will build a new America,
an America in which the people can live in peace
and prosperity.

We don't believe in the philosophy of the Liberty
League.  We don't believe in the philosophy of the
du Ponts.  We don't believe in the philosophy of
the money changers.  We believe in the philosophy
of the people.  We believe in the philosophy of
Jefferson and Jackson and Lincoln.

We believe in the philosophy of the Declaration of
Independence.  We believe in the philosophy of the
Bill of Rights.  We believe in the philosophy of
the Constitution.

We believe in the philosophy of democracy.  We
believe in the philosophy of freedom.  We believe
in the philosophy of equality.

We believe in the philosophy of the New Deal.  We
believe in the philosophy of Franklin D. Roosevelt.

We believe in the philosophy of the people.  We
believe in the philosophy of progress.  We believe
in the philosophy of hope.

We believe in the philosophy of the future.  We
believe in the philosophy of America.
