Dec 15 1872

Dear Miss Blackall,

I am sorry to still be at something
of a loss, in the assurances
you wish.  But I am sure
you will understand the
situation, when I tell you
that I have not yet heard
from Mrs. B.  of the terms, that we
are to serve, nor even of the
day she is to arrive.  I
have written to her, urging
her to advise me at once
in the matter, and shall
do so again, if I do not
hear from her in two or
three days.  In the mean
time, I am sure you will
see the propriety of my not
committing myself, to
anything that I may not
be able to carry out.

I regard our calling each
other by name, as a
pleasant & proper
arrangement.  I am
glad you like it.  The
only thing I have to
say, is, that I am
afraid our Falling Rock
visit must be deferred.
Jack is really so much
intrusted with business.
