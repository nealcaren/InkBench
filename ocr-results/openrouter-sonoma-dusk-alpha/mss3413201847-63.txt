A JOINT RESOLUTION

Proposing an amendment to article seven of the Constitution
of Pennsylvania, requiring the registration of electors.

Section 1. Be it resolved by the Senate and House of
Representatives of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania in General
Assembly met, That the following amendment is proposed to the
Constitution of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, and the same
shall be submitted to the qualified electors thereof, at the
general election to be held on the Tuesday next after the first
Monday of November in the year of our Lord one thousand nine
hundred and twelve, for the purpose of voting for or against the
same:

Amend section one of article seven, which reads as follows:—
"That every male citizen twenty-one years of age, possessing the
following qualifications, shall be entitled to vote at the polling
place of the election district of which he shall at the time be
a resident and not elsewhere: First. He shall have been a citizen
of the United States at least thirty days. Second. He shall have
resided in the State one year (or, having previously been a
qualified elector or native born citizen of the State, he shall
have removed therefrom and returned, then six months) immediately
preceding the election. Third. He shall have resided in the
election district where he shall offer to vote at least two
months immediately preceding the election. Fourth. If twenty-two
years of age or upwards, he shall have paid, within two years, a
State or county tax, which shall have been assessed at least two
months and paid at least one month before the election," so as to
read as follows:

Section 1. Every male citizen twenty-one years of age,
possessing the following qualifications, shall be entitled to
vote at the polling place of the election district of which he
shall at the time be a resident and not elsewhere: First. He
shall have been a citizen of the United States at least thirty
days. Second. He shall have resided in the State one year (or,
having previously been a qualified elector or native born
citizen of the State, he shall have removed therefrom and
returned, then six months) immediately preceding the election.
Third. He shall have resided in the election district where he
shall offer to vote at least two months immediately preceding
the election. Fourth. If twenty-two years of age or upwards, he
shall have paid, within two years, a State or county tax, which
shall have been assessed at least two months and paid at least
one month before the election. Fifth. All citizens of this State,
whether male or female, who, being otherwise qualified by
continued residence in their election districts, shall have been
registered according to law in the manner which may be provided
by law, as provided by the General Assembly, shall be entitled to
vote at all elections.

Section 2. That any amendment contemplated by the first section
of this resolution shall be made a part of the Constitution when
the same shall have been ratified by a majority of the qualified
electors of the Commonwealth at the general election next
occurring at least three months after the final adjournment of
the present session of the General Assembly.
