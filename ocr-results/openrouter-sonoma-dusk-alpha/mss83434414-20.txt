Surgeons were instructed to report to the Medical Department of the Bureau all cases of freedmen and refugees who had been wounded or otherwise disabled in the service of the United States, and who were in need of medical attendance or other aid.  They were also required to report the names of all freedmen and refugees who had died in hospitals under their charge, giving the cause of death and any other facts of interest in the case.  They were directed to make monthly reports of the sick and wounded in their districts, and to keep a record of all cases treated by them.

They were instructed to make special efforts to secure hospital accommodations for freedmen and refugees in the hospitals of the medical department, and to report any cases of neglect or abuse of freedmen and refugees in the hospitals of the medical department.

Reports were to be made to the Surgeon General of the army, and to the Assistant Secretary of the Bureau, and copies of the reports were to be forwarded to the Medical Director of the department in which the surgeon was stationed.

The reports of the surgeons were to be made on the forms prescribed by the Surgeon General, and were to be forwarded to the Surgeon General's office, Washington, D.C., on or before the 10th day of each month.

The reports were to include a statement of the number of freedmen and refugees treated, the character of the diseases treated, the number of deaths, and the number of cases in which the patients were discharged as cured.
