Esteemed Friend,

Thy note of the 21st. has just reached me, & I am very glad that the enclosed has arrived safely.  It was the last of the series I had from my dear brother, & I felt anxious that it should be forthcoming.  I trust it was not long delayed.  My own letters from home have been few & brief of late, as the summer's work has been unusually pressing upon my father, & I know that when he is thus occupied, he finds but little leisure for correspondence.  I have however had one or two from my mother, from which I learn that they are all in their usual health, though the heat has been oppressive.  She mentions that cousin Eliza is with them, & that they are enjoying her society very much.

When I received thine enclosing the letter, I was on the point of starting for Philadelphia, where I had business which detained me for several days.  On my return, I found thy note awaiting me, & I am rejoiced to learn that the enclosure reached thee in safety.  I had feared that it might have been mislaid in the multiplicity of thy engagements, but I am glad it was not the case.  My father has been much occupied with the concerns of the meeting, & I know that he has had but little time for writing, but I trust that ere long we shall hear from him more fully.

I have been much interested in the accounts I have seen of the proceedings of the Yearly Meeting, & I rejoice to learn that so much harmony prevailed.  It is a subject of deep interest to me, & I have felt a strong desire to be present on such occasions, but my situation here prevents it.  I hope however that through the kindness of Friends, I may be able to obtain full information of all that transpired.

With sincere sympathy for thy various engagements, & earnest desires for thy prosperity, I remain

Thy friend
Saml. J. Underhill
