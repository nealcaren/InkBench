Chapter XXXIII

After Johnston's surrender at Raleigh, we
paused towards Baltimore, where Stanton's army at Appo-
mattox, we started on our way to Washington, while
Grant's towards Richmond, where Sherman's army at
Raleigh, was held in readiness to march to Washington,
should the war close before his arrival at Raleigh, with
out a battle.  For several weeks, while we awaited
the close of hostilities, we were stationed at Manchester,
just below Richmond.  On the 1st of June, we
commenced our march for Washington, and arrived
there on the 19th of the same month.

While in Washington, Gen. Burnside's commission
was confirmed, and he was assigned to the command
of the Ninth Corps.  I was retained on his staff.
I had no desire to go and see the sights, but had
so much to do that I had no time for it, but
gave with the others.  We so many were going
daily to see the sights, that I went with the
others.  We saw the Capitol, the White House, the
Patent Office, the Smithsonian Institute, the Navy
Yard, the Arsenal, the Soldiers' Home, and the
Cemeteries.  We visited the Old Capitol Prison, and
saw the assassins of President Lincoln.  We also
went to Georgetown, and saw the old Observatory.

The Ninth Corps was stationed at Alexandria, and
we were there about two weeks.  We then went to
Baltimore, and were stationed at Fort McHenry for
a few days.  We then went to Philadelphia, and
were stationed at Camp Cadwalader for a few days.
We then went to Harrisburg, and were stationed at
Camp Curtin for a few days.  We then went to
Pittsburg, and were stationed at Camp Howe for
a few days.  We then went to Cleveland, and were
stationed at Camp Cleveland for a few days.  We
then went to Buffalo, and were stationed at Camp
Buffalo for a few days.  We then went to Elmira,
and were stationed at Camp Elmira for a few days.
We then went to Binghamton, and were stationed at
Camp Binghamton for a few days.  We then went to
New York, and were stationed at Riker's Island for
a few days.  We then went to New Haven, and were
stationed at Camp New Haven for a few days.  We
then went to Hartford, and were stationed at Camp
Hartford for a few days.  We then went to Providence,
and were stationed at Camp Providence for a few days.
We then went to Boston, and were stationed at Galloupes
Island for a few days.  We then went to Portland,
and were stationed at Camp Portland for a few days.
We then went to Bangor, and were stationed at Camp
Bangor for a few days.  We then went to Augusta,
and were stationed at Camp Augusta for a few days.
We then went to Concord, and were stationed at Camp
Concord for a few days.  We then went to Montpelier,
and were stationed at Camp Montpelier for a few days.
We then went to Burlington, and were stationed at Camp
Burlington for a few days.  We then went to Plattsburg,
and were stationed at Camp Plattsburg for a few days.
We then went to Ogdensburg, and were stationed at Camp
Ogdensburg for a few days.  We then went to Sackett's
Harbor, and were stationed at Camp Sackett's Harbor for
a few days.  We then went to Rochester, and were stationed
at Camp Rochester for a few days.  We then went to
