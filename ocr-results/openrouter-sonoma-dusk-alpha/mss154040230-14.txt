Department of State
Washington, November 20, 1883

Dear Sir: I have received your letter of the 17th instant, in which you state that you have been informed by one of our consuls in Argentina that the Argentine Congress is in session, and that the committee on foreign relations of the Congress is considering a bill to aid in the construction of a line of steamers between Argentina and the United States, which, if passed, will be supported by the government of Argentina.

In reply, I have to state that no communication on the subject has been made to this government by the Argentine government, and that if such a bill should pass, it will not be published in the press until after it has passed Congress.

I have to say in reply to your request for information, I can go no further than to express the wish that the bill may pass.

Very respectfully,
F. T. Frelinghuysen
