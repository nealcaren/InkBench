1953:

THE KOREAN WAR

President Truman made at Yalta an agreement with the Russians to occupy Korea at the end of the war.  The agreement was made without discussing it with the Soviet Union, Russia, or the other Allies.  The United States divided Korea at the 38th parallel, with the Soviets occupying the north and the United States the south.  The occupation was supposed to be temporary.

In Europe the United States controlled a large area of territory which it soon gave to the European peoples, but in Asia through the world and in Central Europe and in Japan the United States kept control of the territory.

The Army of occupation in Axis territory was a large one, but in Japan the United States, although it had the responsibility of the entire country, had a small army.
