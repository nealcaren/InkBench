accustomed to filling the desk, and receiving
a large number of letters, but
none have ever given me so much
pleasure as those I have received from thee
of late.  I know of no language sufficiently
strong to express my thanks to thee
for thy kindness.  I was very glad to hear
from thy letter of yesterday that Mrs. Sarah
was convalescing.  I hope she will soon
be able to leave her room.  I was very
much pleased to hear of the marriage of
Miss [?] of Baltimore.  I hope she will
be happy.

I am very much pleased to learn that
the "Fugitive" has been sold to the
"North Star."  I think it will be
a great acquisition to that paper.

I am very glad to hear that the
"North Star" is doing so well.  I
hope it will continue to prosper.

I have not heard from my sister
since I wrote to thee.  I shall
write to her soon.

I am very much obliged to thee
for thy kindness in sending me
the "North Star."  I shall be
very glad to receive it.

1848 June 20th

I have just received thy letter of the
18th inst. enclosing a check for $50 for the
Fugitive.  I am very much obliged to thee
for it.  I shall forward it to the proper
quarter immediately.

I am very glad to hear that the
Fugitive is doing so well.  I hope
it will continue to prosper.

I have not heard from my sister
since I wrote to thee.  I shall
write to her soon.

for thy kindness in sending me
