The North of Ireland

The North of Ireland has two per cent more currency in circulation than the whole of the rest of Ireland. But the proportion of the population to the currency is much greater in the North. In the South there are about seven persons to every £100 in circulation. In the North there are about 11. The Northern bankers are already preparing for the change over. They are taking steps to have the notes disposed of in the South exchanged for Northern notes. The change will be made with as little inconvenience to the public as possible. The Northern Parliament has already passed legislation giving them the right to issue their own notes. The new notes will be based on the old, and the public will scarcely notice the change.
