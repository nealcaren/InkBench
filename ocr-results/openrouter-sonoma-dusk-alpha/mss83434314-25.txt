### Instructions for the Letter Book

1. Letters should be copied into the book in the order in which they are received, and the book should be kept in the office for that purpose.  After the name of the person addressed, note the date of the letter received, and the substance of the communication in a few words.  The number of the letter and the page on which it is copied should also be given in the index.

| 2 | No. or date | His or her name |
|---|-------------|-----------------|
|   | Feb. 3, 18-- | Jno. or Jane Doe |
|   | Feb. 4, 18-- | L. M. or N. O. Fox |

2. Each letter should be signed in the letter book by the sender, and the date of the letter sent should be written opposite the name of the person to whom the letter is addressed.  When copies of letters are furnished the names of the persons to whom the letters are sent should be written in the book, and the date of the letter sent should also be added in the office (see example below).

2. The index, file copy, & T. (Greeley's index?) should be made in black ink, and the names of all persons alphabetically arranged.
