Washington April 10 1887

Dear Father

Your letter received yesterday
& Mrs. Patten's enclosed.  She
says from the press she under-
stands my appointment as Consul
to Demerara is not yet
made.  I understand such is
the case.  In the meantime
I'm just about it in
Georgia & Colo. Taylor has
refused appointment as his
health will not permit
his leaving his field.  A friend
of his is out for the
place.  The political
outlook between Alfred & myself
is good.  New Bridge across
the Appomattox for which
the President's Office is moving & which
he has promised Mrs. & my
appointment when he returns
to Washington.  They expect
him here about May 1st.

J. S. Patten
