July 7th 1885

Prospectus

To the Patrons of this Institution:

It has been the aim of the Board of Trustees of the Good Will Seminary to give to the youth of the German Baptist Brethren, the best educational advantages possible within the means at their disposal.  The General Conference of 1884 authorized the location of the Seminary at the Collegeville church, Montgomery Co., Pa., and the Board of Trustees were instructed to take charge of the work.  The Board at once began to lay plans for the erection of a suitable building, and for carrying on the work of the Institution in a manner worthy of the great denomination it represents.  The erection of a commodious and substantial building has been the first object, and this is now well under way, and will be completed in a few months.  The building is so planned as to admit of enlargements as the growth of the Institution may demand.  The main building is three stories high, with a basement, and is 50 by 80 feet.  There will be a wing 40 by 50 feet, and a chapel 40 by 60 feet.  The whole will be built of brick, and will be heated by steam, and supplied with water from the Collegeville water works.  The Institution will be under the management of the Board of Trustees, and the Principal will be responsible to them for the conduct of the school.  The course of study will be such as to prepare students for college, or for teaching in the public schools, or for business.  Special attention will be given to the Bible and to music.  The Institution will be open to students of both sexes.  The rates of tuition will be as low as possible consistent with good work.  Board can be had in the village at reasonable rates.  The school will open on the first Wednesday in September, and continue forty weeks.

For further information, address the Principal, or any member of the Board of Trustees.

John H. Ober, President.
John F. Eby, Secretary.
A. C. Holopeter, Treasurer.

Collegeville, Pa., July 7th, 1885.
