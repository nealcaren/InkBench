passed an act, which was signed by Governor
Holliday on May 23rd, withdrawing from the
city of Richmond the old Capitol building, which
was then used as the state library, and setting
apart the new State Library Building for the
same purpose.

The old canal, which was built along the south side of the James River, is 20 feet wide and 5 feet deep. It extends from the old dam at Westham to the Tidewater, a distance of 7 miles. It was built in 1795 and was used for many years for transportation of flour and tobacco. The locks are still in good condition.

The high ground on both sides of the canal is now occupied by the new city of Manchester. For years
this section of the city was known as the "Rocketts," a name which it received from the fact that the first tobacco inspection warehouse was located there. The old bridge still spans the river at this point. Nothing of the old buildings remain, but soldiers early in the Civil
War destroyed them all in order to prevent their falling into the hands of the Federal forces.
