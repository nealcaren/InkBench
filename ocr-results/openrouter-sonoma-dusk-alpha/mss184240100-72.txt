Thy dear Mother & I have
had our first visit together
since Dolly's death; from
her I learn that thy
last time here was
unusually happy.  I
suppose time in the
abstract does much to
soothe the anguish of
bereavement; but in
the retrospect of the
last 3 mos. I have
felt that the comfort
which I have had in
the thought of thy
happiness has been
one of the alleviations
of my own sorrow.

I was glad to hear
of thy visit to
Sally, & hope she
found thee as
well prepared as
I have been to
accept the dispensation
of our good Father
who doeth all things
well.  Thy letter
to Sally with
the account of the
bereavement which
had so lately come
upon thee was most
touching.  I read
it to thy Uncle
who was much affected
by it.  We both
think of thee with
much interest & 
thank our Heavenly
Father for the hope
we have of seeing
thee here soon.
