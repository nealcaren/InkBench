Frank is apt to be ill when
he is fatigued; but the
last week we have been
in a state of excitement
that has made rest impossible.
Our servant went from
us on Saturday & I
have been with the children
ever since, & my husband
has had full charge of
the house, in which state
of things the sleep has
gone from his eyes.  The
first servant I engaged
took the small pox the
day she came to us.  She
had been vaccinated, but
it proved ineffectual, & the
doctor said she must go
to the hospital.  She was
very sorry to leave us, but
we could not keep her.

I have engaged another
woman, but she does not
come until next week, &
until then we must get
along as well as we can.

The loss could scarcely
have happened at a worse
time, for my husband is
very busy with his new
book, & it is difficult for
him to be interrupted.  He
is now at the North, but
will return in a day or
two.  I wish to have all
in order when he comes
home.  So when I see
Maria, I shall ask her
if she knows of any one
who would be willing to
come to us for a few
days.
