Randolph Gardiner

A. M. Street Theatre

By the famous author of "The Second Mrs. Tanqueray" and "The Notorious Mrs. Ebbsmith"

New York, Oct 22nd 1899

My dear Mr Seymour,

Affairs are again so much in your hands
that I feel called on to explain
my present balance, & acquaint
you with a special question which
is uppermost in my mind.

In case of my death at most
you would have to make
my total assets do so much
your financial business, I think
you have had a pleasant
recollection of what I owe you.
I thought of having a little
chat with you, & making
a deposit with F. M. Avery
for the amount of the
draft which I enclose
in this letter, & which
must be for the present
purposes of the company.

I have been so busy of late
that I have not been able to
attend to it as I should have
wished, but I hope to have
it done before we go on tour.
