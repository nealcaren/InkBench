Carrie Chap Cat
May 24/29

Dear Mary,
I send a book which I think will interest thee this spring.  Gift from the author, who has just had a success with it, not from any sense of its merit, but because it has not been read by many, and that latter reason makes it valuable to me.  I send it with the hope that I may enjoy thy comments upon it.  We have had a very warm spring, and the lilacs are out, and the air is full of the perfume of the flowers.  They are beautiful, but they are so much more beautiful in thy garden than in mine.  I have a few little blossoms, though not many.  Girl, my heart wonders if thee has a garden too.  Come down and see it.  This is not from my garden.
