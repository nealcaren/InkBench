IN & AROUND JAPAN

IN THE GARDEN—HIS CANTON 27

The gardener brings his canton of vegetables ready for two gardeners to do a large amount of the shaving and haircutting in the numerous odd varieties of the open streets. Each of these quick barbershops is supplied with basins, remedies for every "ill flesh is heir to," brains, cosmetics, and reptiles; without any crowd, from bearing buckets of fresh water from the nearest objective purity. With arrangements of this city are primitive as hygiene is concerned, yet foreigners settle in it as if in a climate most objective. Entire carvers are wholly devoid of shops selling one manufactured article. There is no manufacturing of silk, for we enter a street where such trade is carried on. Beyond these passing these in the next foot shops, and last incense stores, marble shops, with silk books, and a great variety of other articles.
