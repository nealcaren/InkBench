of the Bureau has referred the subject
to the President for his opinion.

The breaking up of the old slave
system has left the colored people
without the usual means of support,
and the duty of the Government to
provide for their necessities has
devolved upon the officials of self-trust.
The interior officers of this trust, at
the best, are but partial relief, and
the people suffer.

Of necessity & mismanagement, the
old slave owners are entitled to
the sympathy of the nation, but the
colored people, in the following
instructions, are left to groundless
rumors, and the Freedmen's
Bureau is powerless to protect them.

In their way, as objects of the war,
the equality of the races and the
elevation of the colored people to
the full rights of citizenship, and
the peace of the country demand
that the policy of the President and
the laws of Congress, when disregarded
by the control of all officials, should be
enforced.

The powers of the President over the
Freedmen's Bureau and the interests of civil
rights being limited, the control of all
their relations, difficulties, and breaches
between them, devolve upon Congress,
and the officers of the Bureau should
have the sympathy and support of the
legislative branch of the Government.

Service, as far as practicable, by the
officials of the Bureau, in the
advancement of the interests of the
colored people, and in the protection
of their rights, has been obstructed
by the hostility of the white people
among whom they reside, and
the Freedmen's Bureau has been
unable to afford them the protection
they need.

The friends of the colored people who
have thus been denied the protection
of the laws, and exposed to the
outrages which have been committed
upon them, have complained with
great justice, where their complaints
have been disregarded, the greatest
iniquity of the whole system has been
exhibited.

The officers of the Freedmen's Bureau
have been the victims of this
exclusion from the sympathy of the
people among whom they labor, and
of the officials who have been
unwilling to co-operate with them
in the discharge of their duties.
