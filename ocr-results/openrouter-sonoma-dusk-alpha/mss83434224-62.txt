U.S. at Great Disadvantage
in STRAF

The U.S. is at a great disadvantage in the STRAF (Strategic Army Force), which includes the First and Third Armies, the Air Force, and the U.S.-based divisions of the U.S. Fleet.  This disadvantage was incurred in the aftermath of the 1960 Cuban crisis, which made the U.S. STRACOM (U.S. Strike Command) dependent upon the resources of the U.S. Atlantic and Continental Armies, Navy, and Air Force.  The Pentagon has since supplied the necessary personnel, but the bases in Florida; and Air sites in the Pensacola Base are in Florida; and Air sites in the Pensacola Base are insufficient for the expanded role of STRACOM in the defense of the U.S. Southeast.
