Dear Peter,

Richmond, Illinois,
July 29th, 1873.

My Dear Cousin,

I received your kind letter of the 23rd
with much pleasure, and have been intending
to answer it in the District of Columbia
and have delayed until your return
from the West by your desire
and confirmed by my own
willingness to your power after
an absence of yours power after
system for nearly a half
century of your life in.  I have news of
yourself.

Your affectionate cousin,
M. Bailey
