My dear Sister

I have been thinking of thee all day, & wishing I could see thee, but I suppose it is impossible. I have had a letter from Anna, telling me of thy illness, & I am very sorry to hear it. I hope it is nothing serious, & that thee will soon be better. I am quite well, & have been very busy, as usual. The children are all well, & send their love to thee. Little Mary is growing very fast, & is a great comfort to me. She talks of thee often, & says she wants to see her Aunt Susan. I have been reading thy last letter, & was much interested in what thee said about the meeting. I hope the time will come when we can all meet again, & have a good talk over old times.

I have had a letter from Cousin Elizabeth, enclosing one from her son in California. He seems to be doing well, & is very much pleased with the country. He says the climate is delightful, & that he has no desire to return East. I am glad to hear it, for I think it is a good thing for young men to go West, & make their own way in the world.

The spring is coming on very fast, & everything looks beautiful. The trees are beginning to bud, & the birds are singing all day long. I have been out in the garden, planting some seeds, & hope to have a good crop of vegetables this summer. The children are very fond of the garden, & like to help me. We have had some warm days, but the nights are still cold. I think we shall have a fine season.

I must stop now, as it is time to get tea. Give my love to all the family, & tell them I hope to see them all soon. Write to me when thee can, & let me know how thee is getting on.

Thy affectionate sister

Sarah

P.S. I enclose a little pattern for thee, which I think thee will like. It is for a quilt, & is very pretty.
