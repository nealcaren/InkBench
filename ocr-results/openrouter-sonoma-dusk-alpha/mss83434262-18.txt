To Collectors of Monies Disbursed.

To the Clerks of the Markets, and others, in the City of New York, who have in their possession the monies deposited with them by the inhabitants of this City, for the relief of the poor and orphans of the sufferers by the late fire.

It is with much concern we observe that a large sum of money collected for so laudable a purpose, remains in your hands, unappropriated to the objects for which it was collected.

In order to ascertain the amount of the monies in your possession, we request you will immediately forward to us the notices of all

deposits whereof you have been the depositories.

New York Fire Proof.

As the Forty Days have now nearly elapsed when May 6th last, we shall proceed to the payment of such claims as we shall judge to be entitled to payment out of the fund collected for the relief of the sufferers by the late fire, in the order in which they shall be presented to us, with proper vouchers.

By order of the Committee,

If from any cause the claimant shall be absent, we shall deposit the amount of his claim in the hands of the Chamberlain of this City, subject to his order.
