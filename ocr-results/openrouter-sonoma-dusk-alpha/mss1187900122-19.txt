To Mrs. Douglass: May 6/94

Dear Mrs. Douglass, - I have
been directed by the Committee
of the Boston Woman's Club
to write to you and ask
whether you would be willing
to read a paper before the Club
on the subject of the Woman's
Auxiliary of the Church.  I
have just seen your letter
published in the "Woman's Journal"
in which you so admirably
expressed your views on the
subject.  Mrs. Howe has
been much interested in it, & 
spoke to me about it, & I
thought it would be a very
good subject for one of our
meetings.  Would you be willing
to come & speak to us about
it?  I know it would be
very interesting.  I am
one of the members of the Club
who have always been interested
in the Auxiliary, & I think
you could speak very effectively
about it.  A former member of
the Club, Mrs. Hall, has been
much interested in it, & I
know she would be glad to
have you come.  I hope you
will let me know at once
whether you can do it.

Very truly yours,

Caroline E. Clifford.
