My dear Friend

I am glad to find that they have
been able to bring thee thro' the
ordeal.  We have had our own trials
but have felt our own & thy need
of help.  Mary has been ill & Susan
busy herself & afflicted.  But we
are now all better & I hope thee
will soon be.  We shall be ready
to carry thy messages as soon as
thee can send them.

Tho' we have been anxious for thee
we have been comforted by the
assurance of the faithful Friend
who has been with us & with thee.
We have had a beautiful letter from
thee, which we read with much
interest.  I have had a letter from
our dear Cousin Mary, which is
encouraging.  She has been able
to be out, & is much better.  We
are all glad to hear of thy improvement.

Thy affectionate friend

M. H. Crewdson

P.S.  I have just had a letter from
Susan, which I enclose for thy perusal.
It is from a dear old woman
who has been a faithful servant
in our family for many years.

[On the other side, vertical text]

My dear Friend

I trust thou hast been enabled to
bring thy mind into a state of
tranquillity since we parted.  I have
been much engaged in my own
concerns, but have not forgotten
thee.  I have been to see thy
sister, & found her much better.
She desires her love to be sent
to thee.

I have had a letter from our
friend in the North, which is
interesting.  She has been
able to attend meetings, &
is much improved in health.
We are all glad to hear of
thy welfare.

Thy affectionate friend

S. C.

P.S.  I hope thee will soon
be able to write to us.  We
shall be anxious to hear
from thee.
