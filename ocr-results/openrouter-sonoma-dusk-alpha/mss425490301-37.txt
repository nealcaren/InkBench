The California Federation of Women's Clubs was organized in 1896 with 14 clubs. Today there are 294 clubs with a membership of 25,000 women.  

The clubs have done outstanding work in the field of education and civic improvement. The first public kindergarten in California was established by the Los Angeles club in 1884. The California clubwomen have sponsored legislation for the protection of children, for the improvement of public health, and for the conservation of natural resources.  

There are 22 departments in the California Federation. These include departments of art, literature, music, education, public welfare, and conservation. The clubwomen have also taken an active part in the work of the National Federation.  

The four annual meetings of the Federation are held in different sections of the state.  

The officers for the year 1950-51 were:  

President: Mrs. Josephine R. Woods, Los Angeles  
First Vice-President: Mrs. Laura J. Watts, Los Angeles  
Second Vice-President: Mrs. E. C. Barker, Los Angeles  
Recording Secretary: Mrs. John W. Smith, Los Angeles  
Corresponding Secretary: Mrs. Robert E. Jones, Los Angeles  
Treasurer: Mrs. Harry L. Brown, Los Angeles  

Mrs. Clara L. Smith, of Los Angeles, was the president of the California Federation from 1946 to 1948. She was active in club work for many years and gave much time to the promotion of the Federation's program.
