Department of State
Washington, 3d April 1868

Sir:  Your letter of the 30th ult. has been received.  
It was written in haste, & I do not doubt you  
will send the required information by mail.  
The object was to have it on file, in  
case it should be needed.  I hope you  
will send it for this purpose.  

It has been the invariable practice of this  
Department to require two months of service of  
applicants for examination, & I do not see  
how we can make an exception in your  
case.  However, I will see you on the  
subject.  

The examination is announced for the  
first Tuesday in May.  The questions  
are prepared from the Library of  
the Department.  The general subjects  
are History, Geography, Mathematics,  
and the English Language.  

I wish you success.  Should you  
be successful, the Department will  
be glad to give you an appointment  
in the Consular Service.  

Very respectfully,  
Your obedient servant,  

F.W. Seward
