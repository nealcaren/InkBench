The American Society for the
Prevention of Cruelty to Animals
Incorporated 1866 in New York

To The Probate Judge of Anderson Co. S.C.

Sir,  Mrs. Susan Ottis whose maiden name was
Susan Williams is now dead, as I am informed, & her
death occurred about the last of august.  I believe she
was in a low state of health for some time before her
death, but not confined to her bed.  I do not know of any
property she had, but if she had any, I think she made
no disposition of it.  Her file under the will is quiet, I have
no interest in the estate whatever.  She was the widow
of the late John Ottis, & after his death she was married
to Mr. Thomas W. Hill, who is also dead.  I think she
married Mr. Hill about 3 years after the death of her first
husband.  I have known Mrs. Ottis since her marriage
to Mr. Ottis, & her acquaintance with Mr. Hill was made
through me.  She was a woman of good sense & I think
she would have made a different disposition of her property
if she had intended to give it away.

The lower part of the will

very respectfully
Anne Reeder.

Witness
Wm. H. Reeder.
1844
