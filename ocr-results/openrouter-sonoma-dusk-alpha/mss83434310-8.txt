In the case of John H. Gill, alias John Gill, otherwise called John Gill, who is charged with the crime of murder, alleged to have been committed by him on the 25th day of April, A. D. 1866, in the Parish of Orleans, State of Louisiana, and who is now in custody in the Parish Prison of the Parish and City of New Orleans, charged with said offense.

Private Examination of this defendant, John H. Gill, was taken by me, J. C. Monroe, Judge of the Second Judicial District Court of the Parish of Orleans, sitting in chambers at the Court House in the City of New Orleans, on this 29th day of August, A. D. 1866, between the hours of 10 o'clock A. M. and 4 o'clock P. M., the defendant being first cautioned in the usual manner, and being informed of his right to refuse to answer any question that might be propounded to him, and of his right to the advice and assistance of counsel, and being asked if he had any counsel, replied that he had none, but that he was willing to make a statement of the facts within his knowledge, touching the charge against him, without oath, and that he did not desire to have counsel, and that what he should say was to be used against him, and not for him; and the said defendant then and there made the following statement, in the presence of S. S. Herald, District Attorney, and no other person, which statement was reduced to writing by me, the said Judge, and was afterwards read over to said defendant, and was by him signed and sworn to, as the same appears below:

Private Examination of John H. Gill.

My name is John H. Gill. I am 35 years of age, born in the City of Baltimore, State of Maryland, and have resided in the City of New Orleans for the last 8 years. I am by trade a carpenter and joiner, and am now, and have been for the last 6 months, in the employ of Messrs. P. H. Gilmore & Co., Contractors and Builders, doing business at No. 160 Canal Street, in this City. I have a wife and 3 children, who reside with me at No. 47 Customhouse Street, in this City. On the 25th day of April last, I was at work for my employers, Gilmore & Co., on the Levee, near the head of Governor Nicholls Street, in this City, and about 11 o'clock A. M. of that day, I saw a man named John Murphy, who was a laborer in the employ of the same firm, Gilmore & Co., and who had been drinking, come up to where I was at work, and abuse me for some words that had passed between us a few days before. I told him to go away and let me alone, but he continued to abuse me, and finally struck me with his fist. I then struck him with a hammer which I had in my hand, and knocked him down. He got up and ran away, and I went on with my work. I did not see Murphy any more that day, and did not hear of his being hurt until the next morning, when I was told that he was dead. I then went to the office of the Coroner, and made a statement of the whole matter to him, and was by him sent to the Mayor's office, where I made the same statement to the Mayor, and was by him committed to prison. I had no knife or other weapon in my hand at the time I struck Murphy, and only struck him one blow with the hammer, and that was in self-defense, as he had struck me first. I am entirely ignorant of the cause of Murphy's death, and did not intend to kill him, and am very sorry that he is dead.

(Signed) John H. Gill

Sworn to and subscribed before me, in open court, this 29th day of August, A. D. 1866.

J. C. Monroe
Judge 2d Judicial District Court

Parish of Orleans.
Not Guilty.

2nd. Private Examination of James H. Murphy, a witness, taken by me, J. C. Monroe, Judge of the Second Judicial District Court of the Parish of Orleans, on this 6th day of August, A. D. 1866, between the hours of 10 o'clock A. M. and 4 o'clock P. M., at the Court House in the City of New Orleans, who, being duly sworn, deposed as follows:

My name is James H. Murphy. I am a brother of John Murphy, deceased, and reside in the City of New Orleans. I was present at the time my brother was struck by John H. Gill, on the 25th day of April last, on the Levee, near the head of Governor Nicholls Street. I saw Gill strike my brother with a hammer, and saw my brother fall to the ground. I raised him up, and took him to the office of Dr. J. D. Winston, near by, where he was attended to. My brother lived about 24 hours after he was struck, and died on the morning of the 26th of April. I do not know of any previous difficulty between Gill and my brother, and do not think that my brother struck Gill before Gill struck him. My brother had been drinking that morning, but was not drunk.

(Signed) James H. Murphy

Sworn to and subscribed before me, in open court, this 6th day of August, A. D. 1866.

J. C. Monroe
Judge 2d Judicial District Court

3rd. Private Examination of Dr. J. D. Winston, a witness, taken by me, J. C. Monroe, Judge of the Second Judicial District Court of the Parish of Orleans, on this 7th day of August, A. D. 1866, between the hours of 10 o'clock A. M. and 4 o'clock P. M., at the Court House in the City of New Orleans, who, being duly sworn, deposed as follows:

My name is J. D. Winston. I am a practicing physician, residing in the City of New Orleans. On the 25th day of April last, about 11 o'clock A. M., James H. Murphy brought his brother, John Murphy, to my office, near the head of Governor Nicholls Street, in a wounded condition. I examined the wound, which was a fracture of the skull, on the left side of the head, about 2 inches in length, and extending to the brain. I probed the wound, and found that the brain was injured. I dressed the wound, and did what I could for the patient, but he died on the next morning, about 11 o'clock. The cause of his death was the injury to the brain, caused by the blow on the head. The wound could have been inflicted by a blow from a hammer, or some heavy instrument.

(Signed) J. D. Winston, M. D.

Sworn to and subscribed before me, in open court, this 7th day of August, A. D. 1866.

J. C. Monroe
Judge 2d Judicial District Court

Parish of Orleans.
Not Guilty.

State of Louisiana,
Parish of Orleans.

Be it remembered, that on this 10th day of August, A. D. 1866, before me, J. C. Monroe, Judge of the Second Judicial District Court of the Parish of Orleans, personally came and appeared S. S. Herald, District Attorney for the Parish of Orleans, who, being duly sworn, did depose and say that he has no other or further evidence to offer in the above entitled cause, touching the guilt or innocence of the accused, John H. Gill, alias John Gill, and that he is satisfied with the evidence adduced, and has no objection to the discharge of the accused.

(Signed) S. S. Herald
District Attorney

Sworn to and subscribed before me, in open court, this 10th day of August, A. D. 1866.

J. C. Monroe
Judge 2d Judicial District Court

Whereupon, it is ordered by the Court, that the accused, John H. Gill, alias John Gill, be, and he is hereby, discharged from custody.

Thus done and passed in open court, in the City of New Orleans, on this 10th day of August, A. D. 1866.

J. C. Monroe
Judge 2d Judicial District Court
