Feb. 9th 1865

My dear Husband & 
Philadelphia has been 
very calm with me & 
I have had a letter 
from Michigan to day 
in which we learn 
that our friends there 
had not heard from 
thee, though it is 
thought they might 
have by this time.  
My anxiety on thy 
account is very 
great, but I am 
trying to keep up 
my courage, & have 
not yet given up 
hoping for thy safe 
return.  I am told 
that the time for 
which the soldiers 
were enlisted has 
expired, & that 
they are now free 
to come home.  I 
am very anxious to 
hear from thee.  I 
am afraid thou 
wilt not get my 
letters, & I do not 
know what to do.  
I feel so anxious 
about thee, & 
courage quite 
fails me.  I 
have been to 
see Dr. Parrish 
this morning, & he 
thinks I am doing 
very well.  I 
shall try to be 
patient, but it 
is very hard.  I 
have not heard 
from Cousin Mary 
since I wrote to 
her, but I hope 
she will write 
soon.  I am 
very lonely 
without thee.  
I remain thy 
affectionate wife  
Sarah W. Wistar
