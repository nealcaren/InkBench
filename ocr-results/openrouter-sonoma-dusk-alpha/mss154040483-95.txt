"Party Clean-ups Are
the Crying Need"

Says

Carrie Chapman Catt
Distinguished Suffrage Leader

WANTED: A political housecleaning from cellar to attic. It is not enough that the women of the nation have the ballot. They must use it to clean up politics. This is the message which Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt, president of the National American Woman Suffrage Association, is carrying to the women voters of the country. Mrs. Catt believes that the time has come for women to take an active part in politics and to use their influence to rid the country of corrupt practices. She is speaking before women's clubs and organizations throughout the nation, urging them to organize and to work for clean politics.

In a recent address before the Philadelphia League of Women Voters, Mrs. Catt said:

"The women of America have won the vote after a long and hard-fought battle. Now they must use it wisely. The crying need of the hour is a thorough clean-up of political methods. Corruption, bossism, and graft must be eliminated. Women, with their high ideals and their devotion to the home and family, are peculiarly fitted to lead this crusade for better government.

"Every woman who votes should inform herself on the issues and the candidates. She should not be swayed by personal friendships or by promises of favors. Her vote should be cast for the candidate who stands for clean and honest government.

"The time has come for women to take their place in the councils of the nation. They must organize, they must study the problems of government, and they must work unceasingly for the ideals which they hold dear.

"Let us make this the year of the great clean-up. Let us show the world that American women are equal to the task of purifying the stream of politics."

Mrs. Catt's words are being heeded by women throughout the country. Organizations are being formed, study classes are being conducted, and women are preparing to exercise their new-found power at the polls.

The distinguished suffrage leader is confident that women will rise to the occasion and that the result will be a better and cleaner government for all.

Apr. 18, 1921
Women's Citizen
Vol. 1 No. 4
