Principles of Letter Writing

The Elements of a Letter

A letter is a written conversation.  It should be so planned and written as to convey just what the writer wishes to convey to each reader.

The first thing to consider in writing a letter is the object of the letter.  What is the purpose of the letter?  Is it to convey information, to make a request, to give an order, to express sympathy, to congratulate, to decline an invitation, to accept an invitation, to ask a favor, to make an apology, to make a complaint, or to express thanks?  The purpose will determine the tone of the letter and the way in which it should be written.

The tone of a letter should be the same as that of a conversation.  The writer should write as he would speak if he were face to face with the person to whom the letter is addressed.

The letter should be written in the first person.  The writer should use "I" and "my" and "me" and "mine" and "we" and "our" and "us" and "ours" as he would in speaking.

The letter should be written in a simple, direct style.  The writer should use short sentences and short paragraphs.  He should avoid long words and long sentences.

The letter should be courteous and polite.  The writer should be careful to say "please" and "thank you" and to use the proper forms of address.

The letter should be neat and legible.  The writer should use good paper and a good pen and should write carefully.

The letter should be sincere and natural.  The writer should write as he feels and thinks and not as he thinks he ought to write.

The letter should be brief and to the point.  The writer should say what he has to say and then stop.

The letter should be interesting and attractive.  The writer should try to make his letter as interesting as possible.

The letter should be personal and friendly.  The writer should write as to a friend and not as to a stranger.

The letter should be complete and definite.  The writer should give all the information that is necessary and should be careful to answer all the questions that are asked.

The letter should be timely and prompt.  The writer should answer letters as soon as possible after receiving them.

The letter should be original and individual.  The writer should express his own thoughts and ideas and not copy from others.

The letter should be cheerful and optimistic.  The writer should try to make his letter as cheerful as possible.

The letter should be thoughtful and considerate.  The writer should think of the feelings of the person to whom the letter is written and should be careful not to say anything that will hurt his feelings.

The letter should be grammatical and spelled correctly.  The writer should be careful to use good grammar and to spell all words correctly.

The letter should be punctuated correctly.  The writer should be careful to use the proper marks of punctuation.

The letter should be paragraphed correctly.  The writer should begin a new paragraph whenever the subject changes.

The letter should be arranged correctly.  The writer should follow the rules for the arrangement of a letter.

The letter should be folded correctly.  The writer should fold the letter so that it will fit into the envelope properly.

The letter should be placed in the envelope correctly.  The writer should place the letter in the envelope so that the first page will be on top when the envelope is opened.

The envelope should be addressed correctly.  The writer should write the address of the person to whom the letter is sent in the center of the envelope.

The envelope should be stamped correctly.  The writer should put the proper amount of postage on the envelope.

The envelope should be mailed promptly.  The writer should mail the letter as soon as it is written.
