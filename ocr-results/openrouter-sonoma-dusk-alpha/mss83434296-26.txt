First Steps.  The first step was to provide the clothing on hand at Raleigh, in cold weather more than sufficient for the equipment of the troops on pay rolls.  As stated before, the Asst. Qr. Mr. Genl. was instructed to do this, and report to this office the articles issued, and the balance remaining on hand.

Last.  The last step was to manufacture the articles wanted, many of which had been on hand, marked "C.S.A." which were turned over to the Clothing Bureau.  The contract for this was made with Maj. W. H. Foard, in the Spring of 1862, and the manufactory continued until the end of the year.  The cost of every article of clothing has by this been increased at least 100 per cent.  more than double the prices they were in 1861.
