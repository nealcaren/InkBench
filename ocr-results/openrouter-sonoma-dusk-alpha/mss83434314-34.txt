Fort by Special Order No. 31, [Department of?]
Surgeon General's Office
Richmond, Va. April 17, 1863.

Surgeon R. C. M. Page, Medical Director, A. N. V.

Sir:--Captain Thos. H. Williams, A. Q. M., not being
present to make the proper endorsement on the enclosed
papers, and the Department wishing to give the officer making the
application every assistance in his power, I have endorsed the
papers as per copy of endorsement.  A new copy is now sent
for your signature, being a simple request
to forward the papers to this office when signed.  The
papers are now with the Adjutant General, but will be returned
to you in a day or two.

With regard to the request for a detail of a man
as nurse, you will please communicate with Lt. Col. Smith,
Chief Q. M., and ascertain if it can be granted.  On
this subject, however, should he be unable
to grant the request, before advising the
Surgeon, please inform me of the fact.

2.  It is supposed that the endorsement made was a sufficient
authority to obtain the detail, but if not, the
detail may be made and forwarded to this office for
certificate.

3.  It is desirable that all cases of this character
should be made as far as practicable, to conform to the
general regulations.  The endorsement made on this
application was intended to cover the
case, without giving general instructions which might be
misconstrued.

4.  Certificates of disability for the enlisted men ordering the
discharge will be made out in the usual form, and
sent to this office.  In all other cases
of discharge for disability, which are not ordered by this office, a
certificate will be given by the medical examining board, or
Surgeon in charge, of the disability, with a brief description
of the cause, and the cause of discharge, and forwarded
to this office for the "Discharge" form.
