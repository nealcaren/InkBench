I.

The Editor of the National Intelligencer, Washington, June 18, 1836.

Sir: - In preparing, for the War Department, a report of the operations of the Florida war, for the past year, I find among the papers on the subject, a communication from the Rev. Mr. Hutchins, dated 30th April last, and addressed to the Secretary of War, in which he states that, from the best information he could collect, there were in the peninsula of Florida, at the commencement of the war, about five thousand adult Indians; that of these, about two hundred and fifty had been killed and died of disease; about one hundred and fifty had been taken prisoners, and about one hundred had escaped into the territory of the Choctaws; leaving about four thousand six hundred and fifty still in arms against the United States.  He further states, that in his opinion, it will require about one million of dollars, to prosecute the war to a successful termination.

II.

In relation to the first part of the communication of the Rev. Mr. Hutchins, I have to remark, that from the best information in my possession, his estimate of the number of adult Indians in Florida, at the commencement of the war, is not far from correct; but in regard to the number killed, taken prisoners, or who have escaped, I have no data by which to form an accurate opinion.  It is believed, however, that the number killed and taken prisoners, does not much exceed five hundred; and that the number who have escaped into the nation, is less than one hundred.

III.

In relation to the probable expense of terminating the war, I have to remark, that it will depend much upon the season of the year in which the final operations are undertaken.  If commenced in the dry season, and vigorously prosecuted, it is believed that the Indians can be subdued at an expense not exceeding five hundred thousand dollars; but if deferred until the commencement of the rainy season, the expense will be greatly increased, and the difficulties greatly augmented.

IV.

I have the honor to be, very respectfully, your obdt. servt.

Approved,

W. P. MANGUM, Chairman.

O. H. BURROUGHS, Secy.
