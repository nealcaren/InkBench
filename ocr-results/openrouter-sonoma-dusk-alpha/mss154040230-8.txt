Bureau of Palestinian Affairs
January 14, 1982

[Handwritten: Argentine]

[Stamp: "Argentine"]

Mrs. Carrie Gutt
c/o Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt

My dear Mrs. Catt:

Relations, I had a conversation with Senor Don Roberto Puiggrós, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Province, in which he told me that he would not be able to have an official dialogue, the Argentine government could not be involved in this matter officially, and that the best way to handle the matter would be through the Department of State of the United States.  This is clearly understood by me.

Delivered to the Department of State of the United States, and to the note of the United States, Mr. Francis White, in your excellent letter I must draw your attention to the fact that the matter is of a private organization and not official, in that it has to do with the broader relations between the citizens of the United States and the Palestinian Arabs.

It will be necessary to have an interview with the Minister, and in our last conversation, indicating that he had known much about the matter, he was of the opinion that the only thing that could be done would be through diplomatic channels, "that is to say, to keep it quiet". - Hence

[Handwritten on side: "Minister of Foreign Affairs"]
