I have been a long while
answering thy very kind letter of the 2nd inst. 
The delay has been occasioned by a press of
business & by the fact that I have been 
rather unwell for a week or more.  I am 
now much better, & am glad to say that 
my husband is also improving.  He has been 
afflicted with a severe cold, which has 
brought on a return of his old trouble 
in his throat, & he has been quite sick 
for several days.  I hope, however, that 
he is now convalescing.  We are all 
much obliged to thee for thy kind 
inquiries about us, & for thy sympathy 
in our afflictions.  We have had a good 
deal of sickness in our family this winter, 
but I trust we are now getting through 
with it.  Our little boy has been quite 
sick with a cold & cough, but is now 
better.  The baby is well, & a great 
comfort to us.  She is a very fine child, 
& I am very proud of her.  She is 
now six months old, & is beginning to 
take notice.  She is very fond of her 
father, & he is very much attached to her. 

As to this quarter, we are all 
anxious to hear from thee, & to know 
how thee art getting on.  I hope 
thee'll soon be able to tell us that 
thee art quite well again.  We are 
all well here, except as I have told 
thee.  The weather has been very 
severe, & the cold has been intense. 
The snow is deep, & the roads are 
almost impassable.  We have had 
a good deal of snow this winter, 
& the sleighing has been excellent. 
The children have enjoyed it very 
much.  They are all well, & are 
growing fast.  The oldest boy is 
now quite a large boy, & is very 
helpful about the place.  He is 
a great comfort to me, & I am 
very proud of him.  The girls 
are all well, & are growing up 
fast.  They are all very fond of 
their little sister, & are very 
attentive to her. 

I must now close, as I have 
a good deal to do.  I hope 
thee'll soon write again, & give 
us all the news.  With much 
love to thee & thine, I am 
Thy affectionate friend 
Mary B. Howland
