a little while in Washington, on
my way home.  Give my love
to Susan, & tell her of Iowa
has not worn out my affection
for her, although it has been so
long since I have heard from
her.  I have had so many guests at
home, that of course I have
not written to any of them.
I thought that I would see
thee before I returned
home, but that pleasure is
denied me.  I hope that thou
wilt come to Washington
to see me, if thou canst
possibly do so.  I shall
be here but a short time.

I wish that I could take
thee with me.  I am
going to make inquiries
about thee in every place
I visit, so if thou hast any
friends there, tell me of
them.  I shall write to
thee from every place
where I can get writing
materials.  I hope thou wilt
write as often as possible to
me.  Good bye.

Washington, 19th of 3 mo. 1851
