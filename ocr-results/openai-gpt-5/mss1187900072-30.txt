Washington April 10th 1887
Dear Father

Your letter dated March 24th I received yesterday; it found us all well, and pleased to hear from you.
Mr. Trotter, the new Recorder of Deeds is seriously sick with pneumonia. Gen'l S. D[????] has been appointed Deputy Recorder of Deeds. The lawyers are much put-out about it. Senator Sherman is making a trip South; in Georgia a colored delegation called at his hotel and was refused admission as soon as he heard of it; he paid his bill and went to another hotel. A colored man by the name of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed to the Liberian Mission. The political outlook at present, on the Republican side is wavering between Blaine and Sherman. Congress has made an appropriation for a new Bridge across the Eastern Branch at the foot of Penn'a Ave. Business in the Recorder's Office is booming. G[????], the President of the Anacostia R.R. is now [?]. B[????] has been appointed "temp[????] of Deeds." The President and Mrs. Mathews have been taken back in the [????] Confirmation of Mr. Trotter. They expected he would be rejected; Law[????]
