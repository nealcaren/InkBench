Strength of the Army increased and decreased as crises arose and passed. The Whiskey Rebellion and the threat of war with France and England led to expansion. Economy and latent fear of military power just as often led to reductions.

During President Jefferson’s administration military and naval strength declined, even though the need for troops actually increased when the United States purchased Louisiana. However, in 1802 Jefferson did carry out Washington’s recommendation for establishing a military academy.
