May 24 1923

CARRIE CHAPMAN CATT
404 RIVERSIDE DRIVE
NEW YORK

Dear Mary:

Received a book yesterday—the 24th
gift this spring! If this book tells how to attain
success with a boat when the author (would be)
has neither excuse nor pep and no vocabulary
to express the queer thought he has, it will be most
welcome.  Until I have read it I shall not know
whether to return it or to keep it.  I am sending you
without thanks such a gift.  This is no gift.  It is
a tiny box of life.  I haven't the remotest idea
what any of these are, but I'm told you given you
what you more nearly should from the greenhouse.
If you are fond of plants from my garden or
a gardenful of peas, I shall send you a few of them.
They may not have
all fished out their little blossoms.  When you
give me a little thought I shall take you to see a garden
that is worth looking at.
