I’m disappointed about not
seeing Phel. this month.
I’ve looked forward to
it all winter; but Phel’s
departure to here this
month made the matter
so complicated, & I was
not able to get done before
he left for the frontier.
I’m sorry I can’t do so.
Perhaps we can come in the
fall of next year, if all
then whenever agreeable
to your dear self.
I thank you for your good
wishes so much!
I met Pendelle Phillips
yesterday - and had
quite a little chat with
him; he asked after you;
I thank he’s gone
from the frontier.  I saw
Mr. James Redpath, for
a few hours, 15 in the street.
I was with Tralier; he
was quite jolly - and looks
very well since; he came
from Europe, more flesh
and more color.  I sent
from the transcript:
“Popular Lectures” - I suppose
written by himself - it may
be of interest to you, also
a piece taken from the
Advertiser, about Tralier’s
pictures.  They are having
great attention from there.
Mr. Isaac wrote Tralier
a very flattering letter, en:
a fancy card & address in;
also printed circulars
describing the pictures
also two papers with fine
notices from the Danforth
Daily and the Daily Post.
