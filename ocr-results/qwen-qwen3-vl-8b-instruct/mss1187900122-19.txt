London Sunday May 6, 94.

Dear Mr. Douglass;

Your letter directed to Manchester has followed me all round
till it reached me here.  I am indeed glad to get it.  When I wrote you
I had not seen your letter to Mrs. R. B. K. ; since seeing it I know
from what he and others said, that while they did not expect
"guah" (may they pay you the same
compliment I do, in knowing you
to be incapable of such a thing)
still they would have been
better satisfied if you had
spoken more positively regarding
me and my work.  M. White thought
that since the Society had failed
so miserably and I was thus left
to vouch for myself, you would
not hesitate to vouch for me.
We cannot go thru the country
telling people about this personal
al quanel and why I am un-
accompained by the persons
who brought me over.  At the
deat known member of the
race, a positive letter or vouch.
