Massachusetts Woman Suffrage Association,
3 Park Street,

Boston, July 21, 1889.

Dear Frederick Douglass,

When we first saw your appointment to Hoyt, and also has many kind and beautiful things the newspapers were saying about you, we began to clip and stick them all in with the intention of sending them all in to you — I cut "bits" of them, but alas! in the huge piles of editorial scraps they here all disappeared but three I enclose.

It was pleasant to see the appreciation in which you are held, while you are alive to see it.

I am not sure that we are glad to hear you are here.  We need you here.
