Buc., 24 sept. 1922

Dear Mrs. Chapman, Catt:

We have in our programme of this year
to read lectures on “Famous Women.” Mrs. Reus Ianculescu and
I will do it, she in the L.D.D.F. and I in popular evening course.
But as there are no complete libraries in Romania and not
a single feminist library, it is impossible for us even to know
the titles of the books, necessary for preparation. We address therefore
to you, ask to the other sections of I.W.S.A. to ask your help.

Feeling so much the lips of a poor library we will
try at one time. With our studies, to found one at B. and
perhaps than will we be able to assemble round it the
Women in a club a, we are dreaming of since a long time.
Would your association be able for the generous act of honor
to help with sisters and to send us one of the other standard works
on women movement in your country and specially
books, there we could find biographies of your famous women.
We would speak in a first series of lectures, on political and social
prominent Women and in a second draw the portraits of some
mad and science. But we know so little, it is to say not much
