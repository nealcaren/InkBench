New York, Feb 21st 1879

My Dear Mrs. Drekinson—

I am afraid your business affairs are again suffering.  I’m not the party called upon to come much, but “Can’t Keep a Hotel.”  I am sorry, I think, I surprised you—though I’m not sure.  It might have been worse in a different position—

You told me you would do me at your theatrical bureau, the next I hear you have “a boy” (as it turns) dotting with your affairs & “Mixing those Chidren Up.”  I thought you had a clear experience before with “Anatomical Managers” that had cleared by experience the value of management.  Don’t think I am sending you only that I feel slighted.  I came as soon for you than any man in this business—or out of it—for that matter—(or vice) I have done it cheerfully.
