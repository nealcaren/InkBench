I will thus to acquire necessary experience, to be
believed by those I would help, that I fully know
their trouble & could not but make common cause
with them - If so it is but a sleeping indignation!
And now let me add that on Easter eve I
was sought by the Director of Kewe Institute
(greeted in annexed paper - to tell me he had
proof that "Philanthropic money" as he called it could
be found in the North (in fact had been offered)
at low interest for such such words as I had hinted
& to which he added The suggestion mentioned
and attracted my attention to Part 4, Page 1 of the
Chicago Daily Intercourse of 25 March, giving an account
of a successful colored colonization at Tuskegee,
Alabama - to which I refer you as one to interest
you - It as a number of 6 & pages printed at
immense a° of copies, as its 21 anniversary, which
contains very interesting items about the Columbian
Fair.  When upon this I heard of your work
I thought I could not but write you if only to
ask your opinion & counsel how best to proceed
I feel sure of your sympathy for the cause -
perhaps for "cold lang sync" to remain, hopefully
& with very best wishes of the season for your
continued prosperity, your truly yours

F. S. GUTTENBACH TRUSTEES
