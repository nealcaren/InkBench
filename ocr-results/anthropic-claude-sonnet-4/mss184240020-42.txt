1710 Locust St. Philadelphia
March 21. 1871

My dear Sister,
                I write, first of all,
about Redmond, - because after a long
talk we held about himself last night,
that takes the first place. I read thy
little note to him from Dimthary in
which, while thee hopes to find him
better, thee says "I fear me - ". There has
been nothing startling new with him, only
there seems to be loss of him from week
to week, and his energy and ability to
go or do anything grow less continually.
Last night, after one had gone to bed,
he spoke for the first time. He says
he finds his digestion rather better, but
his stomach lessened so that he can
no longer take much of the remedies
