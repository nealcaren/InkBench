1862 June 17th Evacuation of Cumberland Gap. Tenn The federals
under Genl Morgan advanced toward this place for the purpose
of expelling the rebel Genl Stevenson & found who occupied
the gap with 13000 men and well fortified the same. The
gap is a shelf in the Cumberland mountains, and is so rup-
tured and narrow that there is through the gap for only a single
roadway. It was expected that the rebels would defend
the possession with the utmost tenacity. No such result
followed. When the rebels were informed of the approach
of the federals, they precipitately evacuated the place.
