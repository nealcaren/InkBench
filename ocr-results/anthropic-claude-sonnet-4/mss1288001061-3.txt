And am glad to feel that they have brought thee nearer than they have taken away. I have lived over and beyond greater pain indeed, and am clothed to find myself beloved and useful for a time & share.

I think thee would be sorry for Mrs Inglese were you to see her now; her body tired, her mental powers are sadly impaired. She sees of her life overtaken at times; for she is in the border land between sanity and insanity, and cannot tell the left & other hands than mine. She is quite an aged woman - seventy-five years old.

And yes sad as it is, it is in some way easier than the relations between us! used to be - she has no power any more to sport her life, and believes it comparatively easier, because of the feebleness of her present condition.

The youngest brother, an able fellow of judicial German, never very robust, having a physician, by birth a brilliant professional future before him; when owing to mental strain and overwork his health gave way; he fell a victim to that dreadful disease locomotor ataxy.

We cannot live in New York; the last winter we were in Madison square was a very trying one in regard to weather, and a very sad one to us, as we were feeling keenly our recent bereavement - for his sudden death of my eldest brother's sweet young wife, who had endeared herself to all our hearts in quite a remarkable manner.
