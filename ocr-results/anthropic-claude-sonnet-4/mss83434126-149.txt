The federals were commanded by Genl Curtis & Sigel. the battle was fought for three days without any decisive result, but on the 8th Genl Curtis made additional charges, and at early morn he attacked the rebel along his whole line, the rebels were finally thrown into confusion and fled precipitately on all the attack. The divisions of Van Dorn retreated in one direction that of Price escaped by another. The pursuit was continued by Genl Sigel toward Huntsville, and by the Cavalry & the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete. Killed in 3000 killed and wounded about 1000 prisoners, among the rebel dead was found Genl McIntosh, a formidable fighter in this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians under the command of Albert Pike, their savage conduct was so monstrous that after the battle the federal chief and wounded were found to be scalped. Thus removing this principal enemy sanguinary slaughter. Eleven live 300 killed and 900 Wounded
