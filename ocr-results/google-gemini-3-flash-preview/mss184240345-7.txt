Goshen N.Y.
5. 17. 1891

Dear Mr. Hart,
A thousand
thanks for keeping me all
ready to go to Phyto. — That
is what I desire. — When?
I fully appreciate how
great a service it will
be to take so much
time at present. But
it is almost like a matter
of life & death to me.
I also greatly desire you
may be able to find the

[*cross-writing*]
As it is not my purpose to
come to N.Y. until from
some word you may
send me that you are
ready to come with me.
I mean for you to go
with me to Pittston
then so know what
you go to do. I will in
every case pay Mr. Driver
for his time as well as
yours. You will find him
reasonable I think.
When will you know
anything altogether [?]
and definite. No telegraphing
about this yet. I am
going to live & die for
right if it takes every
cent I am worth.
