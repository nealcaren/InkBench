New York, Feb 21st 1879

My Dear Miss Dickinson
I am afraid your business
affairs are again suffering. You have
not the party calculated to carry
much weight. Nice a man as Rice is
but "can't keep a Hotel" I am sorry, I
think had I engineered your "bus"
with John McCullough it might have
been now in a different position—
You told me you would see me about
your Theatrical business & the next I hear
you have "a Boy" (as it were) doting
with your affairs & "mixing those children
up" I thought you had a dear experience
before with "Amateur managers" & had
learned by experience the value of management.
Don't think I am scolding you only that I
feel slighted. I could do more for you than any
man in this business or out of it for that
matter & would (or will) have done it cheerfully
