And now let me add that on Easter eve I 
was sought by the Mr Travers Director of Howe Institute 
mentioned in annexed paper - to tell me he had 
proof that "Philanthropic money" as he called it could 
be found in the North (in fact had been offered) 
at low interest for such such work as I had hinted 
& to which he added the suggestion mentioned 
and attracted my attention to Part 4 Page 1 of the 
Daily Inter-Ocean Chicago of 25 March no. giving an account 
of a successful colored Colonization at Tuskegee 
Alabama - to which I refer you also, as sure to interest 
you [-] It is a number of 62 pages printed at 
immense no. of copies as its 21 anniversary & which 
contains very interesting items about the Columbian 
Fair.  When upon this I heard of your work 
I thought I could not but write you if only to 
ask your opinion of my sketch & counsel how best to proceed 
I feel sure of your sympathy for the cause & 
perhaps "for auld lang syne" so remain, hopefully 
& with very best wishes of the season for your 
continued prosperity, dear Sir
most truly yours
F. G. Guthrie d'Arusmont [*X*]
