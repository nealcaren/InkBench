“Party Clean-ups Are the Crying Need,”

Says

Carrie Chapman Catt

Distinguished Suffrage Leader

WANTED — some plan that has happened in some way to work out the salvation of the two-party system of the United States, which is now in such a deplorable state that many of its wisest friends fear for its life. It is unexampled in the race of nations. It was the devotion to political parties which brought the United States into being and held it together for one hundred and fifty years, but now it is in a state of anæmia from which it may die.

It is understood that the holding of elections is the only method by which the ills of the body politic can be cured. But the difficulty is that the doctor comes with a new remedy which he wishes to apply to a patient already over-dosed with remedies, each of a different brand. Just now the single taxer, the direct nominator, the recallist, and the initiative and referendumists are all preparing to apply their several specifics which will cure the ills of the patient without any thought as to their effect upon an already over-treated political system.

There is one thing in which all these doctors agree, and that is that the two parties must be kept alive. It is in this particular that they differ from all other proposed remedies. The question is, how can these parties be saved when all the best elements in them are so disgusted with the manner in which they work that they are leaving them in droves?

The Republicans have just had their convention and the Democrats are now in session. Both parties have taken up excellent platforms and have nominated admirable candidates. But the rank and file of the Republicans are so disappointed with their convention that they are not going to work for their party, and the Democrats are even more discouraged than the Republicans.

The trouble with both parties is the same. They are both so badly boss-ridden that the people do not believe that they will ever have anything to say about either of them. The Republican party in its convention was a disgrace to the intelligence of a great nation. It was a machine convention from start to finish. The bosses were in control and the people had no voice. The platform was dictated by the bosses and the candidate was nominated by the bosses. The people had nothing to do with it.

The only way to save the parties is to clean them up. The people must take them over and run them for the benefit of the people. The bosses must be driven out and the people must be put in. The women of the country, who now have the vote, must take a hand in this work. They must organize and work for the purification of the parties.

Mrs. Catt's Views

Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt, in an address before the Women's Republican Club of Philadelphia, said in part:

“The two-party system is the foundation of our government. Without it we cannot have stable government. But both parties are now so corrupt that they are not fit to be trusted with the government of the country.

“The Republican party is controlled by a few bosses who dictate to the convention what it shall do. The Democratic party is in the same condition. The people have no voice in either party.

“The time has come when the women must take charge of the parties and clean them up. We have the vote and we must use it to drive out the corrupt elements and put honest men and women in control.

“The initiative and referendum and other reforms will not save the parties. They must be saved from within by the people themselves.

“I urge the women of America to organize for this purpose. Let us have clean parties and then we can have good government.”

Mrs. Catt is one of the most distinguished women in America. She was the leader of the suffrage movement and is now devoting her time to the cause of good government. Her words carry great weight with the women of the country.
