12th mo. 6 1873

My dear Maggie:

I suppose you despair of
hearing from me again
but I am well, and not such an
idler as you suppose. Things have
gone so smoothly with me, that I have not been
sure of any thing to say to you;
but now I have something to impart,
which I hope will be good
news to you. After very long waiting,
from the above delay stage when you think
you are met to fail, try to pick up your courage
and you'll come through, and be glad
you are better because you must be able
I expected thee might send a note here
through the mail but I find in many mails
