Prospectus

Feb 7, 185

To Subscribers for the Life and Public Services of Thaddeus Stevens,

in the preparation of which the author has been engaged for several years, and which he trusts will prove a labor of love alike to his own heart and to the great body of his fellow-citizens, the following Prospectus is respectfully submitted, and he begs to be permitted to say a few words in explanation of the character and design of the work.

It is designed to comprise a full and impartial history of the public life and services of Thaddeus Stevens, the acknowledged leader of the Republican party in Congress, the most efficient champion of liberty and the rights of man upon this continent. By birth, education, and talents, destined to exert a commanding influence upon the destinies of the Republic, his career has been a bright example of patriotic devotion to the principles of civil liberty, and of lofty self-sacrifice in their defense. In the darkest hour of the nation's trial, when the foundations of the government were assailed, and the ark of our liberties seemed about to be overthrown, he stood forth the bold defender of the rights of man, and the fearless advocate of those measures which have preserved the Union and secured the blessings of freedom to the oppressed.

The preparation of this work has been attended with great labor and research. The author has spared no pains to collect the materials from all accessible sources, public and private, and to arrange them in such a manner as to present a faithful narrative of the life and public services of his subject. He has availed himself of the public records and documents, of private correspondence and papers, and of the oral testimony of those who were associated with Mr. Stevens in his long public career.

To other sources he has had access, and the library of the Historical Society of Pennsylvania has been freely opened to him for the purpose.

Believing that the life of Thaddeus Stevens will form one of the most important chapters in the history of the United States during the last half century, the author has spared no effort to make the biography worthy of its theme, and of the age in which we live.

The work will be published in one large octavo volume of about six hundred pages, printed on fine paper, from new stereotype plates, with a portrait of the subject engraved in the best style of the art.

The mechanical execution will be superior, and the publishers pledge themselves that the volume shall be a credit to American book-making.

The price to subscribers will be Two Dollars and Fifty Cents per copy, for which payment will be required on the delivery of the book.

The edition will be limited to two thousand copies, and no more will be printed from the plates unless the demand should require it.

Subscriptions will be received by the author, and by booksellers throughout the country.

Terms

The work will be stereotyped. A. D. Bancroft, Publisher. Terms $2.50.

John Wike
