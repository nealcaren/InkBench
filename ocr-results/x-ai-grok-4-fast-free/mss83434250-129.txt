All attempts to overthrow government should be punished. Of the many atrocious misdeeds which are prohibited by the laws, the crime of high treason is the most enormous, and consequently is by law punished by the extremest pains and penalties which the legislature can inflict. It has been the practice to affix to it the punishment of death, often aggravated by fines, confiscation, corruption of blood, and forfeiture of lands and goods; and those punishments are so described, that rather than the punishment of death, but the punishment of all, which depends on it, of which the deprivation of the criminal of the means of doing harm.
