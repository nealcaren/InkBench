Philadelphia 3 mo. 15th 1850

My dear Eliza -

If thou couldst not be at our Yearly Meeting, we missed thee much; & our friends & then E. B. enquired for thee.  On arriving in Philadelphia in the last steamboat he had [been] in Philadelphia for that end & by advice of friends of which she was a near & valued member.  Yearning with hopes of an happy issue of the concerns which engross thy own attention, & which engross thy offspring when they are in our memory of old times, which is always uppermost when anything of interest occurs about thy house.  Than which thou cousin art not there to attend to it I feel that my loquacious tongue hath increased of late - excuse any offense on my part.  Believe me affectionately thy sincere friend.  S. J. from thy servant
