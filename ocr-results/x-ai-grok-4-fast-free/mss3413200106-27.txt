15 6th mo. 1852

Dear Miss Blackwel.

I am sorry to trouble thee with any thing -
such as might trouble thee, in this season
of gladness. Yet I wish thee to know
that we are well; as well
as can be expected. I have
been wishing to see
thee, - if thee can find time
to come out - but not say
any thing about it to any one
unless thee thinks it best. We may
go to White Sulphur Springs, as
our Physician has advised.
and she is trying to do
what she can. Just so.
I regard her calling for Bark
Jack as really but thee who distrusted
