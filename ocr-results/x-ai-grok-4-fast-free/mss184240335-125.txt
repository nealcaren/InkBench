From the American Consul at  
London, of which the government  
have sent to the Senate, with  
his message of Decr 3d.  
Several Letters from the Secretary  
of State to our Ministers at Paris  
and London, respecting the affairs  
of the French Republick, and the  
instructions which have been given  
to our Envoys, were yesterday  
laid before the House of  
Representatives; and a report  
from the Committee of Privileges,  
to whom was referred that part  
of the President's speech, which  
relates to the infractions of our  
commercial intercourse with the  
French Republick, was read, and  
considered to be in order for  
to-morrow.  

In the Senate, a motion was  
made by Mr. Burr, to expunge  
from their Journals the resolution  
of secrecy, under which the  
treaty with Great Britain was  
considered the last session. This  
occasioned a warm and long debate,  
in which the merits of the  
treaty were again brought under  
consideration. It was finally  
postponed for the present.
