National American Woman Suffrage Association  
17 W. 36th St. New York  

April 8th 1900  

Dear Mr. Winfield,  

I pondered over your letter & cannot  
see my part in it. If I would go to Portland I would feel  
the pulse of things there a little more than I do at  
present, & of course there would be the work to do. If I did  
not feel that these conferences would do the work, & if I  
didn't think my going would hurt the work more than  
help it, I would go without any persuasion, but I cannot  
go to Portland without feeling that work for the  
district would suffer.  

Itself & the reports in the newspapers are  
the only information I have about your state of affairs &  
we are greatly handicapped by not having more to tell us  
about it.  

Send line to me very soon & let me know if it  
is true that you are in error not a crisis. If it be a crisis  
it might be well for you to hear of anything that gives you  
hope. For if it is a crisis it is a very serious one for you  
know if I go I must let the question be decided by those  
who know more about it than I do, but if you can  
assure me that it is all right I will gladly go.  

Yours sincerely,  
Carrie Chapman Catt
