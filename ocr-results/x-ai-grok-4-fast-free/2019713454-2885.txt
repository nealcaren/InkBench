The Mother's Library

Editor's Portfolio

We mothers know what it is to have our young children cry for the pictures they see in books, & what efforts we make to get them books with pretty pictures, but now suppose you buy the book for your child, & it has only the pretty pictures & no story, or worse the pictures have nothing to do with the story, how disappointed the child will be, & then in after years if the same child wants to write a story & illustrates it herself how anxious she will be that her pictures should relate to the subject. We think she will be sure to give us something worth reading.

Will our little authors help us? With their "Baby" & "Young" & "Juvenile" &c &c essays only doing well what we know they can do - make pictures & stories that belong to each other.

For this we want essays for a Mother's Library. Only seven doing what we know they can - send in your essays with outline sketches or full page pictures as you please. For a consolation you are not to see the prize you may win. If a conscientious artist you must not ask to know where your picture is to go - you must give yourself up to the inspiration of the moment & "tell your tale."
