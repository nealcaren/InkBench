Filed March 6, 1899

SUPPLEMENT A TO RULE

UNITED STATES CIRCUIT COURT
SOUTHERN DISTRICT OF NEW YORK

Passed on the 9th day of February, 1899,

In the matter of the Circuit Court for the Southern District of New York.

At a General Term of said Court, held in and for the Southern District of New York,

At the United States Court House, in the City, County and Southern District of New York, on the 9th day of February in the year one thousand eight hundred and ninety-nine.

Present.  Hon. Lacius D. lacombe,
                      William Lacombe,
                      Circuit Judges.

A motion having been made by Edward B. Thomas, Esq., attorney for the New York Law Journal, for an order amending or adding to Rule 31 of this Court, so as to permit the publication and sale of the decisions of this Court in a separate series under the title "The United States Circuit Court Reports, Southern District of New York," and the said motion having been argued and duly submitted to the Court,

Now, therefore, it is

Ordered, that Rule 31 of this Court be, and the same hereby is amended by adding thereto the following:

"Appendix A.

It is ordered that Edward B. Thomas, John C. Thomson and Lacius D. Bingham, Esqs., are hereby appointed commissioners to receive, compile, edit and prepare for publication such decisions of this Court in the Southern District of New York, as may in their judgment be best adapted for such publication in a series of volumes to be known as "The United States Circuit Court Reports, Southern District of New York," which said volumes shall be published by Edward B. Thomas and John C. Thomson, and that the publication thereof be under their supervision.

The said Commissioners shall have power to designate what decisions shall be reported, and to select, or cause to be selected, such head notes and statements of fact as they may deem proper, and all decisions selected for publication shall be reported in full.

The publication of any decision of this Court in any other publication shall not preclude its subsequent publication in the said "United States Circuit Court Reports, Southern District of New York," and the publication of any decision in the said Reports shall not be deemed a bar to its publication in any other newspaper or report, authorized by the Court, or required by law to publish the same.

The said Commissioners shall receive for their services such compensation as shall be fixed by the Judges of this Court, and the said Edward B. Thomas and John C. Thomson shall pay to the said Commissioners, out of the proceeds of the said publication, such compensation as may be so fixed.

The said "United States Circuit Court Reports, Southern District of New York," when published, shall be received in evidence in all the Courts of this State, and of the United States, as prima facie evidence of the decisions of this Court therein reported.

It is further ordered that a copy of this order, duly certified, be filed and recorded in the office of the Clerk of this Court, and that the same be printed in the said Reports.

Dated New York. February 9th, 1899."
