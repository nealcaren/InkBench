Gordon Parks
Born in Fort Scott, Kansas November 30, 1912 Gordon Parks has had a brilliant career in photography, writing, motion pictures and music.
In 1942, he was the only Black member of the Farm Security Administration photographic staff, where he worked with Roy Stryker.
Later he founded the Standard Oil Company newsletter, a forerunner of today's Ebony. While at Life, in 1948, his photo essay series on Harlem Gang Leader appeared.
Articles in a series of Life's, including the famous story on the Redneck Sharecroppers led by the then unknown George corfield.
Parks wrote scripts and scores for a number of documentaries. His first feature film "The Learning Tree" was published as a novel in 1963. It was made into a film in 1969.
He has three children, three grandchildren and one great grandchild.
