Attorney General.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your letter of the 7th inst., in relation to the proposed appointment of the Regents for the colored university in the District of Columbia.

The question presented to you was, whether the appointment should be made by the President, or by the Attorney General.

The act of Congress of July 23, 1866, confers upon the President authority to appoint the Regents, with the advice and consent of the Senate.

Hon. E. M. Stanton,

Secretary of War.

Sir:

The matter of Bureau Refugees, &c., in the District of Columbia, has been brought to my attention.

It has been suggested to the Attorney General, and through him to the President, that a person be appointed Inspector General for the Freedmen's Bureau, whose duty it shall be to visit the various branches of the Bureau, see that the work is properly done, and report to the Commissioner.

I think this suggestion of the Attorney General is a good one, and I would respectfully ask that you will lay it before the President.

I have the honor to be,

Very respectfully,

Your obedient servant,

O. O. Howard,

Commissioner.

P. S. I have made some inquiries as to the person who could fill this position, and find that Col. E. D. Wells, late of the 2d Mass. Cavalry, would be a suitable person.
