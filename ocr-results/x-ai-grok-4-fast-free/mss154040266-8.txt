Mary Gray

February 7th, 1911

My dear Rosa:

Of each there has been an offering of ballots in the state legislatures of Kansas, Nebraska, Illinois and Washington. In Washington, where we lost it last year, there is every indication of success. But in the other three states I fear it will be lost. 

In Oklahoma there will be no suffrage amendment submitted this year, and in the other states west of the Mississippi submissions are postponed to the next two year periods so great pleasure will be taken in them when they do come.

I have just returned from a month's stay in Colorado, where I saw the law in operation, and I must say that it is working smoothly and better than some of the best suffragist thought possible. The prejudice against the women in the courts is almost wholly gone. The places where they sit on the juries have been furnished especially for them in the court houses, and this has more than anything else done away with the old prejudice. In the mining camps and where they have been thought to be more questionable, I found the places fitted up and the women serving there as well as in the best towns. I am more than pleased, I am enthusiastic over the success that Colorado has made of her experiment.

I wish you would go to Belgium in April, as I know you will like to see the work there. The International Suffrage News, which is to be issued on the 1st of March, will have full particulars of the date and place of the Congress.

Yours for equality

Carrie Chapman Catt
