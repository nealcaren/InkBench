Grant's arrival at Cold Harbor on June 3, however, Early's
position became unsafe, and he decided to withdraw. On the night
of June 12 his army marched on the road toward the Pamunkey
River. At 2 o'clock on the morning of June 13, Mahone's division
of A. P. Hill's corps crossed the bridge over the Pamunkey.
The Federal cavalry on the south side of the Pamunkey
were defeated and driven away by the Confederate advance.
The infantry division of Heth followed Mahone's.
The 15th of June by a night march, Longstreet's corps joined
the army on the march. The column then consisted of 59,000
men, exclusive of the sick and wounded. The wagon trains
were sent by the Vila road to Ashland, while the main
army marched by the direct White House road to
the vicinity of Malvern Hill.
The Potomac army, under its new commander, Grant, was
divided into two wings, the right under Warren and the left
under Hancock, with Burnside in reserve, moving by parallel
roads on the south side of the Pamunkey.
Early's corps led the advance, followed by Hill's, and
Longstreet in the rear. By a forced march Ewell's corps
reached Atlee's Station on the Central Railroad on June 16,
and from there next day to Ashland, where they rested on
the 18th.
This was a complete surprise to the enemy, and Ewell
captured about 500 of the Federals who were guarding
the railroad from White House to Richmond. Ewell then
continued his advance toward the Chickahominy, crossing
it at Meadow Bridge and Long Bridge, and reaching
the Mechanicsville road on the 21st. Here he encountered
the Federals under McIntosh and Davies, but drove them
back.
