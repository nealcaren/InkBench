The motives which to-day control legislatures in our politics would to-day “control legislatures and the enfranchisement of women, but we don't want women in politics. That has been our slogan and we want to keep it so. There are not enough offices and women to go around, we don't want to divide the spoils with women. We don't want to have things if we don't care for them. They might not uphold order, we know best how to conduct our elections, don't want a new class of unknown voters. We have ambitions which we can carry out only if we know what things become of us, but if we, women, stay out, when we ratify, others can enfranchise women if they want to. I think no one who has labored to secure justice from the legislature to-day will say that this is the correct description of the attitude of the modern politician. Isn't it a new phase? They talked the ballot so before the admitted right of women to the ballot.
