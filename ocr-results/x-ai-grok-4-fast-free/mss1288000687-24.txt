ALICE STONE BLACKWELL

Alice Stone Blackwell, daughter of Lucy Stone and Henry B. Blackwell, was born in West Brookfield, Mass., May 14, 1857. She was graduated from the School of Liberal Arts of Boston University in 1881, having previously attended the public schools of her native town and the Chauncy Hall School of Boston.

From 1883 until her mother's death in 1893 Miss Blackwell was associated with her mother as associate editor of the Woman's Journal, and since the later date has been editor-in-chief. She is also president of the Massachusetts Woman Suffrage Association and a member of the executive committee of the National American Woman Suffrage Association.

Miss Blackwell has published several books, including "The Life and Work of Susan B. Anthony," in three volumes (1908); "Songs of Russia" (1897); "Anthology of Vermont Poets" (1911).

Since 1911 Miss Blackwell has also been secretary of the Prison Special Fund Committee.
