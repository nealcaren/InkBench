To point out the crimes of treason, Congress was  
directed, in the next clause, to declare that no attainder  
of treason should work corruption of blood, or forfeiture  
except during the life of the person attainted.  

The constitution provides their enemies, giving them aid  
and comfort[*] Congress may pass Acts to arrest  
and punish those whose acts are treasonable  
against the Union. Such Acts must be tested  
by the laws and treaties of the United States,  
or by the laws of nations.  

Treason against the United States shall consist only in levying war against them, or in adhering to their enemies, giving them aid and comfort. The Congress shall have Power to declare the Punishment of Treason, but no Attainder of Treason shall work Corruption of Blood, or Forfeiture except during the Life of the Person attainted.
