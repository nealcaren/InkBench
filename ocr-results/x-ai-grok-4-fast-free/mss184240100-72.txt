From Richmond we went to Logsden  
from Logsden to Amelia from Amelia to "Bellefield"  
with our friends the Allens. From there we drove  
to Cousin Lucy's where we spent  
some time. We were very much pleased  
with our visit to Cousin Mary; she  
was so kind & affectionate. We  
found her in much better health  
than we expected. We left Cousin Mary  
with many regrets & then went  
to Cousin Sally's where we staid  
a week enjoying ourselves very much.  
We then visited Cousin Betsey & after  
leaving there went to see Cousin Sally G. with  
whom we spent some days very pleasantly.  
We then returned to Richmond & after staying  
a few days with Cousin Betsey set out for home  

[cross-writing]  
Dear Cousin  
I received your letter of the 25th ult. yesterday  
& was much pleased to hear from you. I  
hope you are all well. We are all  
quite well at present. Cousin Betsey's  
health is improving. She looks quite  
well. We have not yet seen Cousin  
Sally but hope to before leaving.  
We have been to see Cousin Mary  
several times & find her very  
comfortable. She seems quite resigned  
to her lot & is very cheerful.  
We shall leave here on Monday for  
Richmond where we shall spend a  
few days with Cousin Betsey & then  
proceed to Fredericksburg to visit  
our friends there. We expect to  
reach home about the middle of  
next week. With much love to all  
I remain your affectionate Cousin  
June Ruffner
