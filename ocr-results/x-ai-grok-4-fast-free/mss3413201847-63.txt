Legislature of Pennsylvania.  
File of the House of Representatives.  

No. Filed Mar. 3, 1911.  

Referred to Judiciary General Committee Mar. 3, 1911.  

A JOINT RESOLUTION  

Proposing an amendment to section seven, article eight, of the Constitution  
of Pennsylvania, so as to provide that the General Assembly may impose  
registration of electors and/or payment of taxes as a prerequisite to  
the right of suffrage.  

Section 1. Be it resolved, if the Senate concur therein, That the  
following amendment be proposed to the Constitution of the Commonwealth  
of Pennsylvania, namely, That section seven, article eight, which reads  
as follows: – "Every white freeman of the age of twenty-one years, having  
resided in this State one year, and in the election district where he or  
she offers his suffrage, ten days immediately preceding such election,  
and within two years paid a State or county tax, which shall be assessed,  
at least ten days prior to such election, shall enjoy the rights of an  
elector. But a citizen of the United States who had previously been a  
qualified voter of this State and removed therefrom and returned, and  
who shall have resided in the election district and paid taxes aforesaid,  
shall be entitled to vote after residing in this State six months." [until  
it may be amended] so as to read as follows:  

"Every citizen of the United States of the age of twenty-one years, who  
shall have been a citizen and inhabitant of the State two years next  
preceding an election, and shall have resided in the election district  
where he or she shall offer to vote, at least thirty days immediately  
preceding such election, and shall have, in addition thereto, the  
following qualifications, shall be entitled to vote in the election  
district of which he or she shall at the time be a resident, and not  
elsewhere:  

(a) He or she shall have paid, at least thirty days immediately preceding  
the election, State or county tax which shall have been regularly assessed  
at least thirty days immediately preceding the election, unless he or she  
shall have been, by law, exempted from taxation.  

(b) He or she shall have made at least thirty days immediately preceding  
the election, due proof of a residence in the election district by the  
registration of his or her name in the manner provided by law; but if  
any person shall have been duly registered in the election district in  
which he or she resides, and is entitled to vote therein, and shall  
remove to another district in the same ward, township, borough or city  
within such time that he or she cannot be duly registered in the said  
election district to which he or she has removed, then he or she shall  
be entitled to vote in the said district to which he or she has removed,  
upon such proof of residence in such district as is required by law when  
registration is required, and in neither of said cases in which such  
proof is required shall the elector be compelled to make proof of the  
payment of a tax or of a registration in the district from which he or  
she shall have removed."  

Section 2. That any amendments proposed by any other article of this  
Constitution, or any amendment to the Constitution proposed at the same  
time with this amendment, so far as the same affects the elections and  
the right of suffrage herein proposed to be amended, shall be void.  

Section 3. That all other parts of the said Constitution and the  
amendments thereof, not here altered or amended, remain in full force  
and effect.
