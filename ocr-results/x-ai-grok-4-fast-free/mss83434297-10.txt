IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES  
February 24, 1903.

REMARKS OF HON. WM. B. KELLY, OF PENNSYLVANIA,  
DELIVERED IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, FEBRUARY 24, 1903.

Mr. Speaker: The effort to prevent the organization of the House for the purpose of prolonging this momentous session of Congress, and thereby preventing the consummation of the efforts and hopes of the Republican party to place the Government in the hands of loyal men, is one of the most audacious attempts to defeat the will of the people ever undertaken in this House.

The gentleman from Kentucky [Mr. Gresham] made a speech yesterday afternoon in which he used toward his Republican associates in this House language which no gentleman would use toward another in private life, and for which he has been called to account by the gentleman from Virginia [Mr. Jones].

The personal difficulty which has arisen out of it will be adjusted in the manner provided by the rules of the House.

It was a speech not addressed solely to the floor of the House, but through the press to the people of the country, and it was made for the purpose of inflaming against the Republican members of this House the passions of the people of the South by declaring that they were endeavoring to force upon the South two Republican Senators from Maryland.

All the right which they claim is based upon the fact that the Legislature of Maryland was not organized until after the meeting of Congress.

If there were any way by which it could be done without danger to the public interest they would have postponed the meeting of Congress until the Legislature of Maryland had been organized and had elected its Senators.

But they have been forced by circumstances into the position they occupy, and they have not taken a single step, and will not take one in the future, which is not necessary for the protection of the public interests.

So far as I am concerned, I shall insist that the House shall be organized as soon as possible, and for that purpose I shall vote against any effort to prolong the session.

I do not intend to participate in the attempt to defeat the will of the people by filibustering in order to defeat the organization of the House and delay the action of Congress.

By the diligent work of the men who are attempting to be managers they will soon see that they are wasting their time and energy.
