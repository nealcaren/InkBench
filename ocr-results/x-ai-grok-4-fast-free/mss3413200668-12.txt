Bruxelles, le 8 Octobre 1920

Mrs. Carrie Chapman Catt

My dear Mrs. Catt,

I was so glad to receive your letter of Sept. 29th yesterday. I have been trying to write you for some time but the innumerable claims upon my time in this new situation have made it impossible.

I hope the report of the Commission on the education of the women of India will reach you in time for the Board meeting so that you may see the use of the $20,000 which you so generously gave. The residents of India will be one of the first beneficiaries of the work this year as the building for the centres of education for women is to begin in Jan. and it will be in full operation by the time of the Congress.

Of the budget of $20,000 which you gave to include some work for real international good will I can say that you will be more than satisfied with the progress of the work and the plans of the Alliance for the coming year.

Anna Howard Shaw

President of the Int'l Alliance for Woman Suffrage
