supplies and provisions for the
American Freedmen's Aid Commission

will be issued by the Bureau to all
Freedmen who are, or may be, under
the charge of said Commission, and
to such other Freedmen as the
Bureau may deem proper to be
placed under the care of said
Commission, and such provisions
will be issued from the supplies now in
use, or that may be received.

But no property will be loaned to said Commission
except such as the Bureau may have on hand
and which can be spared from stock now
on hand or that may be received.

Approved

Asst. Commr. for the Dist. of Col.
C. E. M. STANTON Secty. etc.

I.
The Commissioner will furnish a list of the persons above the age of 12 years and under the age of 65 years to whom he desires that provisions be issued, which list shall be made out in duplicate, one copy of which shall be retained by the Commissioner and one transmitted to the Asst. Commissioner for the District of Columbia.

II.
The Commissioner will designate in which Sub-Dist. he desires the provisions to be issued, and the Asst. Commissioner for the said Sub-Dist. will, upon receipt of such designation, draw from the Chief Quartermaster of the Bureau at Washington such amount of rations not to exceed in bulk 30 days for the number of persons designated, the number of the list being stated in the requisition, and the ration to be issued specified.

III.
The Commissioner will give to the Asst. Commissioner for the said Sub-Dist. a receipt for the amount of provisions issued to him, and the Asst. Commissioner will forward the duplicate to the Chief Quartermaster of the Bureau at Washington.

IV.
The Commissioner will be held responsible for the proper distribution of the provisions to the persons specified in the list, and the Asst. Commissioner for the Sub-Dist. will see that the provisions are properly stored and issued.

V.
A report will be made by the Commissioner to the Asst. Commr. for the Dist. of Col. at the end of each month, stating the number of persons to whom provisions have been issued during the month, and the amount of provisions issued to each.

Approved

Major Genl. for the Freedmen's Bureau
O. O. HOWARD Commr
