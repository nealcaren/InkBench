APPENDIX.

In the Assembly held at Albany, on the 3d & 4th days, praying for the passage of the Bill now before the Assembly.

Anna C. Spear, Julia Ward Howe, 
Caroline M. Kirkland.

In Senate

James S. & John Swett of Boston, presented a petition signed by 40 ladies & gentlemen praying the passage of the same bill.

Lydia Mott, of Albany, presented a petition signed by 100 men & women of the City of Albany, praying the passage of the same bill.

Betsey S. Chase of Albany presented a petition signed by 75 men of the City of Albany praying the passage of the same bill.

The above petitions are for the period to the 10th of March, the period to which the Senate has resolved to extend the time for receiving petitions on the subject of the Married Women's Property Bill.

Woman's Rights Petitions

The following are for the period to the 10th of March, the period to which the Senate has resolved to extend the time for receiving petitions on the subject of the Married Women's Property Bill.

With the signatures of the petition to the women of New York, published in the Legislature, & of the majority of women, published in the New York Tribune.

Petition to Woman's Rights in the National Convention at Worchester Mass. the 23 & 24 of Oct. 1850.

Signed by a number of distinguished ladies among whom are Angelina Grimké Weld, Sarah M. Grimké, Lucy Stone, Antoinette L. Brown, Sarah P. Owen, Caroline M. Severance &c.
