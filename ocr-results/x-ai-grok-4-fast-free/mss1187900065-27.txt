THE AMERICAN SOCIETY FOR THE PREVENTION OF CRUELTY TO ANIMALS.

Manhattan Branch No. 1.

313 E. 22d St. New York

New York Jan^y 30^th 1865

To The Police Judge of Andover Co. Mass.

Sir:

In the case of Augustus Haines whose horse was so badly mistreated last fall, as far as we have heard it was in a very cruel manner, we have in our possession sworn statements.  On the 1^st of last november a society for the prevention of cruelty to animals was formed in Lawrence.  On the evening of the same day they heard of this case and sent for Haines.  He was brought before them.  He acknowledged the facts.  They then administered a severe reprimand.  But as there was then no district attorney to prosecute the case, and the existing laws were not sufficient to reach the case, they could do no more.  We think therefore that a more severe punishment should be administered to him now that the laws are more perfect.  The Society would be glad to know if the indulgences to him will be continued, and to learn that he will be taught that the laws will be enforced against such cruelty.

Very respectfully yours

Henry Bergh

Pres.t
