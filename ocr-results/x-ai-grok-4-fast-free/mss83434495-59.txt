CHAPTER XXVII.

Of Vincennes. It is stated that half the History of the Fort de l'Est was inclosed in the circle of forts around Paris, and that the Germans had no more serious resistance to encounter than from that fort. This is clearly an error, as will be seen when we come to describe the operations against it in September 1870. The history of the Fort de l'Est belongs to that of the whole series of forts around Paris. The forts on the east were Nogent, Rosny, and Vincennes. The first two had no history, the third was the most important of the three. It was this fort which, from the first to the last day of the siege, held the German army at bay on the northeast of Paris. There was no other fort which gave them so much trouble. The Germans, after they had captured the forts on the north and east, turned their attention to this one with all the artillery they could bring to bear upon it. On the 28th Sept., the bombardment began, and continued with varying intensity until the 30th of October, when the fort was surrendered by the governor, who had been left in command after the death of General Vinoy.
