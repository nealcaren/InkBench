original cases, where a jury is applicable, is
guaranteed by the Constitution.  In State courts, where
the judge has no power to punish summarily for
contempt, the accused is tried by jury.  The
commission of treason is the highest crime and
punishment known to the law, and hence the
Constitution requires that the trial be by jury.

And in the case of the Chief Justice to preside, it
was intended as a rebuke to the Senate for its
action in the trial of Justice Chase.

The same provision applies to impeachments of
the President.  The framers of the Constitution
were familiar with the history of the English
judiciary, and the tyrannies practiced upon the
judges by the House of Lords.  The object of this
provision was to secure to the judges independence,
and to prevent their being subjected to the
authority of the Legislature.

It was with full knowledge of the history of the
judiciary in England, especially in the cases of Lords
Coke and Bacon, that the Constitution was formed.

The power of Congress to investigate and expel
members was well defined, and states have all
adopted similar provisions, which have since been
held by the courts to be necessary to the
independent action of the bodies.
