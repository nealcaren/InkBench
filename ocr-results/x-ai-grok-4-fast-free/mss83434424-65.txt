The city of HAMBURG has about 5000 inhabitants. In about
the port there is a free port for the importation of Tobacco
the export of which is immense the chief employ of the
inhabitants of that city which was the most opulent in
Europe till the French war which has reduced it in
circumstances and occasioned the emigration thence of many
of its opulent inhabitants whose places as merchants are
supplied by the French and Genevese etc. the city passing
through Hamburg etc. the revenue from 1766 to 1785 in
which period 36 Million lb. weight of Tobacco passed
through Hamburg which was shipped for Holland and the
Isle of France this city going through the port of Ham
burg 36 firms in Hamburg engaged in the American trade
passed 3000 hhds. of Tobacco from America in 1785
the city being the entrepot for the commerce of the
North of Europe which has been carried on from that port
for upwards of 300 years there are now about 240 vessels
of 25000 Tons belonging to Hamburg the city is governed
by a Senate of 120 members etc. the trade in Tobacco is
now so great that the inhabitants are building a new
canal for the Tobacco trade alone the quantity of Tobacco
consumed in Hamburg is about 1 1/2 Million lb. weight
annually the whole consumption of the North of Europe
from that port is about 10 Millions lb. weight pr. annum
the greater part of the Tobacco imported into Hamburgh
is for re-exportation to France Holland Sweden Denmark
Russia and Prussia the importation of Tobacco into

Hamburg is in the Duchy of Holstein which belongs to
the King of Denmark the city is governed by a Senate
of 120 members the trade of the city is under the
direction of 5 Collegians the houses in Hamburgh are
built of brick and are very elegant the streets are
paved with free stone and are very clean the port is
very large and commodious the city is well supplied
with water by pipes etc. the air is salubrious and
the inhabitants are healthy and long lived.
