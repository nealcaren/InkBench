(1) The Dauntless Male Martyr
will see her this morning.
I know thee'll be glad to hear that her son Joseph
has been released.  I doubt if he would have been had
not the entreaties of his wife been in his favor -
which they were.  As I told thee, he was in the house
where several others were taken, but what charge
was against him I know not - nor the others.
Write soon before leaving.
As for myself I have been quite well
since I saw thee last evening.  The pain in my face
is much better.

(2) Mary will leave to Hall to-morrow
morning if all is well.  I hope we shall
have no things to detain her.  The quilting
was finished yesterday, and everything
is ready for packing up and starting off.
