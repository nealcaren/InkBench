Published by Special Order No. 31, (Department of South Carolina.) — Leave of Absence for Enlisted
Men. — R. C. Gilchrist, Captain, Comdg.

Leave of absence for Enlisted Men. — Pursuant to General Orders No. ___, from the Adjutant and Inspector General's office, dated ________, the following rules for granting leaves of absence to enlisted men are published for the information of all concerned, and officers will be governed accordingly, as far as the same may be applicable to their commands.

I. Officers are hereby required to send at least fifty per centum of the non-commissioned officers and privates of their companies to all routine and guard duties, whenever the same is practicable; and when so detached, such detachments will always be returned to their respective camps or quarters as soon as relieved from such duties. In no case will any man, thus detailed, be absent from his quarters or the camp to which he belongs for more than forty-eight hours without a written permit from his commanding officer.

2. Leaves of absence for a less period than ten days will be given in writing by company officers, but no such leaves will be granted without the approbation of the Commandant of the post or in his absence, the officer next in command.

3. Leaves of absence for more than ten days will be granted only by the Commandant of the post, and will be given in duplicate; one copy to be forwarded to the Adjutant and Inspector General's office.

4. Leaves of absence will not be granted to more than one non-commissioned officer and two privates at the same time, in the same company, unless circumstances absolutely forbid it.

5. Applications for leave of absence must be made in writing, stating the cause, probable duration, and what efforts have been made to supply the applicant's place.

6. Indulgence of this kind [least?] ordering the discharge even though in other cases the indulgence would be granted.

In furnishing each column of the Muster Rolls the suffix and a brief description
of the nature of the discharge will be entered in the "Discharge" column.
