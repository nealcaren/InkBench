Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia,
July 25, 1883.

Honorable
Fredk Douglas,
Washington, D.C.

Dear Mr. Douglas:
I read your
speech delivered on the occasion
commemorative of Emancipa-
tion in the District of Columbia
and was particularly edified
and pleased by your utterances,
and surprised at the vigor &
brilliancy of your power after
an almost constant exercise
of them for nearly a half cen-
tury in the cause of our oppres-
sed people.
