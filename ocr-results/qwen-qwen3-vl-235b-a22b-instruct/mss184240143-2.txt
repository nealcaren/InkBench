New York, Feb 21st 1879

My Dear Mr. Dreskin—

I am afraid your business
affairs are again suffering. I'm here
and the party has succeeded to every
possible extent. "Mee & man as smilin"
but "Can't keep a Hotel" I am sorry, I
think had I engineered your "box"
instead of Mr. McCueoy, it might have
been more in a different position—
You told me you would see me about
your theatrical business etc. next I hear
you have "a boy" (Costume) dolting
with your affairs & "mixing them Chidn
up" I thought you had a clear experience
before with "Avalon Managers" I had
learned by experience the value of Managers
Don't think I am scolding you only that I
feel slighted. I came as more for you than any
man in this business (or out of it) for that
matter. I have (or will) hand it cheerfully
