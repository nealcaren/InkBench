ALBERT H. WALKER,
COUNSELOR AT LAW.
(Including Patent Causes.)

Hartford, Connecticut,

If the colored leaders take
to denouncing the Democratic party;
they will alienate the sympathies
of their best friends in the North.
The abolition of slavery; the
prohibition of discriminating
State laws; the extension
of the ballot to the colored man:
These are secure.  If the colored
leaders denounce anybody for
not also furnishing by law, the
unauthorized and absurd exclu-
sion of colored people
from particular private
places as hotels & theaters; it
will look rather childish.  It
will tend to show that the colored
people can not wait for the abso-
lution for the substance of citizenship.
