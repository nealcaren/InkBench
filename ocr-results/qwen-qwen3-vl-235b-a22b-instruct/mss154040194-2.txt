Buc., 7 April 1922

Dear Mrs Chapman Catt
It is in our programme of the year
to read lectures on “Famous Women.” Mrs Reus Ianculescu and
I will do it, she in the L.D.D.F. and I in popular evening course.
But as there are no complete libraries in Roumania and not
a single feminist library, it is impossible for us even to know
the title of the book, necessary for preparation. We adress therefore
to you to ask the other sections of I.W.S.A. to join your help.

Feeling so much the lips of a fair library we will
try at one time with our studies, to found one at B. and
perhaps then we'll be able to assemble round it the
Women in a club a, we are dreaming of since a long time.
Will your association be able for the generous act of more
help unto sisters and to send us one or the other standard work
on women movement in your country and specially
books, where we could find biographies of your famous women?

We would speak in a first serie of lectures, on political and social
prominent Women and in a second those the portraits of your
mad and science. But we know solittle, it is to say not much
