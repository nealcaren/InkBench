The Light in the Tunnel.
From the Franklin City Chronicle.
A new device for the bewilderment of lovers was applied in the case of a handsome young man on the last train yesterday. A couple sat in the seat before him. The lady was young and seated, and the gentleman was very tall. The train was driven at a speed of about 60 miles an hour, and the gentleman was leaning forward to kiss the lady. The train was rushing through a tunnel, and the light of the tunnel was reflected in the window, and the gentleman was startled at the sight of his own face in the mirror. The lady was not disturbed, and the gentleman was very much embarrassed. The incident was witnessed by several passengers, and the gentleman was very much ashamed of himself.

—The wife of Thomas H. Banner of Hagerstown had been warned before she married him that his temper was violent, and that, sooner or later, he would kill her. She seems to have made up her mind what to do if he struck her, for when he did so, she drew a revolver from her pocket and shot him dead.
