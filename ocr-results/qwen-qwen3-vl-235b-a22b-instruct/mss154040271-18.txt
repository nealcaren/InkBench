May 24 1922

CARRIE CHAPMAN CATT
404 RIVERSIDE DRIVE
NEW YORK

Dear Mary: Received a book yesterday - the 24th
gift this spring! If this book tells how its author (would he)
succeed with a foot when the author (would he)
has neither sense nor pep and no vocabulary
to express the public thought - the has, it will be most
welcome. Until I have read it or to stuff it with thanks or
whether to return it or to stuff it with thanks or
without thanks either way! I am sending you
a tiny box of lily of the valley.  I haven't the remotest idea
what any of them are, but we will find out.
If you are nearer I should have given you
a garden full of plants from the green house
all of which will either die in my garden or
give withered little blossoms.  They moved her
here at last, art wonders in yours!  When you
come down, I shall take you to see a garden
that is worth looking at.
