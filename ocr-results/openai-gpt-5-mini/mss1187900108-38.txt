So though relieved from office you are working away with all your old power. I hope that you are entering upon the winter in good health, & that you will not run any risks by travelling or lecturing till summer comes again. When you last wrote you were in some anxiety about one of your sons, who was ill — I hope that he has long ago recovered, & that all your family are well.

I hoped that the book of Mr Carpenter's sermons & a few other writings, which my nephew Prof. Edwin Carpenter of Oxford is editing, would have been out by this time,— but it will be tardy or later before it appears; it is entitled "Personal & Social Christianity" & is prepared by a short memoir by Miss H. E. Cooke.

I am going about the 20th Dec for a few weeks to my sister Mrs Blake's. I find it hard to leave home,— but my friends much wish me to, & I think I will be well to do so. I have now been about six weeks walking
