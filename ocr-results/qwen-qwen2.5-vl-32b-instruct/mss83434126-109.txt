1859 Jan 28th. William Hickling Prescott, American historian dies, aged [illegible].

1859 February 2nd. Miguel Amarmon, elected President of Spain, by the revolutionary junta on January 6th, following the abdication of Zuloaga.

1859 April 18th. Entrance of Genl. Imianon into the city of Bursa; he assumes the presidency and governs without respect to laws, life, or property.

1859 July 16th. Juarez, constitutional President of Mexico, seizes the ecclesiastical property of his country.

1859 October 3rd. James G. Mason, United States Minister to France, died at Paris.

1859 October 17th. John Brown with twenty-one companions crossed the Potomac River at Harper's Ferry and entered Virginia, where he and his followers incited the slaves to rebel, and to take up arms against their masters. After a short time, Brown was captured and tried and found guilty of treason, and conspiring and advising with slaves and others to rebel, and murder in the first degree, for which he was executed on Friday, December 2nd, 1859, at Charleston, South Carolina.

1869 October 23rd. Indecisive battle between the forces of the Argentine Republic and of Buenos Ayres near Bulnes.
