that of Price escaped by another. The pursuit was continued  
by Genl. Sigel towards Rextharville, and by the cavalry to  
the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete. Rebel loss  
2000 killed and wounded also 1,000 prisoners, among the slew  
dead was found Genl. W. Culbush, a peculiar feature in  
this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians, under  
the command of Albert Pike, their savage instincts were  
manifested after battle the federal slain and wounded were  
found to be scalped, thus unhinging the primative scenes of  
barbaric slaughter. Union loss 500 killed, and 900 wounded.  

**1862**  
March 11th: Genl. McClellan assumes command of the Army of the  
Potomac in the field. - Genl. Fremont assumes command of the  
Mountain Department. Genl. Hallick assumes command of the  
Department of the Mississippi. Manassas occupied by the Federals.  

**1862**  
March 12th: Commodore Dupont captures and takes possession  
of Jacksonville, Fla. - The Federals attacked and drove the  
rebels compelling them to abandon their ambuscades at Paw,  
Tenn.
