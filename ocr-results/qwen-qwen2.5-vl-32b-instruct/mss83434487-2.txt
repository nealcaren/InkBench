**Separate Brigades.**

**HEADQUARTERS, DEPARTMENT OF THE GULF,**
**New Orleans, September 18th, 1864.**

**General Orders**
No. 136.

**WAR DEPARTMENT,**
***ADJUTANT GENERAL'S OFFICE,***
Washington, Aug. 31, 1864.

**"GENERAL ORDERS,"**
No. 251.

Courts Martial for "separate brigades."

Where a post or district command is composed of mixed troops, equivalent to a brigade, the commanding officer of the Department or army will designate it in orders as 'a separate brigade,' and a copy of such order will accompany the proceedings of any General Court Martial convened by such brigade commander.

Without such authority, commanders of post and districts, having no brigade organization, will not convene General Court Martial.

By order of the Secretary of War:

**E. D. TOWNSEND,**
Assistant Adjutant General."

II. * * * Each of the several districts of this Department being composed of mixed troops, equivalent to a brigade, are, in accordance with the above order, hereby designated as "separate brigades."

**BY COMMAND OF MAJOR GENERAL BANKS:**
**GEORGE B. DRAKE,**
Assistant Adjutant General.

**OFFICIAL;**

**Acting Assistant Adjutant General.**
