Repeated dashes were made and furious struggles took place to carry the works by storm, but often, on the verge of success, the Federals were as often repulsed and driven back by the undaunted bravery of the enemy. The Federals were eventually ignominiously repulsed with heavy losses. Federal loss 668 in killed, wounded, and missing. Number of Federals engaged 14,000. Number of rebels engaged 6,000. Gen. Benham was subsequently summoned to Washington to explain and justify his conduct.


**1862 June 17th:**  
Evacuation of Cumberland Gap, Tenn. The Federals, under Gen. Morgan, advanced towards this point for the purpose of expelling the able General Stevenson, who occupied the gap with 13,000 men and well fortified the same. This gap is a defile in the Cumberland mountains, and is so deep and narrow that there is through the gap, for only a single roadway. It was expected that the rebels would defend its possession with the utmost tenacity. No such result followed. When the rebels were informed of the approach of the Federals, they precipitately evacuated the place.
