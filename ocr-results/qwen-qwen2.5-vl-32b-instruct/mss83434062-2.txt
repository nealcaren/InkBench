one of Rebel Gen Morgan men dressed in full Confederate uniform and armed with a tin plate conferring shot as he had kicked the a-a nigger and demanded of one what I was going to do about it--I quietly informed this high-toned gentleman that I should give him one hour to prepare to meet his God and accordingly waited a firing party and executing him after forming my bye-manners, which I took up our march to cherarin. and it is a notable fact that no further outrages have dared been committed upon any member of the keep as down as I got to Charleston I asked for a court of inquiry in the case which was granted and in whose judgment I was exonerated from all blame but Gov Perry having carry'd their thing it was brought up and I was brought before a court martial and again honorably acquitted by a verdict of Not Guilty and I am again on duty one more fleeting further connection with the affair is that W. F. Bennett was the President of the Court and that he Blood by me quite a Brother through it all.
