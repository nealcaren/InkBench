I doubt to be with them at Cork, but it will not be in my power; however, I am now about to tell you something which will make you think me the most fickle of men. I have decided to stay in the country six months longer. And I cannot wonder if Mr. Wright should think the same. Though I am here with far less reason than I have to stay. In consequence of the advice both of Mr. Garnier and Mr. Thompson both think the present a more favorable opportunity for modelling the Antislavery feeling of this country and bringing it to the air of the true antislavery society in America; and each think it would be wrong from me not to attend to the duty.
