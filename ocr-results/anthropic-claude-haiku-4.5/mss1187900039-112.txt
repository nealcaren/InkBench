Washington D.C.
Dec. 6, 1848

Dear Father

Your's of the 1st inst. is just received.
I saw an account of one of your believe in New Jersey published in the N.York Tribune, and I thought that you might have other opportunities, not leaving down again. We are well except the baby, he has a severe cold. Dr. Saguester is attending him, we are afraid he will have the Croup, but hope not. I do not wish to trouble your mind about the affair between Sothare, Back, and myself. I know your mind, and time is bitter convinced. You may have the candor of the difficulty, but I know you dont know the whole truth, and it is hard to find it under the circumstances. I am afraid that New York has gone a guard us. Heart for another war. If you will be as near as any length of time I will write again soon, as I am very busy just now. What would send love, if she knew I was writing. Love to Brother and all.

Your Affectionate
Charles Douglass
