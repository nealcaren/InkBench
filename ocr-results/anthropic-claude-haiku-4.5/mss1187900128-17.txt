I know not where to find pasties for them, I appeal to your judgment. I have ever been friendly to the Colored race, having lived many years at Cape Harlean in the Island of Tortola, where my husband, [word], a prominent Commercial house, under the name of Orleans Laroche & co. We are well known to the present President of Hayti, as for years he was interested in the purchase of coffee for the house of Orleans Laroche & co. One Wm. was also Consul for the United States, and the recognition of Hayti was effected by him. For with kind kind his Record, in the state Department.

Be so kind as to write for us to influence public opinion in the favor of the Colored People.

Begging you to excuse me for abolishing you I remain
Very Respectfully
Francis L. Wilson
