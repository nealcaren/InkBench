that after a battle of two hours they (the Northern forces) were repulsed leaving many dead and wounded, at that order an tranquility reigned in the South

We shall have on the 19th a maid of the York from B&a Minas via Navassa, a cable on 20th via cable and a maid again on the 25th

I continue to believe that the Northern forces will raise the siege and retired & the North, proclaiming perhaps secession, and that the unfortunate state of things will continue just for months.

If the government of Cleveland and Harrison had acted in good faith in their relations with Egypt, this war would not have lasted. - I ask myself if it would not be well for me to go to Port au Prince with Mr. Douglass to see if the two parties could not arrive at an honorable understanding

And, my dear Douglass, this letter is already so long that I must cut it short right here.

Very sincerely and very cordially yours,

Mrs Bassettau Jetty

[with write to Mr Douglass]

E.D Bassett
