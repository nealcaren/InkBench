Washington D.C.
April 13th 1869

Dear Sir/Brother,

Your very kind letter was received yesterday.

From what you said in your letter to Levi's friend that you suspected I was discharged for neglect of duty, or some other such cause; but such was not the case. Levi's friend told me at first that I was among the number, and he intimated to me afterwards that had I been a member of the Law Class I would have been retained. The following is the truth:

Washington has taken charge of the Law Department of the Howard University, and has absolved colored students
