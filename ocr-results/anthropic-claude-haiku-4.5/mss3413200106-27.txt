Dear Miss Blackwell,—

I am blessed with a
propensity to be alarmed at something. You
are something; still you will not suffer
an illogical hopefulness, as three successors
of the might tempt a less reasonable
creature to do.
The truth is, to hot, but beyond
used, to say nothing of dead. What nerves I have
use left but youth. The other evening, so as was
spoken of to me as having done this and that
as nurse in the war. I was called on to admire
her. I had to say, yes, I admire what she did.
But must she, but be a little woman to do
so much. Then I said to my will for us,
upon and she & me, to do good. Oh the
body recommended as we have, but really
I took to see me who offered no need to
recommend him. Seeing where one thinks
not how much he has done it can do, but how
