New York, Feb 21st 1879

My dear Mrs Creelman

I am afraid your business affairs are again suffering. I'm here with the Park Caravan & come most wisely. We're o'splay as smilingly on "EARL Keep a Hot Bed." I am sorry. I think he I experienced my "Best" week with McEntee & it might have been very in a different position—

You lord me upon inasmuch she me about your theatrical business & the very I hear you have a Brpt (do it am) dateline with your affairs & "Mixing the Orders up." I thought you had a dear & pleasant lady with Eugenia Marquardt & had learned by Shermine the circle of humphreit Don't that I am reducing up only, they I feel slighted. I owed to here & you there are Many in the busaed. or our S. S. for that Matilda Verned (or very) him have it cheerfully,
