Senator Attacks County
Administration.

BY PARKE BROWN.

Ruth Deneen McCormick, who has announced her candidacy for the Senate seat held by Ruth Hanna McCormick, yesterday dealt what was construed as a reply to one her sarcal campaign attacks by opponents of the late Senator Deneen, addressed crowds in Lincoln Square in January for the opening of his campaign for the Republican nomination.

Mrs. McCormick, just before she made her radio address, announced her position in the Senate race in an interview. She declared false carps that she is not a bona fide candidate. She said that even though she is supposed to have announced already to win the race, but none she expects to win.

Deneen Raps County Rule.

Senator Deneen, as he opened his campaign in Lincoln Square, read a telegram from Mayor Thompson declaring he would make his campaign because of the primary. Later Deneen in some remarks told of the need of honest, efficient and economical government as the prime issues of his campaign. He accused the city administration of having spent "billions" in their effort to give the people a closed meeting, but later the Senator declared that he would continue a criticism of the condition in the county.

Mrs. McCormick, just before she made her radio address, answered what she termed false reports over the state that she has not definitely announced her candidacy. She said she has been actively campaigning for the race since January for the opening of her campaign.

"These and other false reports have not spread over the state by those who wish to do just what they can to detract to the industry of the propaganda against me," Mrs. McCormick said. "It appears that in attempting to secure some plan, they may be in the thought.

Men in Senate Race.

"I believe it is true," she said, "that I am in the race for the Republican nomination for United States Senator. Every minute up to the closing of the polls on election day, I intend to work in the state for the voters of Illinois asking for Congress and visited every county in the state. I am going to continue in this race, and I am sure that I will carry on this campaign in an honorable way. I shall make it a point to come back to the people of the state after every minute up to the closing of the polls on election day."
