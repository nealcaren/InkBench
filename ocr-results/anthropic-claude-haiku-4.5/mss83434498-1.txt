SPANISH FORT, ALABAMA, APRIL 4, 1865: A letter, in pencil, with cover, C.D.S. at New Orleans, La., stamped 3 (in Blue) ink docket on cover, "Soldiers-letter, &- signed by U.S. Avery, Lt. Col. 95th Ill. Indt." written by Andrew Fern to "Mother" "We are now seizing this fort which is the main entrance to MOBILE. We first invested the fort the 27th last month, and have been here ever since. The fort is quite strong, mounted with several large guns. There is another fort 6 miles above here called FORT BLAKELY, that is invested by Steel, with 15,000 negro troops. The Rebels have a perfect hatred to them, therefore they came out last nite and made three different charges on it. The regiment is the closest of any of them to the fort and we can plainly see the men. We are now digging to under mine their works. We are trying to cut off their communication with MOBILE. Much more excellent content, talks about how they are entrenching to avoid the Reb shells, also putting up breastworks, they are planting some big 100 lb. parrot guns, the Rebs have laid torpedoes very thick on the ground to prevent our approach, also under the ground some torpedoes which have killed quite a number of their men who have walked on them (the forefather of the land mine) -Written in a very neat good hand, nice content concerning BLACK TROOPS and the TORPEDO
