My dear Lizzie,                    1917

I cannot think I seemed

possibly convey to you the very great

pleasure and satisfaction your lovely

graphic letter gave me. I wanted to tell it

all that you told me and you tell it

clearly that it was like a conversation

with you.

The Christmas card came and was

hung up on the food of my bed for alas

I was there. They tell us that travelers

in the tropics must have a diarrhea

bed & thought myself quite immune

as I had not had such a thing

for thirty years but I got one moreover

and was in bed a week. All our troop

had a turn at it, but me got it infinitely

aid it tandem fashion. I did also

get your good by cable in Southampton
