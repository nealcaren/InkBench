WANTED, feminism in politics. Emily Newell Blair says so. Mrs. Blair, sunny-tempered, broad-visioned and the one hopeful figure in her recent Journal articles, is to be reckoned with as one of the women in politics. Her conclusions are drawn from seven years' experience and hard work as vice-chairman of the National Democratic Committee.

She admits that women in political conventions and serve on committees, but she deplores deeply their idle and policies, the charges that they have been widely guilty of merely doing what men want them to do and of voting as men direct them.

Mrs. Blair finds that nothing new has happened in one party has also occurred in the other.

Mrs. Blair suggests that the remedy is the application of more feminism. She would like to see a new party. She asks for both—she would become a power in politics and such power she regards as essential. One is by holding office and the other is by being effective in political organization.

But is the feminism a typical of something once had or is it a brand-new article for which modern politics is yearning? Personally, while I agree with Mrs. Blair in many of her arguments, I do not think her diagnosis is correct.

THE fact is that when the new women voters asked through him opened door into American politics it was one great crying need thrust upon us—the need of cleaning house. The parties have not been cleaned out and, apparently, are as much in need of it as on the day women were enfranchised.

Said Professor McMaster of the University of Pennsylvania, under the title, "Politics in the Mire:"

"A very little study of long forgotten politics will suffice to show that in filibustering and gerrymandering, in stealing governorships and legislatures, in using force at the polls, in colonizing and

that has happened in one party has also occurred in the other.

in distributing patronage to whom patronage is due, in all the tricks and tricks that go to make up the worst form of practical politics, the men are not always so much worse than the governments were always our equals and often our masters.

O'er the years passed. Votes were bought, nominations were engineered, legislation was juggled for and the party machinery got concentrated into politicians, poor men made votes in politics, and the price of election has flown so high that it must continue unabated the investigation.

There is a Tammany Hall on New York, a Republican machine in every state and every state is getting worse. Neither men nor women have seriously attempted to remove them from their honorable use of money, corruption, fraud and whatever struggles to cover up the wicked things that have been done.

No feminism in politics and no woman politicians have as much to American self-government as the voters within the nation drop every other question and devote themselves to the institution of honesty and decency in the administration of party politics. We shall never be a great nation until honesty and integrity are worthy of the respect of our own citizens and their neighbors, until this housecleaning is thoroughly done. every purchased office, must be recognized as a crime and political honesty must be made the common standard of all parties before any simple remedy can be applied effectively.

I ask aid from women of all parties that they will no longer support a party because it is their party, and for the purposes profit to certain of the men, would startle the nation and the world. It would awaken respect where it does not now exist. It would put the right kind of women into politics and take our women into the wrong kind of masculinity. Instead of the right kind and conducted by men and women against the positive wickedness of their parties.

With Mrs. Blair and her friends, conduct a Republican and her friends, conduct a Round Table and answer questions:

Are women getting out of politics all they should?

Are women giving to politics all that they might?

Can the result for more feminism, less masculinity, or more decency?

When the conclusion is reached, there will be need of another Round Table to answer the next question:

How shall the result wanted be attained?
