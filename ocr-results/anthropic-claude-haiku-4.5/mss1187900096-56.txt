Nantucket, July 18, 1891.

My dear friends,—

Mrs. Charlotte and I
congratulate you upon your safe
arrival back in Washington. —
Charlotte is sojourning in Nantucket
for a few weeks. Mrs. Marvin be-
mains at New Haven. Mrs. Mo.
Comes frequently to pass the day
with me and I relieve the
Rossi. She suggested my working
to you. Alluding has become
quite burdensome to her & she
is as feeble, seemingly like thistle
down. But that she has improved
since her arrival on the Island
is clearly evident. She did not
think of walking out at all
when I was in New Haven—now
she does some walking than
