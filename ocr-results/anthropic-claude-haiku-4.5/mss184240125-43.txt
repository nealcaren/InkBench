I came to Boston in Nov. &
made a short visit before winter, &
I am still here, now waiting for winter
to be gone. I have lived very quietly, only
been to the opera a few times. I have
seen none of the celebrities, they were
too costly. My heart is not broken, does
not break easily. I thought I should
see you here this spring, because the bishop
said so. It has been a trying winter,
but spring & violets are near the time.
After winters, me often, & is happy if
her bit of house-work fills all itself.
But if she is shut in, she so hates
in-door drudge. That she frets, the housekeeper chafes. She has a good, practical man, to
keep her from doing unhealth of things. Paint
cage. Take heed you dear always.

Lovingly- Mame Lage-
