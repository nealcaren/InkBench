MRS. MARY WARE DENNETT
81 SINGER ST.
ASTORIA, LONG ISLAND
NEW YORK CITY

May 21
1925

Dear Mr. Browne:

I can't let your note about
Mrs. Blackwell go unanswered, even
if the check I inclose to send
must be omitted.

I have always admired Mrs Blackwell,
and, in late years, have loved her
experience. Her analysis of beautiful Christ-
mos and Portuguese ones, and her
indomitable spiritual grace are both
lovely addition to the past back-
ground of her life work.

It wrings my heart to hear that
she, too, is facing a financial morass.
There are so many who are! I am one
of them myself, in consequence I would
have in making the fund which
are varying. Being a stamp artist
is a most painful handicap,
in every way, but in its particular
hard to endure, when appeals like
this come.

Thank you for including me
as one of her friends, - which
I most heartily am.

Sincerely,

Mary Ware Dennett.
