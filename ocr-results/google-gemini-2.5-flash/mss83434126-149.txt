The federals were commanded by Gen. Curtis & Sigel. This battle
was fought for three days without any decisive result, but on the
3rd Gen. Curtis made additional charges, and at early morn
he attacked the rebels along his whole line, the rebels were in
stantly thrown with confusion and fled precipitately on all the
attacks. The division of Van Dorn retreated in one direction
that of Price escaped by another. The pursuit was contin
ued by Gen.Sigel toward Reitheraville, and by the Cavalry to
the mountains, the rout of the enemy was complete, Rebel loss
2000 killed and wounded also 1000 prisoners, among the rebel
dead was found Gen. Mc' Culloch, a peculiar feature in
this battle was the presence of several thousand Indians, under
the Command of Albert Pike, Their savage instincts were de
monstrated after battle the federal slain, and wounded were
found to be scalped, thus renewing the primeval scene of
sanguinary slaughter, Union loss 500 killed, and 900 wounded.

1862 March 11th Gen. Mc'Clellan assumes command of the Army of the
Potomac in the field. - Gen. Fremont assumes Command of the
Mountain Department - Gen. Halleck assumes command of the
Department of the Mississippi - Manasses occupied by the federals.

1862 March 12th Commodore DuPont captures and takes possession
of Jacksonville, Fla, - The federals attacked and drove the
