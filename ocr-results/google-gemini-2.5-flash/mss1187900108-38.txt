party; - a suicidal step it will
be for them, as your strong and
unanswerable arguments clearly
show; these arguments, Coming from
you, must have great weight with
the colored people, who are the
ones you specially want to influence
by them.  So though relieved
from office you are working away
with all your old power.  I
hope that you are entering upon
the winter in good health, & that
you will not run any risks by
travelling or lecturing till summer
comes again.  When you last
wrote you were in some anxiety
about one of your sons, who was ill
I hope that he has by now recovered,
& that all your family are well.
I hoped that the book of
Mr Carpenter's sermons & a few
other writings, which my nephew
Prof. Estlin Carpenter of Oxford is
editing, would have been out by
this time, - but it will be some
or later before it appears; it is
entitled "Personal & Social Christ
ianity" & is prepared by a short
Memoir by Miss F. L. Cooke.
