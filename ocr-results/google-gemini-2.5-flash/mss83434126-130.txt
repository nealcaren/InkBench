1861 Aug 6th Congress closed its extra session
1861 Aug 7th The town of Hampton Va. destroyed by the rebels
The privateer York, burned by the U.S. Gunboat Union, the
crew taken prisoners.
1861 Aug 10th Battle of Wilson's Creek Mo. The federals under the
command Gen’l Lyon, made a planned attack on the rebels
and drove them from their encampment and contested the field
for six hours hard fighting and in leading a bayonet charge
Gen’l Lyon was killed instantly, Col Sigel carried out Lyon's
plan and retired in an orderly manner to Springfield,
having captured 400 horses and 40 prisoners. Union loss 900.
killed 700 wounded. Rebel loss in killed and wounded was
much greater. The brave Gen’l Lyon the hero of the West
gave his honor, his fortune ($30,000) and his life to his country
that his country might live. Number of federal troops engaged
was 5000 - number of rebel troops engaged was 9000
1861 Aug 12th President Lincoln issues a proclamation appointing
the 30th day of September as a day of fasting and prayer,
1861 Aug 14th Major Gen’l John C. Fremont obtains and places the city
of St Louis, Mo. under Martial Law.
1861 Aug 16th Major Gen’l John C. Fremont in command of St Louis
Fremont and its military surroundings.
