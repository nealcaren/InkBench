Washington April 10" 1887

Dear Father

Your letter dated March 24' was received yesterday, it found us all well, and 
pleased to hear from you. 
Mr. Trotter, the new Recorder of Deeds is very 
seriously sick with pneumonia. Gerrit S. Soquon 
has been appointed deputy Recorder of deeds.
The Lawyers are much put out about it. 
Senator Sherman is making a trip South; in 
Georgia, a colored delegation called at his ho-
tel and was refused admission, as soon as 
the heard of it, he paid his bill and went to 
another hotel.  A. colored man by the name 
of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed to 
the Siberian mission. The political air 
is wavering between Blaine and Sherman. 
Bennett has made an appropriation for a 
new Bridge across the Eastern Branch 
at the foot of Penn. Ave, business in 
the Recorders Office is booming. Grizwell, 
and Griswold, President of the Ancient R.R. is mar-
ried.  Barbados has been appointed "clerk 
of the Senate" The President and Mr. 
Matthews have been taken back in the 
rumples. Confimation of Mrs Trotter. They 
expected he would be rejected. Sawyer
