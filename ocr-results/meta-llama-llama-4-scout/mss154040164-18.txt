Dear Study;

I am not sure I could possibly convey to you very great pleasure and satisfaction, your breezy letter gave me. I read it and re-read it slowly, and told it to all: as you told me to do; and I told it clearly; that it was like a conversation with you.

The children were out and was being put on the floor of our bedroom; they tell us that Florence was there. They say the must have diarrhea and I thought myself guilty meanwhile; but I thought not; I had not had such a thing as diarrhea for thirty years; but I got one miserable and was in bed for a week. I did not think I did it justice; but I had a turn at it, but I did it in rather poor fashion.
