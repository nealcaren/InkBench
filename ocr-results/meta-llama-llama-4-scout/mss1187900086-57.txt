Cedar Kie. Anacostia. D.C.
July 13. 1889.

My dear Bassett,

Soon after your previous excellent letter, and before I could see him about the Consulship & Le. Thomas, Mr. Blaine, with a note of a warning departed from Washington for Bar Harbor, where he now is and is about to remain till October; at least as say the papers. He evidently found the climate of Washington at all points inferior to his pleasure so soon for him. In any last interview with him, I was convinced that his time, toil, and anxiety, had made inroad upon his health and constitution. and that he must either take rest, a rest would take him. For this, and other reasons, knowing that a personal interview with him would be far better for us than writing him, I have not mentioned the Le. Thomas proposal to him as yet.
