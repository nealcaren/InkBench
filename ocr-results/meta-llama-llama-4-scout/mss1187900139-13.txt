Honorable  Mr 

James G. Blaine 

Sir:  I have the honor to ask the favor of a leave of absence from Port au Prince, beginning on the 20th of June, expecting the time required to reach Habana and The return to Port au Prince, if the latter there be deemed my duty. 

I am required to state in this request for leave of absence, the number of days I was abroad from my post, during the preceding year. 

I trust a special fully complies with this instruction on the, by referring you to my dispatch No. 98 dated Dec: 18th 1890 

On the 19th of July and on the 3d of the same month, arrived at my post, "Columbia", One." I am fully prepared and eager to "start to day", both under "To return to my post of duty by the steamer leaving New York on the following day, both under instruction from the Department, 28827"
