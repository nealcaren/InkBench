1710 Chestnut St. Philadelphia
March 28, 1878

My dear sister,

I write, first of all, about Edwinn, because after a long talk we had about himself last night, that takes the first place. I read the little note to him fromborough in which, while she hopes to find him better, she says "I fear me"- She has been mostly starving near to him, only these spells to be less of him from week to week, and his energy and ability to go or do anything more than barely continue. Last night, after we had gone to bed, he spoke for the first time. He says the pulse beating rather better, but his stomach is bad so that he can no longer use most of the remedies - whether of the allergists or homeopathic school - that used to cleanse
