Washington, D.C.
Dec 6th 1863

Dear Father

Yours of the 21st inst. is just received.  I read an account of one of your lectures in New Jersey published in the N.Y. Tribune, and I thought that you might have other appointments, and hearing from you.  All are well except the baby, he has a severe cold & Rhequata is attending him, we are afraid he will have the croup; but hope not.  I dont wish to trouble your mind about the affair between Nathan, Fred., and my self. I know your mind and time as it was occupied. You may know the cause of the difficulty, but I know you dont know the whole truth, and it is hard to find it out under the circumstances.  I am afraid that New York has gone against us, look for another war.  At any length of time I shall write again soon, as I am very busy just now.  Little would send her love, if she knew I was writing.  Love to mother and all.

Your aff. Son
Charl N. Douglas
