Washington April 10" 1887
Dear Father

Your letter dated March 27th was 
received yesterday, it found us all-well, and 
pleased to hear from you.
Mr. Trotter, the new Recorder of Deeds is seri=
ously sick with pneumonia. Gerrit S. Loquien 
has been appointed Deputy Recorder of deeds.
The Lawyers are much perturbed about it.
Senator Sherman is making a trip South; in 
Georgia, a colored delegation called at his ho=
tel and was refused admission, as soon as 
the head of it, he paid his bill and went to 
another hotel. A Colored man by the name 
of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed 
to the Siberian Mission. The political 
war on the Republican side 
is wavering between Blaine and Sherman. 
Congress has made an appropriation for a 
new Bridge across the Eastern Branch 
at the foot of Penn. Ave. Business in 
the Recorders Office is booming. Groesbeck,
the President of the Anacostia R.R. is mar=
ried. Barbados has been appointed "Clerk 
of Deeds". The President and Mr. 
Mathews have been taken back in the 
prompt confirmation of Mr Trotter. They 
expected he would be rejected, Sawyer
