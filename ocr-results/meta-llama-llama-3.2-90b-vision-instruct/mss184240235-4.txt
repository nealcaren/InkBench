My dear Nephew Dickinson 

I have been so anxious to hear from thee since thy last letter came, & was so sorry to find that thy uneasiness about dear Lizzie's condition still continues.  My feelings in regard to her are no less keen now.  My friends in the city seem to know as little about her as we do, & I had been hoping that thy letter would throw some light upon her state - which can only be known to those who see her daily by our faithful friend Lizzie.  In the boy's last conversation with me he seemed greatly depressed - & told me that things were going on very badly - & that the poor boy's heart was breaking like his dear father's.  I wish thee could see thy way clear to come on & bring thy family with thee - that we may have the pleasure of being together - In the boy's last letter to me he spoke despairingly of Walter's condition - & told me that he was getting very badly - and that he despaired of his recovery - or of seeing him well again.  The family are all well - & have been wonderfully blessed this winter - & I feel as though we had much to be thankful for - & that thy presence among us - with thy dear family - would be a great blessing. 
Yours affectionately.
