RUTH McCORMICK, DENEEN SOUND CAMPAIGN CRIES

Senator Attacks County Administration.

BY PARKER BROWN

Both Senator Charles S. Deneen and Representative Ruth Hanna McCormick, who has announced her candidacy to see to it that the actual campaign starts the week in January for the opening of real hostilities.

Senator Deneen addressed more than 105 of his followers—Chicago ward committeemen, aldermen, other public officeholders, committeemen, and officeholders—at a luncheon at the Hotel Morrison. It was closed to the press. The senator made public part of his speech, a criticism of the condition in the loops.

Mrs. McCormick, just before leaving for the west on a campaign train, issued a statement charging that a “propaganda factory” is circulating false rumors that she is a “bomb candidate.” She said that neither she nor any one else expects to win the nomination, but she also expects to win the nomination.

“Since the election of 1928,” read Senator Deneen’s manifesto, “every week has revealed some sinister reports of fraud, chiselling, malfeasance, or misfeasance by certain public officials in the city’s administration. The people have been deceived, hoodwinked, and in part have permitted unworthy motives to influence their votes. In party they have been influenced by small favors to them and in degree by the cohesive power of public plunder.”
