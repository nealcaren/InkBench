SPANISH FORT, ALABAMA:  APRIL 3: 1865 4 pgs. in pencil, with cover. C.B. is at
New Orleans, La., stamped 3 (Due Ink) docket on cover; "Soldier's letter, & 
signed by Wn. Avery; Lt. Col. 95 Infty. Intly." Written by Andrew Fen
mother:" We are now seizing this fort which is the main entrance to MOBILE.
We first invested the fort the 27th last month, and have been here ever since.
We have not lost many men yet, I killed and 15 wounded, is all of the reg't
There is another fort 6 miles above here called FORT BLAKELY; that is invested
by steel, with 15,000 negro troops. The Rebels have a perfect hatred to them
therefore they came out last nite & made three different charges on our 
regroes.  They 1st line. Our rifle pits are within 15 rods of the Rebel works,
We are now digging to under mine their works. We are trying to cut off their
communication with MOBILE. Much more excellent content, talks about how they
are entrenching to avoid the Rebels, also putting up breastworks; they are 
planting some big 100 lb. parrot guns; the Rebs have laid torpedoes very thick
in the channel which have sunk 2 of their gunboats, the Rebs have also sunk in 
the ground some torpedoes which have killed quite a number of their men who 
have walked over them (the forfather of the Land mine.) Written in a very 
neat bold hand, hics content concerning BLACK TROOPS and the TORPEDO!
