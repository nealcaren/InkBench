Lieut.-Colonel J. F. S. Winnington, Worcestershire Regiment, 
commanding the Northamptonshire Regiment, who has fallen 
in action, was born in 1876, and entered the Worcestershire 
Regiment from the Militia in 1896. He went to India in 1897, 
saw service in the South African War in 1899-1500, and was 
at the relief of Ladysmith, including the operations at 
Kop, Vlakfontein, the Tchrda Heights, and Pieter's 
Well as one operations in the Orange Free State and those in 
Cape Colony south of the Orange River. During the present cam-
paiga he was the recipient of the D.S.O. in recognition of his services.
Lleut.-Colonel Winnington married Joyce Mary, daughter of 
Major W. F. Christian, of Koutton Beuchamp, and is survived by 
Mrs. Winnington and two children. Lient.-Colonel Winnington, 
round smuterman, is execoedin erlcket and has played for

Major Aubrey Hugh Darnell, D.S.O., Australian Imperial 
Force, who has died of wounds received in action, was in 
service with the late R. F. A. Darnell, senior chaplain 
to the Forces in Ireland, and of Mrs. Darnell, of Bellevue Park, 
Cork. He was born in May, 1886, and had his commission in 
the A.I.F. on June 17, 1915. He went to Egypt with the 
A.I.F. on October 28 following, and sailed for 
Gallipoli on April 26, 1915. He was on the beach during the 
landing, and was slightly wounded on May 8. 
During the war he had received the Companionship of the 
D.S.O.
