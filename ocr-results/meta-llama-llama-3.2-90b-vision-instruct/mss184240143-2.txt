New York, Oct 22d 1879

my dear Mr Stephenson

I am afraid your business 
affairs are again suffering. I'm here 
not the party called upon to copy 
most unwarrant.  "Me as men as weirids 
our 'Can't Keep a Secret'  I am sorry, think how I mortified my Boss 
most I'm McKeon it might have 
been now in a different position.

You told me you would see me about 
your Theatrical business & the next I hear 
you have "a boy (as it turns) doting 
with your affairs & "mixing them 
up"  I thought you had a clear experience 
before with Amateur managers that 
learned by experience the value of Management 
and that I am saddling you only that I 
feel slighted.  I could do more for you than any 
man in this business or our affair that
