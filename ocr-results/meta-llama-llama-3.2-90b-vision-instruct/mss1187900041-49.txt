Washington D.C.
April 18th, 1869

Dear Sir,

Your reply to my letter was received yesterday.
I saw what you said in your letter to Lewis Seward that you supposed Lewis was discharged for neglect of duty, or some other such cause.  But such was not the case.  Genl. Howard did not know at first that Lewis was among the number, and he wrote to me afterwards that had he known he would have been a member of the Law Class.  The following is the extract:

Mr. Langston has taken charge of the Law Department of Howard University and has about twenty colored students -
