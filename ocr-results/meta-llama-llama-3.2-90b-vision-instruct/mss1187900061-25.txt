Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia,
July 20th, 1883.

Honorable
John D. Douglass,
Washington, D.C.

Dear Mr. Douglass:

I lately received a copy of an eloquent speech delivered on the occasion of the commemoration of Emancipation in the District of Columbia and was particularly edified and pleased by your utterance and impressed at the vigor of your powers after an almost unexampled defense of our cause for nearly a half century in the cause of oppressed people.
