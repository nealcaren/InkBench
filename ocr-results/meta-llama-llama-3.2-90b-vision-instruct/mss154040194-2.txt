Buc, 21 Aug. 1922

Dear Mrs Chapin Cott 

We took in our programme of this year "to read lectures on Famous Women."  Mrs Jameson and I will do it, she in her E.D.S.F. and vice popular evening courses.  But as here are no complete libraries in Roumania and not "a single feminine library, it is impossible for us even to know the title of the book necessary propaganda.  I ask therefore you and to the other sections of I.W.S.A. to our help.

Feeling so much the lips of a "few" library we will my old time W.C.T. and vice studies, to found one at B. and perhaps shall we be able to assemble round it the Women in a club as, we are dreaming of nice a long time.  Would your association be able for the generous act of more help, with sisters who sends one or two standard works on women movement in your country and specially lives, there we could find biographies of your famous women.

We would speak in a first series of lectures on political and social prominent Women and in a second [the?] the portraits of Women and Science.  But we know so little, it is to say not much
