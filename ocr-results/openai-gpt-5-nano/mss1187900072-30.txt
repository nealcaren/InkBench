Washington April 10th 1887

Dear Father

Your letter dated March 24th I received yesterday, it found us all well, and pleased to hear from you. Mr. Foster, the new Recorder of Deeds is extremely sick with pneumonia. Genl. L. Soguern? has been appointed Deputy Recorder of Deeds. The Saughers are much put out about it. Senator Sherman is making a trip South in Georgia, a colored delegation called at his hotel, and was refused admission, as soon as he heard of it, he paid his bill and went to another hotel. A colored man by the name of Taylor from Kansas has been appointed to the Siberian Mission. The political air look at present, on the Republican side is wavering between Blaine and Sherman. Congress has made an appropriation for a new Bridge across the Eastern Branch at the foot of Penna Ave., Business in the Recorder Office is booming.
