New Haven, Aug. 15, 1890.

Dear Mrs. Douglass:

Judge Sheldon has just informed me that this family had received recently a letter from you, and that you are in Washington. We learned that you were in Boston with Mr. Douglass, and so none of us know how to address a line either to you or to him.

I am quite desirous of hearing how Mr. Douglass is and what his plans are for the rest of the season. Can you give us any idea as to when you will come this way? We are also looking for [illegible text].
