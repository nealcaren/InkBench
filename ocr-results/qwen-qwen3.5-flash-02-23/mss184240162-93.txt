As to taking him out to fight
C said, any soldier would have
much preferred the chance of
being killed in battle to death
by Court Martial - so that there
was no aggravation in that.
I wish you would inquire &
examine - before you take it again
& omit it in Washington if you
can - something else one to
better any way it seems to me.

And then please bring in
now 3 5th U. S. Col. Inf.
1 North Car - there, instead of
54th if you can. The facts of
the two are - that 54. went in with
over a thousand men in excellent
condition as to arms, equip. -
Mant & health - was exposed to
moderate fire - I next morning
found all but 100 men on duty &
3 officers only, slightly wounded.
The 1st North Carolina - were
double quicked the last mile -
came into the hottest fire of the
whole action - a few came only from
the fresh enemy - four guns - &
only 600 men at the most - came out
wounded 68 killed 131. = 199 - two
killed - eight severely wounded.
Col. Gillmore's staff (two of them)
told me, there was no other understanding
in that Quarter, than that the 1st
did the best fighting on the
field unless it was 3th Co. (Col.
Stanley) - & Stanley also was witness -
ing close by them - (his regiment)
