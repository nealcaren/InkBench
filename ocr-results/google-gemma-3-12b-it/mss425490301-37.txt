The California State Federation of Colored Women's Clubs was organized in Oakland in 1906 by Mrs. Eliza Warner, of Los Angeles.
For each section of the State program many clubs in the various sections have built and maintained Institutional Homes for young women, homes for children, and the much needed “colored women’s Home, Los Angeles’s Women’s Day Nursery, Los Angeles; Fanny Hall Settlement, San Francisco; and Valentine’s House, San Francisco. These institutions receive the constant aid and attention caused a Monumental Day to be held at each State Convention.

The State gives an annual scholarship which began in 1916.
