It will be remarked that the provision as to slaves escaping from one state into another; not to the status or condition of another in the states where they are held, while another clause in the constitution relates to fugitives from justice. * Neither clause has any application to citizens or persons who are not fugitives. And it would be a singular species of reasoning to conclude that, because the constitution prescribes certain rules of conduct towards persons escaping from one state into another, therefore there is no power to make rules relating to other persons who do not escape from one State into another. If Congress were expressly empowered to pass laws relating to persons when escaping from justice or labor by fleeing from their own States, it would be absurd to infer that there could be no power to pass laws relating to these same persons when staying at home.
