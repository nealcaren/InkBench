Washington, D.C.
Decr 6th

Dear Father,

I was account of one of our senior lecturers in New Jersey published in the N.Y. Times, and I thought that you might hear other appointments and hearing that you might hear other appointments, and hearing
about, & & exaggerated him, we were afraid he would have the troops, but by the time I don't wish to trouble
your mind about the affair between Sarah. and me
I know yours must be attending him.  I after received. You
may know the cause of the difficulty, but before you think
know the whole truth, and it is hard to find yours under
the circumstances.  I am afraid that New York has grow
against us, I look for another war. If you will be at home
any length of time I will write again soon, as I am
very busy just now.  Little world, kind here, of the time 
it was coming. Your to Mother and all.

Your Aff. Son
Christian [Douglas?]
