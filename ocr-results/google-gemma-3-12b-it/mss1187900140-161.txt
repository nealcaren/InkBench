## The Evening Star.
WASHINGTON, D. C., SATURDAY, FEBRUARY 23, 1884.

**THE PRESIDENT’S MESSAGE.**

The President’s message to Congress, read yesterday, is a document of considerable length and of a serious character. It is a review of the condition of the country, with recommendations for legislation on various subjects. The tone is one of optimism, and the general conclusion is that the nation is in a prosperous and contented condition.

**FINANCE AND COMMERCE.**

The President commends the financial policy of the administration, and expresses the opinion that it has contributed to the prosperity of the country. He recommends that the laws relating to national banks be amended so as to provide for the retirement of the remaining four per cent bonds, and that the Secretary of the Treasury be authorized to issue bonds to replace them. He also suggests that the laws relating to the coinage of silver be revised, so as to provide for the free coinage of silver at the ratio of 16 to 1.

The President recommends that Congress pass laws to regulate the hours of labor, to protect the interests of workingmen, and to provide for the enforcement of contracts. He also suggests that the laws relating to the transportation of goods be revised, so as to prevent unjust discrimination against shippers.

**THE NAVY.**

The President expresses the opinion that the Navy is not adequate to the protection of the commerce of the country. He recommends that Congress provide for the construction of additional ironclad vessels, and that the Navy be increased in number.

**THE INDIANS.**

The President expresses regret at the continued difficulties with the Indians. He recommends that Congress provide for the further civilization of the Indians, and that the Indian Territory be opened to settlement.

**THE CIVIL SERVICE.**

The President commends the work of the Civil Service Commission, and recommends that the law relating to the civil service be extended to all departments of the government.

**FOREIGN RELATIONS.**

The President expresses the opinion that the relations between the United States and foreign countries are friendly. He recommends that Congress ratify the treaty with China, which provides for the exclusion of Chinese laborers from the United States.

**CONCLUSION.**

The President concludes his message with an expression of confidence in the future of the country, and with a recommendation that Congress devote its attention to the important questions before it.

**LOCAL NEWS.**

**POLITICAL MEETING.**

A large and enthusiastic political meeting was held last night at the Metropolitan Hall. The speakers were Senator Logan and Congressman Blair. The meeting was largely attended by Democrats, and the speakers denounced the Republican administration.

**COURT NEWS.**

The trial of John Smith for larceny commenced yesterday in the Criminal Court. The prosecution is being conducted by District Attorney Thompson.

**DEATHS.**

Mrs. Mary Jones, aged 78 years, died yesterday at her residence on Pennsylvania Avenue.

**MISCELLANEOUS.**

The weather today is mild and pleasant. The streets are crowded with people. The theaters are well attended.
