Gens, Office and so here I am this accounting to you for my pleasant days of the olden times in the United States / I spent but a day an a half there after in historical associations and could have spent much longer time there very agreeably but I had to obey (end of page two) orders and not inclinations. It is a paradise of orange groves and hot inclinations. In the yard I noticed the date palm and banana as among the tropical fruits grown here fruit in the season of it which I judge to be at least from six weeks to two months earlier than with us as a little I have been here our weather has come down on us a little (degrees) The more I had to obey here in the same office that Moore left to become adjt of our dept. as you may have heard. he took a fine leap up from a private to lst Lieut. and Adjt. but I consider it a very worthy bestowment of favor or influence. Sammy has come to be as tough as an alligator and at meal times has almost as open countenance. his company with one other garrison the Fort (Marion). It is a curious looking piece of old pottery but judged it a material of which it is constructed. I should judge it somewhere of a battering before it would come down. here of late two little sands on the main have sharpened up our appetites for something glorious from Vicksburg. we have four negro sergts in this Dept or parts of them besides the 54th Mass (colored) just here Col. James Montgomery of Kansas noted for in his commands (end of page three) “intelligence of our struggle" and guided by somebody stand by “inteligent contraband” late we approached a battery of eight guns from the rear took a turn of RR bridge, across the cowswitches and also a telephone track, tore up the track for three miles, the miller, the bridge and railroad property to the amounts of two millions of dollars.
