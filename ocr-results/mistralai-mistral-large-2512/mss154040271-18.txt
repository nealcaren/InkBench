May 27,1922

CARRIE CHAPMAN CATT
404 RIVERSIDE DRIVE
NEW YORK

Dear Mary,

I received a book yesterday of the 27th
gift this spring.  I think this took me to a new
success week after week and no vocabulary
has reached the heights of the last, it still the most
depressing week I have read.  I shall not ruin
my chance until I have read it twice.  I took it to work Thursday
whether to return it or to keep it, I am sending you
without a doubt over rules.  This is no jest, I just
want to know if there are but two million and
how they are.  I should know from the ground
what any of the nearer facts to me.  I have a
garden full of peach trees in my garden or
at garden.  I will watch the new blossoms.  I have moved them
after frosts little flowers in yours.  When you
give me the wonders in your rose garden
I shall start to take you to see a garden
that is worth looking at.

Lots of love,
Carrie
