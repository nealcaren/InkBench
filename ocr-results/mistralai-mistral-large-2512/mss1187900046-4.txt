Jan'y 23, 1874.

Washington D.C.

My Dear Father,

I have received your
kind letter and was
very glad to hear
from you, but I am so
sorry that you are feeling
so. I trust you are going to
Rochester. I hope it will
be all right there.
When you go there, I
shall take good care of your
letters and not think of
destroying them in any way.
We have had a few very
cold days here - we have

many pleasant weather.
2.40
