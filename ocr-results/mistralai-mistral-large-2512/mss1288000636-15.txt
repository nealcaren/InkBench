The Master's Touch

In the still air the music lies unheard;
In the rough marble beauty hides un- seen;
To make the music and the beauty needs
The master's touch, the sculptor's hand.

No waiting on the music's will or the marble's wish to be aught;
No waiting on the world to grow in sweetness, for beauty to light the face of things;
For the common things of earth to know their own significance.

The Master's touch, the sculptor's hand,
The voice of the Master, and the word of the sculptor,
And beauty and music are in the common air;
And the world is more lovely for the touch of His hand.
