Washington 16th
Nov. 6th 89

Dear Father

Yours of the 13th. is just received.
I saw the account of one of your lectures in the
Jersey Journal in the N.York Tribune, and thought
that your might have other appointments west besides
from here.  All are well except the baby, he has a severe
cold.  Mrs. Benquista is attending him, we are afraid he
will have the croup; but hope not.  I don't wish to trouble
your mind about the affair between Nathan and myself.
I know your mind, and time is better occupied.  You
may know the cause of the difficulty, but before you offer
your opinion of the whole truth, and it is hard to find out under
the circumstances.  I am afraid that New York has gone
against us, blood for another man.  If you will be at home
any length of time I will write again soon, as I am
very busy just now.  Libbie would send love, if she knew
I was writing.  Love to Mother and all.

Yours aff. Son
Chas. Noyes
