New York, Oct 21, 1879

My Dear Mr Drexler,

I am afraid your business
affairs are again suffering.  I'm here
both to partly exonerate myself
and to say as I would
for "East" keep a Hotel.  I am sorry, I
think I've engineered your "box"
most firm.  I've thought it might have
been worse in a different direction.
You noted you would do no act in
your theatrical business without my word.  I hear
you have "a boy" (as Stern) dealing
with your affairs.  I thought you had a dear experience
before with "American managers."  I had
learned by experience the value of management
and think I am reaping your crop that I
feel slighted.  I am sure for you that any
man in this business ever saw of for that
matters I needed (for now) has done it cheerfully.

Office, No. 12 Union Square, New York

RANDOLPH GARDINER,
BUSINESS MANAGER,
MRS. JOHN DREW'S
