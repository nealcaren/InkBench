Rochester, Oct. 14, 1941

My Dear Brother Frederick:

This is a fine crisp
cold morning, and while
the girls are taking their
last nap I will pen a line
to you.  As soon as this
morning's about the only
real quiet time I have,
the rest of the day is "a
full of work and duties."
We are all settled down to
our every day life.  Each day
has its own work and keeping
and work worth getting
ready to move ahead keeping
things going here.  Spring 1941
