Washington D.C.
April 18th, 1869

Dear Father,

Yours reply to my letter was
received yesterday.
From what you said in your letter to
Lewis, I feared that you supposed I was
discharged for neglect of duty, or some other
such cause; but such was not the case.
Well, Howard did not know at first that
I was among the number, and he wrote
me a few words to the effect that I had been
a member of the Law Class which
now being retained. The following is the
truth:

Mr. Langston has taken charge of the
new Department of the Howard University
and has about twenty colored students—
