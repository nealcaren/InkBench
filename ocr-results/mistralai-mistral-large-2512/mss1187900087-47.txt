that after a battle of two hours they (the Northern forces) were repulsed leaving "many dead and wounded," and tranquility reigned in the South. We shall have on the 19th a raid of the 10th from Port Royal via Warsaw, a cavalry 20th via Cuba and a raid again on the 25th next. I continue to believe that the Northern forces will raise the siege and retreat to the North, proclaiming perhaps "secession," and that the unfortunate state of things will continue 12 months.

If the Governments of Cleveland and Harris now had acted in good faith in their relations with McRae, this war would not have lasted. I ask myself if it would not be well for me to postpone writing Mr. Douglas to see if the two parties contending cannot arrive at an honorable understanding. Now, my dear Douglas, this letter is already so long that I must cut it short right here.

Very sincerely and very cordially yours,
Mrs. Bassett & Betty [ED Bassett]
(write to Washington)
