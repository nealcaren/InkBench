My dear Mr. Bridgman,

Whereas they concurred in the hope that it
has been like a calm to my troubled brain, yet
an injustice has been unconsciously done.
In justice to them, my position in the
past two years in the Journalism chair
was taken from me, but I am not the loser
alone from this act; the school has lost
degrees in its standing.  The boys know
it to be a fact, yet never a word of
discontent has been uttered by them.
They have been loyal to me and my successor in
the work, and I am very thankful for
such men.  The only compensation which
possessed any value to me, you offered me
in S[????] House, but I declined.
They have been so kind since then.  I have
been treated with the utmost kindness, and
I am glad that I have been able to do some
work for them.  My relations have
been of the best, and I shall always have
which I can look back with pleasure.  I am
sorry that I cannot accept your offer.  I appreciate
it, and I hope that I may be able to do you
some service in the future.  I shall always have
kindest regards for you.

Yours sincerely,
