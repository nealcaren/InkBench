Washington D.C.
July 15, 1883.

Dear Brandford,

I received
yours affectionate letter on the
12th and was very glad
to hear from you, and to know
that you are getting along
nicely.
Everything is going along
smoothly here, enduring the
weather which is at times ab-
solutely intolerable.  It is now
about nine o'clock and the
thermometer stands about 84½°.
Charlie has not left yet for the Eastern Seaboard and
I doubt if he will be (or
not) until the latter part of
next week.  I do not hear anything [?]
