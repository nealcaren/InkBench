129 E. 72 St.
N.Y. City
4 26 June, 1882.

Dear Miss Blackwell,—

I am blessed with a
responsibility; still you will not draw
any original conclusion, as three successive
epistles might tempt a less reasonable
creature to do. The truth is, is that, not beyond
creed, to say nothing of deed, that resource else
was left but you. The other evening, as I was
shaken to the core at having done this and that
as nurse in the war, I was called on to admire
her. That is to say, yes, I admire what she did.
But must she not be a noble woman to do
so much. When I said it my word for us
you and she & me, to come good. To the
only recommendation we can have; But really
I took to see one who dared not exceed to
recommend them. Seeing whom one thinks
not how much he has done or can do, but how
much he is.

I heard was talking Greek,
& went no further; but you who understand
Greek, is this really so unintelligible?
