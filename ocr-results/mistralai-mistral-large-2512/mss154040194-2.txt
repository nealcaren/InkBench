Buc., 7 Aprilie 1922

Dear Mrs Chapman Catt,

We turn in our propaganda of the year
to near lectures on famous women. Mrs. Janculescu and
I will do it in the I.D.F.T. and in popular evening courses.
But as there are no complete libraries in Roumania and not
a simple feminist library, it is impossible to even know
the titles of the books necessary for preparation. We ask therefore
to you and to the other sections of I.W.S.A. to help us.
Feeling so much the lack of a feminist library we will
try at one time with our sisters, to found one at B. and
perhaps then we'll be able to assemble around it the
women in a club as we are dreaming of since a long time.
Will you, association, be able for the generous act of some
help with sisters and to send us one of the other standard works
on women movement in your country and specially
books where we could find biographies of your famous women.
We would speak in a first series of lectures on public and social
prominent women and in a second draw the portraits of your
