"Do you agree with the Governor in his im-
plication that suffrage may prove to be
a misfortune experiment?"
"The point that I should make," replied Mrs.
Howe, "is, who has the right to determine
whether it is a misfortune or not?  It is a por-
tion of the community the right
to make the decision.  There are some who
do not admit that universal man-
hood suffrage is a blessing.  Suffrage does
not belong to men to give or to take away.
By existing forms of law men must give it to
us.  I do not allow that the obligation is
on us to prove that it is a good thing.  Even
though it shall appear to some to be an un-
fortunate experiment."
