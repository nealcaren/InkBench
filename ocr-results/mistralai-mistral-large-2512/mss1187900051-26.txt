CITY HALL,
East Rush, 8 P.M.
Rochester, N.Y., Aug. 17, 1877.

Friend Douglas,

I started from Rochester yesterday
morning at 8, went to Hemlock Lake,
stopped at 6 o'clock. I was in the swamp
a few miles north of the Rush Reservoir.
I attached a pocket instrument upon the
wire and communicated with the person
who took my place during my absence.
He telegraphed me the substance of your
postal card. Before I left home I had
your telegram, and went to the Rock. Sav. Bank,
where the officers were prepared and notified
them of its new conditions, and of your wish
for a duplicate. They said that they
could not give another until they had
received their axe from the clearing house
of NY, which would be the first week
