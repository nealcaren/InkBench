GEORGE H. WILSON, Secretary.

Chicago, Ill. W. S. J. June 1st.

My dear Mr. Douglas:
I learn for the East that you have just offered
terms to Mr. Dvořák, or know that Mr. Dvořák has
just accepted terms. Does this
mean that he will come to us? As I remember
our former correspondence, I think you said
that he would not leave for me. I remember
how he did much to help us in Paris. I hope
you will take the matter up
with him to Chicago later in the summer &
then I will try to see him too. If
the musical scheme will have me,

Very truly yours,
[Signature]
J. Moore Statson,
26 West 61st St.,
New York.
