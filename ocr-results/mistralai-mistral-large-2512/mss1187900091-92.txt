THE REPUBLICAN LEAGUE,
NEW HAVEN, CONN.

September 28, 1890.

My dear Douglas:

On my passage thro'
New York yesterday from Phila: where I
spoke on Friday evening, I called at New-
ark B.T.Co's office for news about the sailing
of our steamer. And this is what the clerk
in the office assured me of:
The steamer is the "Orange Nassau."
She will sail from Pierrepont Stores (along
side of the Wall street ferry), Brooklyn,
on Friday next, 4th instant, at noon, for
Port Antonio.
I presume that we can count on this
information as entirely reliable.
I have always understood that the
"Orange Nassau" is the poorest steamer of the
line. But she is staunch and sound, and I
guess we can last it for six days on board. 99
