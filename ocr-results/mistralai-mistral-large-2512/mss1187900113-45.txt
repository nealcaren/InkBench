And now let me add that in Easter eve I
was sought by the Director of Home Institute
mentioned in a newspaper to tell me he had
very gladly found that Philadelphia money as he called it could
be found in the North (I'm sure he had been assured)
at low interest for such work, and was glad indeed
and attached my attention to start a Bag of the
Daily Inter Ocean of 25 March giving an account
of a successful colored evangelist at Tuskegee
also a number of other items to which I refer you as some of interest
to your committee. It is a university which
contains very interesting items about the Columbus
Fair. When upon this I heard of your wish
I thought I could not but write you fully to
ask your opinion, and counsel how best to proceed.
I feel sure of your sympathy for the cause.
Perhaps for auld lang syne you will remember hopefully
it with very best wishes of the season for your
continued prosperity, most truly yours
